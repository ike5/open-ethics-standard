# Healthcare — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Healthcare & Medical Services  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to healthcare providers, medical device companies, pharmaceutical firms, and digital health platforms. Healthcare occupies the highest-stakes consumer domain: unethical practices can directly cause physical harm, financial devastation, and loss of life.

---

## Category 1: Billing & Pricing Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HC-1.1 | **Surprise Billing**            | Billing patients for out-of-network providers involved in their care without prior disclosure | Financial exploitation via information asymmetry |
| HC-1.2 | **Procedure Upselling**          | Recommending unnecessary tests or procedures to increase billing   | Medical fraud; patient harm           |
| HC-1.3 | **Opaque Pricing**              | Refusing to provide cost estimates before non-emergency procedures  | Withholding information needed for informed consent |
| HC-1.4 | **Facility Fee Concealment**    | Charging separate facility fees not disclosed at time of service    | Hidden cost practice                  |
| HC-1.5 | **Insurance Code Manipulation** | Deliberately coding procedures at higher reimbursement levels than clinically warranted | Insurance fraud                       |

## Category 2: Patient Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HC-2.1 | **Data Monetization**           | Selling or sharing de-identified patient data that can be re-identified | Violation of patient trust; privacy breach |
| HC-2.2 | **Consent Bypass**               | Embedding data-sharing consent in treatment consent forms, making refusal impractical | Coerced consent                      |
| HC-2.3 | **Health App Data Leakage**      | Mental health, reproductive, and addiction apps transmitting data to third-party advertisers | Exploitation of sensitive health data |
| HC-2.4 | **Research Data Gating**         | Withholding anonymized clinical trial data from independent researchers while claiming scientific rigor | Obstructing verification; knowledge hoarding |

## Category 3: Pharmaceutical Practices

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HC-3.1 | **Evergreening**                 | Making minor modifications to extend patent life and block generics | Anti-competitive; restricts access to medicine |
| HC-3.2 | **Pay-for-Delay**                | Paying generic manufacturers to delay market entry                  | Anti-competitive; inflates drug costs  |
| HC-3.3 | **Direct-to-Consumer Disease Awareness** | Running ads that medicalize normal conditions to drive drug sales | Creating false health anxiety         |
| HC-3.4 | **Opioid Promotion Deception**   | Misrepresenting addiction risks of controlled substances to prescribers | Public health endangerment           |
| HC-3.5 | **Clinical Trial Selective Reporting** | Publishing only favorable trial results while suppressing negative findings | Scientific fraud; patient endangerment |

## Category 4: Access & Equity

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HC-4.1 | **Algorithmic Triage Bias**     | Deploying AI triage or diagnostic tools trained on non-representative populations | Discriminatory care allocation        |
| HC-4.2 | **Service Desert Creation**     | Consolidating facilities in affluent areas while closing rural or low-income clinics | Systemic access denial                |
| HC-4.3 | **Emergency Care Gatekeeping**  | Requiring insurance verification before stabilizing emergency patients | Violation of emergency care obligations |
| HC-4.4 | **Prescription Access Barriers** | Step-therapy requirements that delay necessary medication based on cost rather than clinical evidence | Prioritizing cost over patient welfare |

## Category 5: Medical Devices & Technology

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HC-5.1 | **Planned Obsolescence in Devices** | Designing implantable or critical devices with unnecessary limited lifespans | Endangering patients for profit       |
| HC-5.2 | **Security Neglect**             | Failing to patch known vulnerabilities in internet-connected medical devices | Patient safety risk                   |
| HC-5.3 | **Interoperability Lock-in**    | Using proprietary data formats to prevent patients from transferring records to competitors | Data hostage-taking; obstructing care continuity |
| HC-5.4 | **AI Diagnostic Opacity**       | Deploying AI diagnostic tools without explainability or physician oversight mechanisms | Unaccountable automation in life-critical decisions |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish price lists** — make costs for the top 50 most common procedures publicly available and updated quarterly.
2. **Implement data minimization** — collect only the data necessary for treatment; conduct annual audits of third-party data sharing.
3. **Adopt open clinical trial reporting** — register all trials publicly; post results within 12 months of completion regardless of outcome.
4. **Establish equity review boards** — review facility placement, staffing, and algorithmic tools for disparate impact on vulnerable populations.
5. **Maintain device security programs** — publish vulnerability disclosure policies and commit to patching critical device vulnerabilities within 30 days.
