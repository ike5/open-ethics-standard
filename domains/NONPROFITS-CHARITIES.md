# Domain: Nonprofits & Charities

**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This standard defines ethical practices and violations within the nonprofit and charitable sector, covering financial transparency, fundraising integrity, governance accountability, impact measurement, and organizational accountability. It addresses practices that undermine donor trust, exploit beneficiaries, enable self-dealing, or evade meaningful impact assessment. Organizations operating under this domain standard commit to transparent, accountable, and beneficiary-centered charitable operations.

---

## Financial Transparency

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| NP-1.1 | Overhead disclosure | Disclose complete overhead and administrative costs in financial statements and fundraising materials, clearly distinguishing program and operational spending | Concealing overhead costs by reclassifying administrative expenses as program expenses to inflate program spending ratios |
| NP-1.2 | Program expense accuracy | Report program expenses accurately, reflecting only direct costs of charitable program activities | Inflating program expense figures by including shared costs, management time, or fundraising expenses under program categories |
| NP-1.3 | Restricted fund honorship | Use donor-restricted funds exclusively for their designated purposes and report on their use with specificity | Misusing donor-restricted funds for general operations, different programs, or purposes inconsistent with the donor's intent |
| NP-1.4 | Financial statement accessibility | Publish audited financial statements, IRS filings, and annual reports in accessible formats on the organization's website | Making financial statements difficult to find, understand, or access, or publishing only summary data without full disclosure |
| NP-1.5 | Compensation transparency | Disclose executive compensation, including salary, benefits, bonuses, and deferred compensation, in publicly available filings | Obscuring executive compensation through related-party payments, deferred arrangements, or off-book benefit structures not reflected in public filings |

## Fundraising

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| NP-2.1 | Emotional appeals accuracy | Use honest, representative stories and data in fundraising appeals, avoiding exaggeration or manipulation | Exploiting emotional vulnerability through exaggerated crisis imagery, misleading statistics, or fabricated beneficiary stories to maximize donations |
| NP-2.2 | Recurring donation consent | Obtain clear, informed consent for recurring donations with transparent cancellation processes | Implementing dark patterns in donation flows—pre-checked recurring boxes, hidden cancellation links, or confusing wording—to trap donors into recurring giving |
| NP-2.3 | Donor data protection | Protect donor personal and financial data, and do not sell, rent, or share donor lists without explicit consent | Selling or renting donor contact information, giving history, or demographic data to third parties without explicit opt-in consent |
| NP-2.4 | Fundraising cost disclosure | Disclose fundraising costs and ratios honestly, including the cost to raise each dollar | Understating fundraising costs by allocating them to program categories or failing to disclose the true cost of acquisition campaigns |
| NP-2.5 | Gift acknowledgment honesty | Provide accurate descriptions of what donations fund and avoid implying impact that exceeds the organization's demonstrated capacity | Overstating the impact of individual donations—implying one gift achieves outcomes that actually require many contributions or organizational infrastructure |

## Governance

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| NP-3.1 | Board independence | Maintain an independent board with a majority of members who have no financial or familial relationships with staff or vendors | Allowing board self-dealing where members approve transactions, contracts, or compensation that benefit themselves or their associates |
| NP-3.2 | Executive compensation oversight | Set executive compensation through an independent board process benchmarked against comparable organizations and disclosed publicly | Approving excessive executive compensation without independent benchmarking, or obscuring total compensation through complex benefit structures |
| NP-3.3 | Related-party transaction disclosure | Disclose all related-party transactions, including the nature of the relationship and the terms, in financial statements | Failing to disclose related-party transactions between the organization and board members, executives, or their family members |
| NP-3.4 | Board term limits | Implement board term limits and rotation policies to ensure governance renewal and prevent entrenchment | Maintaining indefinite board tenures that prevent governance renewal, concentrate power, and resist accountability |
| NP-3.5 | Conflict of interest policy | Adopt and enforce a written conflict of interest policy requiring annual disclosure and recusal from conflicted decisions | Lacking a conflict of interest policy, or having a policy that is not enforced when board members vote on matters involving their personal interests |

## Impact

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| NP-4.1 | Outcome measurement rigor | Measure and report program outcomes using rigorous, transparent methodologies with clear indicators and benchmarks | Avoiding meaningful outcome measurement by reporting only output metrics—people served, meals delivered—without assessing actual impact on beneficiaries' lives |
| NP-4.2 | Beneficiary voice integration | Systematically incorporate beneficiary perspectives into program design, evaluation, and governance decisions | Excluding beneficiary voices from program design and evaluation, making decisions about communities without meaningful input from affected individuals |
| NP-4.3 | Impact reporting honesty | Report impact results honestly, including negative findings, limitations, and areas where goals were not met | Marketing impact using cherry-picked metrics, misleading anecdotes, or statistical manipulations that overstate actual program effectiveness |
| NP-4.4 | Needs-based programming | Design programs based on assessed community needs rather than organizational convenience or donor preferences | Running savior complex marketing campaigns that portray communities as helpless and the organization as the sole rescuer, rather than amplifying community agency |
| NP-4.5 | Adaptive learning | Use evaluation findings to adapt programs, discontinue ineffective initiatives, and share lessons learned publicly | Continuing ineffective programs because they are popular with donors, refusing to publish negative evaluation results, or resisting program adaptation |

## Accountability

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| NP-5.1 | Whistleblower protection | Establish and publicize confidential whistleblower channels with clear anti-retaliation protections for staff, volunteers, and partners | Suppressing whistleblower reports through intimidation, retaliation, or hostile work environments that discourage reporting of misconduct |
| NP-5.2 | Audit independence | Engage independent auditors with no conflicts of interest and publish audit results without material modification | Limiting audit scope, restricting auditor access, or selecting auditors with conflicts of interest that compromise independence |
| NP-5.3 | Complaint resolution | Maintain accessible, transparent complaint resolution processes for beneficiaries, donors, and community members | Operating without a meaningful complaint resolution process, ignoring complaints, or failing to provide response timelines and outcomes |
| NP-5.4 | External accountability participation | Participate in external accountability frameworks, third-party evaluations, and sector oversight mechanisms | Refusing to participate in external accountability assessments, third-party evaluations, or sector oversight mechanisms that could expose organizational shortcomings |

---

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this standard should:

1. **Publish Annual Transparency Reports** — Release detailed financial statements, program impact data, executive compensation, and governance information in accessible formats annually.
2. **Implement Fundraising Ethics Policies** — Adopt written policies governing emotional appeals, recurring donation consent, donor data handling, and cost disclosure.
3. **Establish Governance Standards** — Enforce board independence requirements, conflict of interest policies, term limits, and related-party transaction disclosure.
4. **Adopt Impact Measurement Frameworks** — Define clear outcome indicators, collect beneficiary feedback, and publish honest impact reports including negative findings.
5. **Create Accountability Mechanisms** — Establish whistleblower protections, independent audit processes, and accessible complaint resolution channels.
6. **Train Board and Staff** — Require annual ethics training for board members, executives, and fundraising staff covering transparency, governance, impact, and accountability standards.
