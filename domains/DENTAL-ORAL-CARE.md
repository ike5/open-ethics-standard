# Dental & Oral Care Services — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Dental & Oral Care Services (Dental Practices, DSOs, Orthodontics, Oral Surgery, Dental Products)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to dental and oral care services. Dental care sits at the intersection of health necessity and elective procedure, and unethical practices in this domain exploit information asymmetry between providers and patients, prioritize revenue over clinical need, and create access barriers that disproportionately harm low-income and underserved communities.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DN-1.1 | **Unnecessary Treatment Upselling** | Recommending and performing dental procedures that lack clinical justification, such as replacing serviceable fillings or extracting wisdom teeth without symptoms | Patient exploitation; clinical fraud |
| DN-1.2 | **Insurance Maximum Exploitation** | Designing treatment plans to consume the maximum annual insurance benefit regardless of clinical priority, sequencing work to maximize reimbursements rather than patient outcomes | Insurance abuse; treatment distortion |
| DN-1.3 | **Out-of-Network Surprise Billing** | Failing to disclose out-of-network status for specialists or ancillary providers within a referral chain, resulting in unexpected patient liability | Surprise billing; informed consent violation |
| DN-1.4 | **Implant and Cosmetic Procedure Markup** | Applying extreme markups on dental implants, veneers, and cosmetic procedures while obscuring the actual material and laboratory costs from patients | Price opacity; value misrepresentation |

## Category 2: Quality

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DN-2.1 | **Lab Work Substitution Without Disclosure** | Using a different dental laboratory or lower-cost fabrication method than the patient was led to expect, including offshore labs without quality oversight | Bait-and-switch; quality deception |
| DN-2.2 | **Material Grade Downgrade** | Placing lower-grade or non-precious materials while billing for premium or noble alloys in crowns, bridges, and other restorations | Material fraud; patient harm |
| DN-2.3 | **Reused Single-Use Instruments** | Reprocessing and reusing instruments or devices labeled as single-use, including suction tips, burrs, and disposable mirrors, creating cross-contamination risk | Infection control violation; patient endangerment |
| DN-2.4 | **Infection Control Protocol Gaps** | Failing to maintain consistent sterilization protocols, autoclave monitoring, or barrier techniques between patients, particularly in high-volume practices | Patient safety violation; cross-contamination risk |
| DN-2.5 | **Rushed Clinical Judgement Under Production Pressure** | Allowing daily production targets set by DSOs or practice owners to influence clinical decisions such as treatment timing, material selection, or extraction versus restoration | Clinical judgment compromise; patient harm |

## Category 3: Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DN-3.1 | **Medicaid Acceptance Refusal** | Declining to accept Medicaid patients while maintaining that the practice serves the community, effectively excluding low-income populations from care | Systemic access denial; health equity violation |
| DN-3.2 | **Rural Dental Desert Creation** | Consolidating practices into affluent suburban areas while closing rural or low-income clinics, creating geographic zones with no accessible dental providers | Geographic access abandonment; health disparity reinforcement |
| DN-3.3 | **Pediatric Dental Underinvestment** | Allocating minimal appointment slots for children, particularly those on public insurance, resulting in long wait times and deferred care during critical development periods | Pediatric access neglect; developmental harm |
| DN-3.4 | **Specialist Referral Barriers for Low-Income Patients** | Failing to establish referral pathways for low-income patients who require endodontic, periodontic, or oral surgery services beyond general practice capability | Abandonment of complex care needs; class-based care disparity |

---

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DN-4.1 | **Patient Data Sale to Dental Product Companies** | Selling patient treatment histories, demographic data, and diagnostic records to dental product manufacturers or marketing firms without meaningful informed consent | Health data privacy violation; patient exploitation |
| DN-4.2 | **Insurance Claim Data Monetization** | Aggregating and selling insurance claim data including procedure codes, treatment outcomes, and payment patterns to data brokers or analytics firms | Data brokerage; insurance privacy violation |
| DN-4.3 | **Treatment Plan Sharing With Third-Party Financiers** | Sharing detailed treatment plans and financial need assessments with third-party healthcare credit companies who use the data to target patients for financing products | Patient targeting; financial conflict exploitation |
| DN-4.4 | **Review Solicitation Manipulation** | Systematically soliciting positive reviews from satisfied patients while discouraging or impeding negative reviews through selective follow-up or review gating | Reputation manipulation; consumer deception |

---

## Category 5: Insurance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DN-5.1 | **Frequency Limitation Gaming** | Splitting procedures or rescheduling treatment to circumvent insurance frequency limitations on cleanings, examinations, or radiographs | Insurance fraud; benefit manipulation |
| DN-5.2 | **Alternative Benefit Clause Exploitation** | Billing for the most expensive procedure when a less costly alternative would suffice, exploiting clauses that require payment at the submitted procedure level | Benefit maximization at patient and payer expense |
| DN-5.3 | **Pre-Authorization Delay Tactics** | Failing to submit pre-authorization requests in a timely manner or submitting incomplete information to justify proceeding without authorization while holding patients liable for denial | Administrative burden shifting; patient financial risk |
| DN-5.4 | **Coordination of Benefits Manipulation** | Exploiting dual-coverage situations by billing both insurers for the full allowed amount rather than coordinating benefits, resulting in overpayment or patient balance billing | Double billing; coordination fraud |
| DN-5.5 | **Downcoding Non-Disclosure** | Accepting insurance payment at a lower procedure code while billing the patient for the balance of the higher-coded procedure without disclosing that downcoding occurred | Balance billing concealment; patient overcharge |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Practice evidence-based treatment** — base all treatment recommendations on documented clinical necessity; reject production-driven treatment planning; disclose material substitutions and lab sources.
2. **Ensure pricing transparency** — provide written treatment cost estimates before procedures; disclose out-of-network status before referral; itemize material and laboratory costs for major restorations.
3. **Expand access** — allocate a meaningful proportion of appointment capacity to Medicaid and underserved patients; maintain referral pathways for specialist care; avoid geographic concentration that abandons rural communities.
4. **Protect patient data** — obtain explicit opt-in consent before sharing any patient data with third parties; prohibit sale of treatment or claims data to product companies and financiers; enforce honest review solicitation without gating.
5. **Honor insurance integrity** — submit accurate procedure codes; coordinate benefits properly; process pre-authorizations promptly and completely; disclose any downcoding or balance billing to patients.
