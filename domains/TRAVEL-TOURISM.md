# Travel & Tourism — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Travel & Tourism  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to travel agencies, tour operators, online booking platforms, and tourism boards. Tourism shapes local economies and cultures; unethical practices exploit both travelers and destinations, turning cultural heritage into extractable resources.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Fees

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TR-1.1 | **Drip Pricing on Packages**     | Advertising package prices without disclosing mandatory surcharges until checkout | Hidden cost practice                  |
| TR-1.2 | **Currency Conversion Markups**  | Offering dynamic currency conversion at unfavorable rates without disclosing the markup | Financial exploitation at point of sale |
| TR-1.3 | **Hidden Resort & Tourism Fees** | Adding mandatory daily fees not included in advertised room or package rates | Fee concealment; total cost deception |
| TR-1.4 | **Last-Minute Price Inflation**  | Raising prices dramatically for bookings near travel dates for non-premium inventory | Exploitation of urgency               |
| TR-1.5 | **Non-Refundable Trap Booking**  | Defaulting bookings to non-refundable rates while making flexible options hard to find | Steering toward disadvantageous terms |

## Category 2: Environmental Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TR-2.1 | **Overtourism Facilitation**    | Promoting high-volume tourism to fragile destinations beyond ecological carrying capacity | Environmental degradation            |
| TR-2.2 | **Wildlife Exploitation Tours**  | Selling animal encounter experiences (elephant rides, dolphin swims, tiger photos) sourced from abusive facilities | Animal cruelty profiteering           |
| TR-2.3 | **Carbon Offset Greenwashing**   | Selling carbon offsets for flights without verifying additionality, permanence, or real impact | Environmental deception               |
| TR-2.4 | **Coral Reef Damage Concealment** | Promoting reef activities (sunscreen, anchoring, trampling) without disclosing ecological impact | Environmental harm facilitation       |
| TR-2.5 | **Cruise Waste Opacity**         | Concealing waste discharge practices and fuel sulfur content from passengers and port communities | Pollution concealment                 |

## Category 3: Labor & Local Economy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TR-3.1 | **Tip Credit Exploitation**     | Structuring hospitality wages to rely on tips while charging guests premium service fees | Wage suppression; double-charging     |
| TR-3.2 | **Seasonal Worker Visa Dependence** | Employing seasonal workers under visa conditions that create fear of deportation if they report violations | Labor coercion                        |
| TR-3.3 | **Tour Guide Wage Suppression**  | Requiring guides to work for tips only or below minimum wage while collecting booking fees | Labor exploitation                    |
| TR-3.4 | **Local Revenue Leakage**        | Structuring all-inclusive resorts so minimal revenue reaches the local economy | Economic extraction from host communities |
| TR-3.5 | **Supply Chain Opacity**         | Failing to verify labor conditions in excursion and transport sub-contractors | Complicity in labor violations        |

## Category 4: Cultural & Community Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TR-4.1 | **Cultural Commodification**     | Packaging sacred ceremonies, traditional dress, and rituals as entertainment for tourists | Cultural disrespect; exploitation     |
| TR-4.2 | **Sacred Site Tourism Without Consent** | Operating tours to indigenous or sacred sites without community permission or benefit sharing | Violation of sovereignty and dignity  |
| TR-4.3 | **Community Displacement**        | Tourism development displacing long-term residents through rising costs or direct acquisition | Forced displacement for profit        |
| TR-4.4 | **Poverty Tourism**              | Selling tours of impoverished communities as "authentic experiences" without benefit to residents | Exploitation of vulnerability         |
| TR-4.5 | **Heritage Distortion**           | Revising historical narratives at heritage sites to satisfy tourist expectations over accuracy | Historical erasure; misrepresentation |

## Category 5: Data & Surveillance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TR-5.1 | **Traveler Tracking & Profiling** | Building comprehensive travel behavior profiles from booking, loyalty, and location data | Surveillance without clear purpose     |
| TR-5.2 | **Border Data Sharing**          | Sharing traveler data with government agencies beyond legal requirements | Over-compliance; privacy erosion      |
| TR-5.3 | **Loyalty Program Data Sale**    | Selling frequent-traveler data to third parties including insurers and marketers | Data exploitation of loyal customers  |
| TR-5.4 | **Location-Based Push Marketing** | Sending targeted promotions based on real-time geolocation during travel | Intrusive behavioral advertising      |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Display total price upfront** — include all mandatory taxes, fees, and surcharges in the first displayed price; eliminate drip pricing.
2. **Conduct carrying capacity assessments** — evaluate and publish environmental impact before promoting high-volume tourism to any destination.
3. **Ensure fair local economic participation** — commit to minimum percentages of revenue staying in host communities; publish local sourcing metrics.
4. **Obtain free, prior, and informed consent** — secure community approval before operating tourism at cultural and sacred sites; share revenue equitably.
5. **Implement traveler data rights** — allow travelers to access, export, and delete their profiles; prohibit sale of travel data to third parties without explicit opt-in consent.
