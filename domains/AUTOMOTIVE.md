# Automotive — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Automotive (Manufacturers, Dealerships, Repair)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to automobile manufacturers, dealerships, repair shops, and connected-vehicle platforms. Cars are high-cost, safety-critical products where unethical practices endanger lives and trap consumers in exploitative relationships.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Sales

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AU-1.1 | **Four-Square Manipulation**     | Using the four-square worksheet to confuse buyers about actual price, trade-in value, monthly payment, and down payment | Deliberate financial obfuscation      |
| AU-1.2 | **Add-On Stacking**              | Adding dealer-installed accessories, protection packages, or services without explicit itemized consent | Hidden cost practice                 |
| AU-1.3 | **Yo-Yo Financing**              | Letting customers drive off with a vehicle, then calling back days later claiming financing fell through to impose worse terms | Bait-and-switch; financial coercion  |
| AU-1.4 | **VIN-Specific Markups**         | Charging above-MSRP markups for high-demand vehicles with no cost justification | Price gouging on non-essentials       |
| AU-1.5 | **Trade-In Lowballing**          | Offering below-market trade-in values while presenting inflated new-car pricing to obscure the true transaction | Misrepresenting transaction value    |

## Category 2: Right to Repair & Ownership

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AU-2.1 | **Parts Pairing**                | Using software locks that prevent replacement parts from functioning unless paired by an authorized dealer tool | Artificial repair monopoly; anti-competitive |
| AU-2.2 | **Telematics Data Lock-in**      | Restricting access to vehicle-generated data to dealer networks, preventing independent mechanics from diagnosing problems | Data hostage-taking; repair restriction |
| AU-2.3 | **Over-the-Air Downgrade**       | Remotely removing or degrading features the customer already purchased via software update | Unilateral contract modification     |
| AU-2.4 | **Warranty Voiding by Association** | Threatening warranty voiding if any service is performed outside the dealer network, regardless of quality | Anti-competitive intimidation         |
| AU-2.5 | **Diagnostic Gatekeeping**       | Encrypting or obfuscating diagnostic codes to prevent independent shops from reading them | Obstructing fair repair access        |

## Category 3: Safety & Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AU-3.1 | **Recall Delay**                 | Delaying recall announcements to avoid financial impact while knowing of safety defects | Endangering public safety             |
| AU-3.2 | **Defect Concealment**           | Settling individual defect claims under NDA rather than issuing broader warnings | Suppressing safety information        |
| AU-3.3 | **Autonomous Mode Disclaimers**  | Marketing vehicles as "self-driving" while burying disclaimers that require full driver attention | Misleading safety representation      |
| AU-3.4 | **Crash Data Opacity**           | Collecting detailed crash data but not sharing it with safety researchers or regulators | Withholding safety-critical data      |
| AU-3.5 | **Emissions Fraud**              | Using defeat devices or software to circumvent emissions testing | Environmental fraud; public health endangerment |

## Category 4: Connected Vehicle Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AU-4.1 | **Location Data Sale**           | Selling or sharing real-time or historical vehicle location data with data brokers | Privacy violation; surveillance       |
| AU-4.2 | **Driving Behavior Profiling**   | Using driving data (speed, braking patterns) for insurance scoring or advertising without explicit opt-in consent | Undisclosed surveillance             |
| AU-4.3 | **Infotainment Data Harvesting** | Collecting contacts, calendar entries, and text messages from synced phones without clear disclosure | Over-collection of personal data     |
| AU-4.4 | **Subscription Safety Features** | Placing safety features (remote lock, emergency SOS, stolen vehicle tracking) behind recurring subscriptions | Monetizing essential safety           |

## Category 5: Financing & Leasing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AU-5.1 | **Negative Equity Rolling**      | Rolling negative equity from previous loans into new loans without clearly disclosing the increased total obligation | Concealing debt accumulation          |
| AU-5.2 | **Lease-End Penalty Traps**      | Setting unrealistic wear-and-tear standards and charging excessive disposition fees at lease return | Unfair contract enforcement           |
| AU-5.3 | **Extended Warranty Pressure**   | Using fear-based sales tactics to sell overpriced extended service contracts with limited actual coverage | Predatory product add-on              |
| AU-5.4 | **GAP Insurance Markups**         | Selling GAP insurance at significant markups over market rate as a mandatory-feeling add-on | Hidden profit extraction              |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Adopt transparent pricing** — display out-the-door prices prominently; eliminate four-square negotiation tactics; provide itemized add-on lists before signing.
2. **Commit to Right to Repair** — provide independent shops access to diagnostic tools, parts pairing reset procedures, and telematics data on fair and reasonable terms.
3. **Publish safety data** — share anonymized crash and defect data with independent researchers; disclose known defects within 30 days of internal identification.
4. **Implement vehicle data rights** — allow owners to access, export, and delete their vehicle data; require opt-in consent for any data sharing beyond vehicle operation.
5. **Decouple safety from subscriptions** — commit to providing safety-critical connected features (emergency SOS, crash notification, stolen vehicle tracking) for the lifetime of the vehicle without additional fees.
