# Real Estate — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Real Estate & Housing  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to real estate brokerages, property management companies, landlords, and housing platforms. Housing is a fundamental human need; unethical practices in this domain perpetuate inequality, displacement, and financial hardship.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Fee Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RE-1.1 | **Junk Fee Stacking**            | Charging undocumented fees (application, processing, admin, amenity) beyond advertised rent | Hidden cost practice; rent inflation  |
| RE-1.2 | **Algorithmic Rent Fixing**       | Using shared data platforms to coordinate rent increases across competing landlords | Price fixing; anti-competitive collusion |
| RE-1.3 | **Bait-and-Switch Listings**     | Advertising units at one price/condition, then claiming they're unavailable and offering inferior alternatives | Classic bait-and-switch              |
| RE-1.4 | **Predatory Security Deposits**  | Demanding deposits exceeding legal limits or withholding without itemized justification | Financial exploitation               |
| RE-1.5 | **Concession Clawbacks**         | Offering "one month free" promotions with lease terms that recover the discount through higher base rent | Concealed total cost                  |

## Category 2: Discrimination & Fair Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RE-2.1 | **Digital Redlining**            | Using algorithms to show or hide listings based on neighborhood demographics | Fair Housing Act violation; systemic segregation |
| RE-2.2 | **Steering**                     | Directing buyers or renters toward or away from neighborhoods based on protected class characteristics | Discriminatory practice               |
| RE-2.3 | **Source-of-Income Discrimination** | Refusing tenants who use housing vouchers despite ability to pay | Economic discrimination               |
| RE-2.4 | **Accessibility Misrepresentation** | Advertising units as accessible that do not meet ADA or local accessibility codes | False advertising; exclusion of disabled individuals |

## Category 3: Maintenance & Habitability

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RE-3.1 | **Retaliatory Neglect**          | Reducing maintenance responsiveness for tenants who file complaints or organize | Retaliation; habitability violation   |
| RE-3.2 | **Renoviction**                   | Using renovations as pretext to evict tenants and re-list at significantly higher rents | Displacement for profit               |
| RE-3.3 | **Utility Passthrough Abuse**     | Charging tenants for landlord-responsible utilities through opaque "RUBS" allocations | Cost shifting; billing fraud          |
| RE-3.4 | **Mold & Hazard Concealment**    | Failing to disclose known environmental hazards (lead, mold, asbestos) before lease signing | Withholding health-critical information |

## Category 4: Data & Surveillance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RE-4.1 | **Tenant Surveillance Overreach** | Installing smart-home devices (cameras, locks, sensors) that monitor beyond common areas without consent | Privacy invasion                     |
| RE-4.2 | **Screening Data Profiling**     | Using social media, criminal history, and credit scores beyond what is legally permissible to deny housing | Discriminatory screening              |
| RE-4.3 | **Smart Lock Coercion**          | Requiring tenants to use landlord-controlled smart locks without providing backup physical access | Access control; vulnerability to lockout |

## Category 5: Mortgage & Financing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RE-5.1 | **Reverse Redlining**            | Targeting minority neighborhoods for predatory loan products with unfavorable terms | Discriminatory lending                |
| RE-5.2 | **Appraisal Manipulation**       | Systematically undervaluing properties in minority neighborhoods, depressing wealth building | Racial wealth gap perpetuation       |
| RE-5.3 | **Foreclosure Mill Practices**   | Processing foreclosures without proper documentation review or homeowner outreach | Due process violation                 |
| RE-5.4 | **Adjustable Rate Obscurity**    | Presenting adjustable-rate mortgages without clearly modeling worst-case payment scenarios | Misleading financial disclosure       |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish total cost of occupancy** — disclose all fees, deposits, and monthly obligations before application, in a standardized format.
2. **Implement anti-discrimination auditing** — conduct annual fair-housing audits of listing algorithms, tenant screening, and agent behavior.
3. **Establish maintenance SLAs** — commit to response timelines for habitability issues and publish maintenance resolution metrics.
4. **Adopt data minimization in screening** — limit background checks to legally required and job/tenancy-relevant information; provide adverse action notices.
5. **Disclose smart-home data practices** — publish a tenant data notice before installation; provide opt-out alternatives for non-essential monitoring.
