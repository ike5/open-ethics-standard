# Food & Beverage Manufacturing — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Food & Beverage Manufacturing (Producers, Processors, Packagers)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to food and beverage manufacturers, processors, and packagers. Food is a daily-life essential where unethical practices directly threaten public health, exploit vulnerable populations, and obscure the true origins and contents of what people consume.

---

## Category 1: Ingredient Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FB-1.1 | **Hidden Sugars**                 | Listing added sugars under alternative names (evaporated cane juice, fruit juice concentrate, barley malt) to obscure total sugar content | Ingredient obfuscation; health misrepresentation |
| FB-1.2 | **Artificial Additive Renaming** | Rebranding synthetic additives with natural-sounding names (e.g., "plant-based coloring" for petroleum-derived dyes) | Deceptive ingredient labeling |
| FB-1.3 | **Allergen Cross-Contamination Concealment** | Failing to disclose shared facility or shared line allergen exposure despite known contamination risk | Withholding safety-critical information |
| FB-1.4 | **Ultra-Processed Classification Avoidance** | Designing formulations and labeling to avoid classification as ultra-processed while meeting the scientific criteria | Regulatory and consumer classification circumvention |
| FB-1.5 | **Substitute Ingredient Swaps**  | Quietly replacing higher-quality ingredients with cheaper alternatives without updating packaging or notifying consumers | Silent quality degradation; bait-and-switch |

## Category 2: Marketing to Children

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FB-2.1 | **Cartoon Characters on Unhealthy Food** | Using licensed characters, mascots, or animated figures to market high-sugar, high-sodium products directly to children | Exploiting developmental vulnerability |
| FB-2.2 | **School Sponsorship**            | Sponsoring school programs, sports teams, or cafeteria equipment in exchange for product placement or exclusivity agreements | Commercializing education; targeting minors |
| FB-2.3 | **Health Halo on Junk Food**      | Prominently highlighting a single positive attribute (e.g., "made with whole grains," "no artificial flavors") on products that are otherwise nutritionally poor | Misleading health perception |
| FB-2.4 | **Digital Targeting of Minors**   | Deploying gamified ads, influencer partnerships, or branded online content aimed at children and teens | Covert marketing to vulnerable populations |
| FB-2.5 | **Toy and Premium Incentives**    | Including toys, collectibles, or premiums with unhealthy meals to drive child demand | Manipulative purchase incentives for minors |

## Category 3: Supply Chain

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FB-3.1 | **Supplier Squeeze**              | Using market power to impose below-sustainability prices on farmers and raw-material suppliers, driving labor abuse and environmental shortcuts | Exploitative procurement; livelihood destruction |
| FB-3.2 | **Deforestation-Linked Ingredients** | Sourcing palm oil, soy, cocoa, or beef from suppliers linked to illegal or unsustainable deforestation without adequate traceability | Environmental destruction; supply chain opacity |
| FB-3.3 | **Unfair Trade Practices**       | Bypassing fair-trade premiums, reneging on contracts, or imposing unilateral price changes on smallholder producers | Farmer exploitation; contract abuse |
| FB-3.4 | **Forced Labor Tolerance**        | Failing to audit or act on forced-labor indicators in supplier operations across multi-tier supply chains | Complicity in modern slavery |
| FB-3.5 | **Water Rights Exploitation**      | Operating water-intensive production in water-stressed regions, depleting community water access | Environmental injustice; resource theft |

## Category 4: Labeling

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FB-4.1 | **Misleading Serving Sizes**      | Defining unrealistically small serving sizes to make nutritional numbers appear favorable | Deceptive nutritional presentation |
| FB-4.2 | **Origin Misrepresentation**      | Labeling products as locally sourced, "product of," or heritage-linked when ingredients are predominantly imported | Geographic deception; authenticity fraud |
| FB-4.3 | **Process Claims Without Verification** | Marketing products as "artisanal," "small-batch," "handcrafted," or "natural" without defined standards or third-party verification | Unverified process claims; consumer deception |
| FB-4.4 | **Certification Logo Misuse**     | Displaying certifications (organic, non-GMO, humane) that have lapsed, don't cover the full product, or come from shell certifiers | False trust signaling |

## Category 5: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FB-5.1 | **Recall Delay**                 | Delaying product recall announcements to clear inventory or minimize brand damage while contamination is known | Public health endangerment |
| FB-5.2 | **Test Result Suppression**       | Suppressing internal microbiological, chemical, or allergen test results that indicate safety failures | Withholding safety evidence; regulatory evasion |
| FB-5.3 | **Facility Inspection Avoidance** | Using scheduling tactics, shell entities, or jurisdictional loopholes to evade regulatory inspections | Regulatory circumvention; safety negligence |
| FB-5.4 | **Contaminant Threshold Gaming**  | Formulating products to test just below regulatory contaminant limits rather than minimizing contaminants | Minimal compliance; risk normalization |
| FB-5.5 | **Expiry Date Manipulation**      | Extending shelf-life dating beyond product safety evidence to reduce waste costs | Consumer safety gamble; date fraud |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Ensure full ingredient transparency** — list all added sugars collectively; use standardized additive nomenclature; disclose shared-facility allergen risks prominently on packaging.
2. **Cease marketing to children** — prohibit use of cartoon characters, licensed IP, toy incentives, and school sponsorship for products that fail defined nutritional thresholds.
3. **Implement supply chain traceability** — map ingredients to origin; audit tier-1 and tier-2 suppliers for forced labor and deforestation; publish annual supply chain transparency reports.
4. **Standardize and verify labeling claims** — use realistic serving sizes; require third-party verification for process and origin claims; remove lapsed certifications within 30 days.
5. **Prioritize rapid safety disclosure** — initiate recalls within 24 hours of confirmed contamination; report all internal test failures to regulators; maintain unannounced inspection readiness.
