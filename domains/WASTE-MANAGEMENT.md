# Waste Management — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Waste Management & Disposal  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to waste collection companies, landfill operators, recycling facilities, and waste-to-energy providers. Waste management affects every community; unethical practices poison environments, exploit workers, and shift costs onto those least able to resist.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Billing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WM-1.1 | **Weight-Based Billing Manipulation** | Overestimating waste weight through uncalibrated scales or inconsistent measurement | Systematic overcharging               |
| WM-1.2 | **Fee Escalation Without Service Change** | Increasing rates annually without corresponding improvements in collection, recycling, or disposal quality | Monopoly price exploitation           |
| WM-1.3 | **Contamination Fine Abuse**      | Issuing excessive contamination fines for recyclables without clear guidelines or visual standards | Penalty profiteering                  |
| WM-1.4 | **Fuel Surcharge Opacity**        | Adding fuel surcharges that do not adjust downward when fuel prices decline | Asymmetric pricing                    |
| WM-1.5 | **Contract Auto-Renewal with Escalation** | Auto-renewing contracts with rate escalation clauses buried in fine print | Forced acceptance of higher costs     |

## Category 2: Environmental Practices

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WM-2.1 | **Landfill Leachate Concealment** | Failing to monitor or report leachate contamination of groundwater near landfill sites | Environmental law violation; public health risk |
| WM-2.2 | **Recycling Contamination Fraud** | Diverting collected recyclables to landfills or incineration while representing collection as recycling | Service fraud; environmental deception |
| WM-2.3 | **Illegal Dumping Tolerance**     | Accepting waste at unpermitted sites or knowingly transporting waste to illegal disposal locations | Environmental crime facilitation       |
| WM-2.4 | **Methane Emission Underreporting** | Underreporting landfill methane emissions to avoid regulatory scrutiny and carbon credit obligations | Climate data fraud                    |
| WM-2.5 | **Incineration Emission Concealment** | Failing to monitor or disclose toxic emissions from waste-to-energy facilities | Air quality risk concealment           |

## Category 3: Labor & Worker Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WM-3.1 | **Sanitation Worker Safety Neglect** | Failing to provide adequate PPE, training, or safety protocols for hazardous waste handling | Endangering worker health             |
| WM-3.2 | **Chemical Exposure Non-Disclosure** | Not informing workers about the specific hazardous materials they may encounter on routes or at facilities | Withholding safety-critical information |
| WM-3.3 | **Contractor Misclassification**  | Treating route drivers and facility workers as independent contractors | Worker classification fraud            |
| WM-3.4 | **Heat Illness Prevention Failure** | Requiring prolonged physical labor in extreme temperatures without mandated rest or hydration breaks | Worker health endangerment            |

## Category 4: Data & Community

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WM-4.1 | **Waste Composition Data Sale**   | Selling data about commercial waste composition to competitors of generator companies | Corporate espionage facilitation       |
| WM-4.2 | **Customer Profiling from Waste** | Using waste volume and type data to profile households for targeted marketing | Privacy violation                     |
| WM-4.3 | **Facility Siting in Low-Income Areas** | Concentrating transfer stations and landfills in communities with less political power to resist | Environmental racism                  |
| WM-4.4 | **Community Benefit Agreement Failure** | Failing to deliver promised community investments, parks, or infrastructure after facility siting | Broken social contract                |
| WM-4.5 | **Odor and Emission Concealment** | Underreporting or misrepresenting nuisance odors and emissions affecting nearby residents | Community health impact concealment   |

## Category 5: Recycling & Sustainability Claims

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WM-5.1 | **Zero-Waste Claims Without Verification** | Marketing zero-waste commitments without third-party verification or transparent diversion metrics | Greenwashing                          |
| WM-5.2 | **Recycling Rate Inflation**      | Reporting recycling rates that include waste sent to facilities that subsequently landfill it | Statistical deception                 |
| WM-5.3 | **Extended Producer Responsibility Avoidance** | Lobbying against EPR legislation that would shift waste management costs to product manufacturers | Shifting public cost to taxpayers     |
| WM-5.4 | **Carbon Credit Misrepresentation** | Selling carbon credits from landfill gas capture that do not represent genuine additional emissions reduction | Carbon market fraud                   |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Implement billing transparency** — use calibrated, auditable scales; publish rate methodologies; provide real-time weight data to commercial customers.
2. **Publish diversion metrics** — disclose actual recycling, composting, and landfill rates verified by independent auditors; report destination of all collected materials.
3. **Ensure worker safety** — provide comprehensive PPE and hazmat training; implement heat illness prevention protocols; disclose all chemicals workers may encounter.
4. **Conduct environmental justice reviews** — assess cumulative environmental burden on communities near existing and proposed facilities; prioritize mitigation in overburdened areas.
5. **Adopt data ethics** — prohibit sale of customer waste data; conduct annual privacy impact assessments; publish facility emissions and leachate monitoring data publicly.
