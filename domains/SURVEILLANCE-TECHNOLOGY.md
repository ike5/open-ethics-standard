# Surveillance Technology — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Surveillance Technology & Equipment  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to manufacturers, developers, and distributors of surveillance technology and equipment, including facial recognition systems, predictive policing platforms, interception tools, monitoring software, and associated analytics. Surveillance technology concentrates power asymmetrically: the watched have no reciprocal ability to watch the watchers. When sold without ethical constraint, these tools enable authoritarian control, suppress dissent, violate privacy at population scale, and disproportionately harm marginalized communities.

---

## Category 1: Sales & Export

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SV-1.1 | **Authoritarian Government Sales**   | Providing surveillance systems to governments with documented records of human rights abuses, political repression, or free speech suppression | Enabling authoritarian control; human rights complicity |
| SV-1.2 | **Dual-Use Export Evasion**           | Circumventing export controls by classifying surveillance tools as civilian or commercial products | Regulatory evasion; enabling repression |
| SV-1.3 | **End-User Verification Failure**    | Failing to conduct due diligence on end users, allowing intermediaries to redirect tools to prohibited entities | Negligent enabling of abuse            |
| SV-1.4 | **Repressive Regime Customization**  | Customizing surveillance features—such as language packs, target profiles, or integration with local enforcement systems—for known repressive governments | Active complicity in repression       |

## Category 2: Civil Liberties

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SV-2.1 | **Facial Recognition Bias**          | Deploying facial recognition systems with demonstrated demographic bias leading to higher false-positive rates for people of color | Discriminatory enforcement; wrongful identification |
| SV-2.2 | **Predictive Policing Enabling**     | Providing tools that target individuals or neighborhoods for policing based on historically biased data without bias mitigation | Automating and amplifying systemic bias |
| SV-2.3 | **Mass Surveillance Facilitation**   | Building or selling systems designed to monitor entire populations rather than specific investigative targets | Enabling population-level privacy violation |
| SV-2.4 | **Protest Monitoring Capability**     | Marketing or enabling features that identify, track, or profile protesters and political dissidents | Suppressing democratic participation; enabling political repression |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SV-3.1 | **Bulk Collection Tool Marketing**   | Designing and marketing tools specifically for dragnet data collection rather than targeted, lawful investigation | Enabling mass privacy violation       |
| SV-3.2 | **Data Retention Facilitation**      | Building systems that indefinitely retain surveillance data without retention limits, deletion protocols, or oversight triggers | Enabling perpetual surveillance; privacy violation |
| SV-3.3 | **Cross-Referencing Capability Concealment** | Failing to disclose that surveillance data can be cross-referenced with other databases to build comprehensive individual profiles | Hiding scale of privacy intrusion     |
| SV-3.4 | **Interception Backdoor Marketing** | Marketing network interception capabilities or backdoor access as product features to government or corporate clients | Enabling unlawful surveillance; undermining communications security |

## Category 4: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SV-4.1 | **Shadowy Corporate Structures**     | Operating through shell companies, complex ownership structures, or front organizations to obscure surveillance product origins | Accountability evasion; enabling covert repression |
| SV-4.2 | **Government Contract Opacity**      | Classifying or concealing surveillance contracts, preventing public awareness of government monitoring capabilities | Undermining democratic oversight; consent violation |
| SV-4.3 | **Algorithmic Bias Non-Disclosure**  | Failing to publish or disclose known bias, error rates, or demographic performance disparities in surveillance algorithms | Hiding discriminatory impact; accountability avoidance |
| SV-4.4 | **Audit Trail Prevention**           | Designing systems that prevent independent auditing, logging, or forensic review of surveillance operations | Enabling unaccountable monitoring; obstructing oversight |

## Category 5: Worker Impact

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SV-5.1 | **Employee Monitoring Tool Development** | Building surveillance products specifically designed to monitor, track, or control workers beyond what is necessary for legitimate business operations | Enabling workplace oppression; privacy violation |
| SV-5.2 | **Union-Busting Surveillance**       | Providing tools or services designed to identify, track, or undermine labor organizing activities | Suppressing worker rights; enabling anti-union campaigns |
| SV-5.3 | **Workplace Behavior Scoring**       | Creating systems that assign behavioral scores to employees based on surveillance data for performance or compliance ranking | Algorithmic management without consent; dignity violation |
| SV-5.4 | **Keystroke Logging Facilitation**    | Offering keystroke logging or screen capture as employee monitoring features without affected worker disclosure | Covert surveillance; privacy violation |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Conduct human rights due diligence** — screen all customers and end users against human rights records; refuse sales to governments or entities under sanction or with documented abuse patterns.
2. **Implement use restrictions** — contractually prohibit use of products for mass surveillance, political repression, or labor suppression; require lawful-use attestations from buyers.
3. **Publish bias and error audits** — commission independent audits of algorithmic performance across demographics; disclose error rates publicly; suspend deployment where bias exceeds defined thresholds.
4. **Enforce data retention limits** — build deletion protocols into all systems; default to minimal retention; never design for indefinite storage.
5. **Maintain corporate transparency** — publish customer lists where legally permissible; disclose government contracts; refuse to operate through obscuring corporate structures.
6. **Protect workers from surveillance products** — refuse contracts for union-busting or excessive worker monitoring tools; disclose all monitoring capabilities to affected workers.
