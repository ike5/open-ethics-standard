# Cannabis & CBD — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Cannabis & CBD (Producers, Dispensaries, CBD Brands, Testing Labs)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to cannabis and CBD producers, dispensaries, testing laboratories, and related businesses. Operating in a landscape of inconsistent regulation, this industry faces heightened ethical risk where unsafe products, deceptive labeling, and predatory practices can directly harm consumers in an emerging market.

---

## Category 1: Labeling & Potency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CB-1.1 | **Inaccurate THC/CBD Content**    | Selling products with actual cannabinoid content significantly above or below labeled amounts, whether through negligence or deliberate inflation | Consumer safety risk; misrepresentation |
| CB-1.2 | **Synthetic Cannabinoid Misrepresentation** | Marketing synthetic cannabinoids or isolated CBD as "natural" or "full-spectrum" products, concealing the presence of lab-synthesized compounds | Product identity fraud              |
| CB-1.3 | **Unverified Health Claims**      | Making therapeutic or medical claims (pain relief, anxiety reduction, disease treatment) without FDA approval or clinical evidence | Health fraud; exploiting vulnerable consumers |
| CB-1.4 | **Terpene & Cannabinoid Profile Fraud** | Falsifying or omitting terpene profiles and minor cannabinoid content to make products appear more desirable or match market trends | Mislabeling; informed consent failure |
| CB-1.5 | **Origin Misrepresentation**      | Labeling products as sungrown, organic, or locally sourced when they are greenhouse-grown, conventionally farmed, or imported | Origin fraud; false premium positioning |

## Category 2: Pricing & Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CB-2.1 | **Tax Gouging in Legal Markets**  | Passing excessive or unjustified tax surcharges directly to consumers beyond what regulation requires, exploiting the legal market premium | Price exploitation; regulatory abuse |
| CB-2.2 | **Medical vs. Recreational Price Gaming** | Charging different prices for identical products depending on whether the buyer holds a medical card, inflating costs for patients with documented needs | Patient exploitation                |
| CB-2.3 | **Equity License Exploitation**   | Using social equity license holders as fronts while real control, profits, and decision-making remain with established operators | Exploiting restorative justice programs |
| CB-2.4 | **Predatory Delivery Fees**       | Imposing excessive delivery or service fees targeting medical patients and low-mobility consumers who lack dispensary access | Exploiting accessibility barriers   |

## Category 3: Safety & Testing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CB-3.1 | **Lab Shopping for Favorable Results** | Sending product to multiple testing laboratories and selecting the certificate of analysis with the most favorable potency or safety results | Testing fraud; consumer deception    |
| CB-3.2 | **Pesticide Residue Concealment** | Selling products that fail pesticide residue standards by withholding failed test results or using unaccredited laboratories | Toxin exposure; health endangerment |
| CB-3.3 | **Heavy Metal Contamination**      | Distributing flower or concentrates contaminated with lead, arsenic, cadmium, or other metals from soil or processing equipment without testing | Heavy metal poisoning risk           |
| CB-3.4 | **Solvent Residue in Concentrates** | Selling extracts with residual butane, propane, or ethanol above safe limits without disclosing processing methods or test results | Chemical exposure; process opacity   |
| CB-3.5 | **Mold & Microbial Concealment**   | Failing to test for or disclose mold, yeast, and microbial contamination, especially in products sold without regulatory oversight | Infection risk; consumer endangerment |

## Category 4: Marketing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CB-4.1 | **Appeal-to-Minors Packaging**    | Using candy-style packaging, cartoon imagery, sweet flavors, or branding that mimics snack products likely to attract underage consumers | Youth targeting; regulatory circumvention |
| CB-4.2 | **Medical Claims Without FDA Approval** | Advertising cannabis or CBD products as treatments, cures, or preventatives for specific diseases without FDA-reviewed clinical evidence | Health fraud; regulatory violation   |
| CB-4.3 | **Celebrity Endorsement Without Disclosure** | Using paid celebrity or influencer endorsements without disclosing financial arrangements, creating misleading social proof | Undisclosed paid promotion          |
| CB-4.4 | **Addiction Denialism**           | Marketing cannabis as entirely non-addictive while minimizing or omitting information about dependency risk, withdrawal, or use disorder | Minimizing health risk; informed consent failure |

## Category 5: Banking & Financial

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CB-5.1 | **Cash-Only Forcing Due to Federal Conflict** | Maintaining cash-only operations that expose employees and customers to robbery risk rather than pursuing available banking alternatives or transparently explaining limitations | Unnecessary safety risk; financial exclusion |
| CB-5.2 | **Predatory Lending to Growers**  | Extending high-interest loans to cultivators with crop-as-collateral terms that exploit the limited financing options available in a federally restricted industry | Predatory lending; debt trapping     |
| CB-5.3 | **Insurance Redlining**           | Denying coverage, charging excessive premiums, or imposing unreasonable conditions on cannabis businesses based on industry stigma rather than actuarial risk | Discriminatory financial practices   |
| CB-5.4 | **Investor Equity Extraction**     | Structuring investment deals that give financiers disproportionate equity, IP rights, or profit participation from social equity operators | Exploiting disadvantaged license holders |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Ensure accurate labeling** — test every batch with accredited, independent laboratories; display certificates of analysis prominently; correct potency discrepancies within 10% tolerance.
2. **Practice fair pricing** — separate legitimate tax pass-through from markup; offer consistent pricing between medical and recreational channels; reject equity license front arrangements.
3. **Guarantee product safety** — use only accredited laboratories; publish all test results including failures; implement mandatory heavy metal, pesticide, and microbial screening.
4. **Market responsibly** — prohibit packaging and flavors appealing to minors; cease unapproved health claims; disclose all paid endorsements; communicate dependency risks honestly.
5. **Promote financial equity** — pursue safe banking solutions; cap interest rates on cultivator loans at reasonable thresholds; refuse front arrangements that exploit equity license holders.
