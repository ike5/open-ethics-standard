# Credit Reporting & Credit Bureaus — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Credit Reporting & Credit Bureaus  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to credit bureaus, credit scoring companies, and consumer reporting agencies. Credit reports determine access to housing, employment, insurance, and financial services; inaccuracies and opaque practices in this domain can destroy livelihoods with no meaningful recourse.

---

## Category 1: Data Accuracy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CR-1.1 | **Mixed Files**                  | Merging credit files of different consumers who share similar names or identifiers, creating erroneous reports | False attribution; credit damage      |
| CR-1.2 | **Dispute Investigation Failure** | Conducting perfunctory investigations of consumer disputes, often simply verifying with the furnisher rather than independently reviewing | Denial of due process                 |
| CR-1.3 | **Outdated Information Persistence** | Retaining negative items beyond the legally required removal timeframe (7 years for most items, 10 for bankruptcy) | Credit score suppression beyond law   |
| CR-1.4 | **Incorrect Public Records**      | Attributing court judgments, tax liens, or bankruptcies to the wrong consumer | Reputational and financial harm       |
| CR-1.5 | **Furnisher Error Propagation**   | Failing to flag or correct systemic errors from data furnishers, allowing inaccurate data to persist across cycles | Compounding inaccuracy                |

## Category 2: Access & Equity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CR-2.1 | **Discriminatory Scoring Proxies** | Using factors like ZIP code, shopping patterns, or banking behavior that correlate with race and income as scoring inputs | Algorithmic discrimination             |
| CR-2.2 | **Thin File Penalties**           | Penalizing consumers with limited credit history — often low-income, immigrant, or young consumers — with low scores that block access | Structural exclusion                 |
| CR-2.3 | **Score Opacity**                | Providing credit scores without clear explanation of how specific behaviors affect the score, preventing informed improvement | Information asymmetry exploitation   |
| CR-2.4 | **Credit Deserts**                | Withdrawing scoring models or products from communities with limited traditional banking, deepening financial exclusion | Redlining by algorithm                |

## Category 3: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CR-3.1 | **Dispute Resolution Delays**     | Taking longer than the legally required 30 days to investigate and resolve disputes, during which negative items remain on reports | Prolonging credit damage              |
| CR-3.2 | **Pay-to-View Credit Data**       | Charging consumers to access their own credit reports and scores beyond the one free annual report | Monetizing access to personal data    |
| CR-3.3 | **Adverse Action Notice Inadequacy** | Providing vague adverse action notices that fail to specify which credit report item led to denial | Blocking consumer self-correction     |
| CR-3.4 | **Free Report Funneling**          | Directing consumers from free annual credit report sites to paid subscription products through dark patterns | Coerced upsell                       |

---

## Category 4: Data Practices

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CR-4.1 | **Trigger Lead Sales**           | Selling consumer contact information to lenders when a credit inquiry triggers a prescreened offer, without meaningful consent | Privacy violation; data brokering     |
| CR-4.2 | **Data Retention Beyond Requirement** | Retaining consumer data indefinitely beyond legal requirements, creating perpetual surveillance profiles | Unnecessary data hoarding             |
| CR-4.3 | **Unauthorized Soft Inquiry Aggregation** | Conducting account review inquiries that aggregate data for scoring models without consumer awareness | Invisible data collection            |
| CR-4.4 | **Employer Access Facilitation**   | Providing credit reports to employers for hiring decisions beyond the scope of financial responsibility roles | Scope creep; employment discrimination |

---

## Category 5: Security & Breach Response

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CR-5.1 | **Breach Notification Delay**    | Delaying notification to consumers after data breaches beyond legally required timelines to manage public relations | Withholding security information      |
| CR-5.2 | **Inadequate Identity Verification** | Allowing credit report access with minimal verification, enabling identity theft and account takeover | Security negligence                   |
| CR-5.3 | **Credit Freeze Accessibility**    | Making credit freezes difficult to place or remove, requiring multiple steps or paid services in practice | Blocking consumer protection tools    |
| CR-5.4 | **Breach Insurance Upselling**    | Using data breach events as opportunities to sell identity theft monitoring rather than improving baseline security | Profiting from own negligence         |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Implement rigorous data accuracy protocols** — conduct independent verification of disputed items; maintain error rate dashboards; resolve mixed files within 15 days of identification.
2. **Eliminate discriminatory proxies** — audit scoring models annually for disparate impact; remove factors that correlate with protected classes; provide free score simulators.
3. **Provide unrestricted consumer access** — offer unlimited free access to credit reports and scores; provide clear, plain-language explanations of all scoring factors.
4. **End trigger lead sales** — prohibit sale of consumer contact data triggered by credit inquiries; require explicit opt-in for any prescreened offers.
5. **Strengthen security and breach response** — implement multi-factor authentication for all report access; notify consumers of breaches within 72 hours; provide free credit monitoring for 5 years after any breach.
