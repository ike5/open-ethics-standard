# Digital Advertising — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Digital Advertising (Ad Tech, Programmatic, Social Ads, DSPs)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to digital advertising platforms, ad tech intermediaries, demand-side platforms, and social media advertising systems. Digital advertising's opacity, surveillance infrastructure, and real-time targeting capabilities enable discrimination, fraud, and environmental waste at unprecedented scale—often invisible to the people affected.

---

## Category 1: Data Collection

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DI-1.1 | **Cross-Site Tracking Without Consent** | Following users across websites and apps via tracking pixels, device graphs, or identifier sharing without affirmative consent | Surveillance without knowledge        |
| DI-1.2 | **Fingerprinting as Cookie Alternative** | Using browser fingerprinting, canvas fingerprinting, or device identification techniques to circumvent cookie restrictions and user privacy choices | Evading user privacy preferences      |
| DI-1.3 | **Children's Data Harvesting**    | Collecting behavioral, location, or demographic data from users under 13 (or under applicable age thresholds) for ad targeting | Exploiting minors                     |
| DI-1.4 | **Health & Sensitive Category Inference** | Inferring health conditions, pregnancy, financial distress, or other sensitive attributes from browsing behavior for ad targeting | Sensitive data exploitation           |
| DI-1.5 | **Data Retention Beyond Purpose**  | Retaining granular behavioral data indefinitely or well beyond the period needed for ad delivery, creating dossiers on individuals | Purpose creep; data hoarding          |

## Category 2: Ad Fraud

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DI-2.1 | **Bot Traffic Selling**           | Selling ad impressions or clicks generated by bots, botnets, or automated scripts as if they represent genuine human audiences | Fraud; advertiser deception          |
| DI-2.2 | **Made-for-Advertising Sites**    | Creating or distributing ads through low-quality websites designed solely to generate ad revenue with no editorial value or real audience | Traffic fabrication                   |
| DI-2.3 | **Viewability Manipulation**      | Using techniques (stacked ads, auto-scroll, 1x1 pixels) to register ad impressions as "viewable" when they are never actually seen by a person | Impression fraud                      |
| DI-2.4 | **Domain Spoofing**              | Misrepresenting the domain or app where an ad will appear, selling premium inventory that actually runs on low-value or unsafe placements | Inventory fraud                       |
| DI-2.5 | **Attribution Fabrication**       | Manipulating last-click or view-through attribution to claim credit for conversions that occurred through other channels | Misrepresenting ad effectiveness     |

## Category 3: Targeting & Discrimination

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DI-3.1 | **Redlining via Exclusion Targeting** | Using geographic, demographic, or behavioral exclusions to prevent certain communities from seeing housing, credit, or employment ads | Algorithmic discrimination            |
| DI-3.2 | **Housing & Employment Ad Discrimination** | Enabling or tolerating ad targeting that violates fair housing and equal employment laws by allowing exclusion by zip code, race proxy, or income level | Civil rights violation                |
| DI-3.3 | **Mental Health Targeting for Predatory Ads** | Targeting individuals exhibiting signs of depression, anxiety, or emotional distress with ads for predatory loans, gambling, or exploitative services | Vulnerability exploitation            |
| DI-3.4 | **Financial Distress Targeting**  | Targeting users identified as financially vulnerable with ads for high-interest loans, for-profit debt relief, or subprime financial products | Predatory targeting                   |
| DI-3.5 | **Political Microtargeting Opacity** | Enabling granular political ad targeting without disclosing the targeting criteria, audience size, or demographic basis to the public | Undermining democratic transparency   |

---

## Category 4: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DI-4.1 | **Dark Ads with No Public Archive** | Running political, issue, or commercial ads visible only to narrow audience segments with no public record of their content or targeting | Undisclosed messaging; shadow advertising |
| DI-4.2 | **Undisclosed Sponsored Content**  | Publishing paid or promoted content without clear, prominent disclosure that a commercial interest paid for placement | Deceptive content; astroturfing       |
| DI-4.3 | **Political Ad Origin Concealment** | Allowing political ads to run without disclosing the true funder, using shell organizations or intermediaries to mask origin | Electoral transparency violation      |
| DI-4.4 | **Ad Library Inaccessibility**     | Maintaining ad transparency libraries that are incomplete, delayed, unsearchable, or lack targeting data | Undermining accountability             |
| DI-4.5 | **Influencer Marketing Obfuscation** | Failing to enforce clear disclosure (#ad, #sponsored) on influencer partnerships and paid product placements | Deceptive endorsement                  |

---

## Category 5: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DI-5.1 | **Carbon Cost of Programmatic Ad Chains** | Running real-time bidding auctions involving dozens of intermediaries for each impression, generating significant computational energy waste | Environmental externalization        |
| DI-5.2 | **Auto-Play Video Waste**         | Serving auto-play video ads that consume bandwidth and energy whether or not the user watches them, inflating viewability metrics | Resource waste; environmental cost    |
| DI-5.3 | **Data Center Impact of Ad Tech** | Operating massive data infrastructure to store, process, and auction behavioral profiles with no accountability for the resulting carbon footprint | Unchecked environmental impact        |
| DI-5.4 | **Redundant Bid Request Pollution** | Sending bid requests to hundreds of demand-side platforms simultaneously for a single impression, multiplying energy consumption | Infrastructure waste                  |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Enforce consent-first data collection** — require affirmative, informed consent before collecting cross-site or cross-app behavioral data; honor global privacy controls.
2. **Audit for ad fraud** — conduct quarterly third-party audits for bot traffic, viewability manipulation, and domain spoofing; publish summary findings.
3. **Eliminate discriminatory targeting** — remove exclusion targeting options for housing, employment, and credit ads; audit algorithms for proxy discrimination quarterly.
4. **Maintain complete ad transparency** — publish all ads (including political) in a searchable, real-time archive with targeting criteria, spend, and sponsor identity.
5. **Measure and disclose environmental impact** — report programmatic supply chain energy consumption and commit to reducing redundant bid requests and auto-play waste annually.
