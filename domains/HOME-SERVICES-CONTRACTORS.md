# Home Services & Contractors — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Home Services & Contractors (Remodelers, Plumbers, Electricians, Roofers, Landscapers)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to home service contractors, remodelers, and tradespeople. Home services involve high-value, low-frequency transactions where information asymmetry is extreme — homeowners rarely know the true scope, cost, or quality of work — creating persistent incentives for inflated pricing, shoddy work, and consumer exploitation during some of the largest financial decisions of a household's life.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HS-1.1 | **Bid Low and Inflate**           | Submitting intentionally low bids to win contracts, then inflating costs through change orders, "unforeseen conditions," and scope creep | Bait pricing; contract deception |
| HS-1.2 | **Unnecessary Upsell**            | Recommending unnecessary repairs, replacements, or system upgrades that serve revenue rather than the homeowner's interest | Fear-based selling; trust exploitation |
| HS-1.3 | **Material Markup Concealment**   | Marking up materials 50–200% above retail while presenting prices as "at cost" or failing to disclose markup rates | Hidden profit; price misrepresentation |
| HS-1.4 | **Emergency Price Inflation**     | Exploiting urgent situations (burst pipes, storm damage) to charge multiples of standard rates without transparent justification | Disaster exploitation; price gouging |
| HS-1.5 | **Allowance Under-Specification** | Setting unrealistically low material allowances in bids, knowing the homeowner will exceed them and pay the difference at full margin | Deceptive budgeting; hidden overage |

## Category 2: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HS-2.1 | **Misclassification**            | Classifying workers as independent contractors to avoid payroll taxes, workers' compensation, and benefits while controlling schedules and methods | Tax fraud; worker rights violation |
| HS-2.2 | **Undocumented Worker Exploitation** | Employing undocumented workers at below-market wages with threats of deportation if they report safety violations or wage theft | Coercive labor; modern slavery indicators |
| HS-2.3 | **Safety Negligence**             | Failing to provide fall protection, proper ventilation, or PPE on job sites; cutting corners on OSHA compliance to save time | Worker endangerment; regulatory violation |
| HS-2.4 | **Wage Theft**                    | Failing to pay overtime, final paychecks, or agreed-upon rates, knowing workers have limited recourse | Financial exploitation; labor law violation |

## Category 3: Quality

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HS-3.1 | **Code Violation Concealment**    | Covering up work that fails building code inspection — hiding improper wiring, unsealed plumbing, or structural shortcuts behind drywall or finish surfaces | Safety concealment; regulatory evasion |
| HS-3.2 | **Unlicensed Subcontracting**     | Subcontracting work to unlicensed or uninsured tradespeople while representing the entire team as qualified and credentialed | Credential misrepresentation; liability gap |
| HS-3.3 | **Warranty Avoidance**            | Writing warranties with vague terms, impossible claim procedures, or company-owned arbitration that effectively render guarantees unenforceable | Hollow warranty; accountability avoidance |
| HS-3.4 | **Substandard Material Substitution** | Replacing specified materials with cheaper alternatives while charging for the original specification | Material fraud; quality degradation |
| HS-3.5 | **Incomplete Scope Delivery**     | Leaving work unfinished or partially completed while demanding full payment per contract milestones | Non-delivery; payment extortion |

---

## Category 4: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HS-4.1 | **Lien Threats**                  | Filing or threatening mechanics' liens to coerce payment for disputed or incomplete work | Financial intimidation; coercive collection |
| HS-4.2 | **Project Abandonment**           | Starting projects, collecting deposits or progress payments, then disappearing or de-prioritizing the job for more lucrative work | Contract abandonment; financial harm |
| HS-4.3 | **Deposit Forfeiture**            | Requiring large upfront deposits (30–50%) that exceed material costs and are non-refundable regardless of contractor performance | Unbalanced risk; deposit exploitation |
| HS-4.4 | **Contract Ambiguity Exploitation** | Drafting intentionally vague contracts that allow wide interpretation of scope, materials, and timeline, then charging for every "clarification" | Informed-consent failure; contract manipulation |

## Category 5: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HS-5.1 | **Home Surveillance During Service** | Installing or accessing home security cameras, smart doorbells, or interior cameras during service visits without homeowner awareness | Unauthorized surveillance; privacy violation |
| HS-5.2 | **Smart Home Data Access**         | Accessing smart home systems (thermostats, locks, Wi-Fi networks) during service calls and retaining credentials or data | Unauthorized system access; cybersecurity risk |
| HS-5.3 | **Customer Information Sale**     | Selling customer contact information, project details, home layouts, or property values to lead generators, real estate brokers, or data aggregators | Data brokerage; privacy commodification |
| HS-5.4 | **Estimation Data Harvesting**    | Collecting detailed home information during estimates (room counts, appliance ages, income indicators) for resale regardless of contract award | Covert data collection; dual-use exploitation |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Practice transparent bidding** — provide itemized bids with material, labor, and markup breakdowns; disclose change-order processes and rates before contract signing; cap markup on materials at published rates.
2. **Protect worker rights** — classify workers according to legal standards; verify work authorization; provide OSHA-compliant safety equipment and training; pay all wages and overtime in full.
3. **Deliver verifiable quality** — invite independent inspections before covering work; list all subcontractor licenses and insurance on contracts; provide warranties with clear claim procedures and third-party arbitration options.
4. **Respect consumer protections** — limit deposits to documented material costs; provide lien waivers upon each payment; include scope, materials, timeline, and dispute resolution in plain-language contracts.
5. **Safeguard homeowner data** — prohibit unauthorized access to home networks, cameras, or smart systems; delete project data after completion unless retention is consented to; never sell customer information to third parties.
