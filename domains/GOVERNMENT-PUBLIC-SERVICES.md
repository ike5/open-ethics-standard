# Government & Public Services — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Government & Public Services (Federal, State, Local Agencies, Public Utilities)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to government agencies and public service providers. Governments hold a monopoly on coercive power and public trust; unethical practices in the public sector undermine democratic accountability, erode civil liberties, and disproportionately harm those with the least recourse.

---

## Category 1: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GP-1.1 | **FOIA Delay Tactics**           | Using excessive redactions, inflated fee estimates, endless extensions, and "search and review" backlogs to effectively deny Freedom of Information requests | Information suppression; democratic accountability erosion |
| GP-1.2 | **Algorithmic Decision Opacity**  | Deploying automated systems for benefits eligibility, risk scoring, or policing without publishing decision logic, training data, or audit results | Unaccountable automated governance |
| GP-1.3 | **Procurement Favoritism**       | Tailoring bid specifications, using sole-source justifications, or fast-tracking contracts to favored vendors while maintaining the appearance of competition | Corruption; competitive process circumvention |
| GP-1.4 | **Data Withholding from Oversight** | Refusing or delaying data sharing with legislative oversight bodies, auditors, or inspector generals | Obstructing constitutional checks and balances |
| GP-1.5 | **Meeting and Record Opacity**    | Conducting substantive decision-making in unofficial channels (private email, messaging apps) to avoid public records requirements | Records evasion; public process circumvention |

## Category 2: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GP-2.1 | **Surveillance Overreach**       | Collecting bulk communications, location, or association data beyond specific investigative warrants or statutory authority | Constitutional violation; mass surveillance |
| GP-2.2 | **Data Sharing Between Agencies Without Notice** | Sharing citizen data across departments (tax, health, welfare, immigration) without notifying affected individuals or providing opt-out mechanisms | Secret data integration; consent bypass |
| GP-2.3 | **Biometric Collection Without Consent** | Operating facial recognition, fingerprint, or iris scan systems in public spaces without informed, specific, and revocable consent | Biometric surveillance; bodily autonomy violation |
| GP-2.4 | **Predictive Policing Without Safeguards** | Using historical crime data to deploy predictive policing tools that perpetuate existing biases without independent auditing or community oversight | Algorithmic bias; discriminatory enforcement |
| GP-2.5 | **Citizen Profiling Aggregation** | Building comprehensive citizen profiles by merging datasets from multiple agencies to enable social scoring or risk classification | Mass profiling; digital authoritarianism |

## Category 3: Access & Equity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GP-3.1 | **Digital Divide in Service Delivery** | Moving essential services exclusively online without maintaining accessible offline alternatives, excluding residents without reliable internet or digital literacy | Systemic exclusion; access inequality |
| GP-3.2 | **Language Access Failures**      | Providing critical forms, notices, and services only in English in jurisdictions with significant non-English-speaking populations | Discriminatory access; civic exclusion |
| GP-3.3 | **Disability Accommodation Gaps** | Failing to meet ADA and equivalent standards for physical access, digital access, and reasonable accommodations in service delivery | Disability discrimination; legal non-compliance |
| GP-3.4 | **Service Desert Creation**       | Closing field offices, service centers, or public facilities in low-income or rural areas while maintaining presence in affluent areas | Geographic inequity; service withdrawal |
| GP-3.5 | **Means-Testing Complexity**      | Designing benefit applications with excessive documentation requirements that function as access barriers for the most vulnerable populations | Administrative exclusion; benefit denial by complexity |

## Category 4: Procurement

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GP-4.1 | **Vendor Lock-In**               | Designing systems and contracts that make migration to alternative vendors prohibitively expensive, ensuring perpetual dependency | Anti-competitive entrenchment; fiscal irresponsibility |
| GP-4.2 | **Cost Overrun Tolerance**        | Accepting chronic cost overruns on public projects without enforcing contractual penalties, conducting root-cause analysis, or terminating underperforming vendors | Fiscal negligence; accountability failure |
| GP-4.3 | **Revolving Door Contracting**    | Hiring former agency staff as consultants or contractors on the same programs they oversaw, creating inherent conflicts of interest | Conflict of interest; self-dealing |
| GP-4.4 | **Emergency Authority Abuse**      | Bypassing competitive bidding under emergency declarations for non-emergency procurements | Procurement process circumvention; authority abuse |
| GP-4.5 | **Small Business Exclusion**      | Structuring contract requirements (bond thresholds, experience levels) that effectively exclude small and minority-owned businesses | Marketplace exclusion; equity failure |

## Category 5: Accountability

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GP-5.1 | **Whistleblower Retaliation**    | Demoting, reassigning, or terminating employees who report waste, fraud, abuse, or safety concerns through official or public channels | Anti-accountability; witness intimidation |
| GP-5.2 | **Inspector General Understaffing** | Underfunding or understaffing oversight offices to render them ineffective, then citing "no findings" as evidence of compliance | Oversight defunding; manufactured compliance |
| GP-5.3 | **Audit Finding Non-Implementation** | Accepting audit recommendations publicly but failing to implement corrective actions, track progress, or report on remediation status | Performative accountability; reform resistance |
| GP-5.4 | **Political Interference in Operations** | Pressuring career staff to alter findings, delay enforcement, or prioritize politically favorable outcomes over factual analysis | Institutional integrity violation |
| GP-5.5 | **FOIA Exemption Overuse**        | Applying broad exemption claims (national security, deliberative process, privacy) to withhold information that would cause political embarrassment rather than genuine harm | Transparency evasion; public right denial |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Ensure information access** — process FOIA requests within statutory deadlines; publish proactive disclosures of budgets, contracts, and algorithmic system documentation; maintain official records on government systems only.
2. **Protect citizen data** — require specific statutory authority and individualized suspicion for surveillance; notify individuals of cross-agency data sharing; prohibit biometric collection without explicit legislative authorization and opt-in consent.
3. **Guarantee equitable access** — maintain offline service pathways; provide multilingual materials matching community demographics; conduct ADA compliance audits annually; locate offices proportional to population need.
4. **Reform procurement** — enforce competitive bidding with transparent scoring; prohibit revolving-door contracting within two years; implement contractual cost-overrun penalties; reserve a share of contracts for small businesses.
5. **Strengthen accountability** — protect whistleblowers with statutory anti-retaliation enforcement; fund inspector general offices at a minimum of 1% of agency budget; publish quarterly audit remediation progress reports.
