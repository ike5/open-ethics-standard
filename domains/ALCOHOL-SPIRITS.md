# Alcohol & Spirits — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Alcohol & Spirits (Breweries, Distilleries, Retailers, Delivery Platforms)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to the alcohol and spirits industry. Alcohol is a regulated substance with significant public health implications, and unethical practices in this domain exploit vulnerable consumers, obscure health risks, and undermine regulatory structures designed to protect communities.

---

## Category 1: Marketing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AL-1.1 | **Underage Audience Targeting via Social Media** | Using platform algorithms, influencer partnerships, or youth-oriented content to expose individuals below legal drinking age to alcohol marketing | Deliberate circumvention of age-gating to recruit underage consumers |
| AL-1.2 | **Health Benefit Claims**                     | Promoting alcohol consumption with unsubstantiated claims about cardiovascular, digestive, or other health benefits | Misleading consumers into believing alcohol carries therapeutic value |
| AL-1.3 | **Alcohol-by-Volume Opacity**                 | Obscuring or minimizing ABV information on packaging, menus, or digital listings | Preventing consumers from making informed decisions about potency and intake |
| AL-1.4 | **Lifestyle Association Marketing**           | Linking alcohol brands to success, attractiveness, or social acceptance in ways that exploit insecurity | Creating psychological dependency on alcohol for social belonging |
| AL-1.5 | **Event Sponsorship Without Safeguards**      | Sponsoring public events without providing non-alcoholic options or transportation alternatives | Prioritizing brand exposure over attendee safety and responsible consumption |

## Category 2: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AL-2.1 | **Below-Cost Selling to Drive Consumption**   | Pricing alcoholic beverages below cost to encourage higher volume purchases | Using loss-leader pricing to promote excessive consumption |
| AL-2.2 | **Happy Hour Pressure Tactics**               | Designing limited-time promotions that incentivize rapid or binge drinking | Engineering urgency that overrides responsible pacing decisions |
| AL-2.3 | **Tax Avoidance via Product Reclassification** | Reclassifying products (e.g., spirits as liqueurs) to exploit lower tax brackets | Evading regulatory revenue structures meant to offset social costs |
| AL-2.4 | **Volume Discount Escalation**                | Offering progressively steeper discounts for larger quantities | Incentivizing bulk purchases that exceed reasonable personal consumption |
| AL-2.5 | **Dynamic Pricing During High-Risk Periods**  | Raising prices during events known for excessive drinking while simultaneously promoting those events | Profiting from and enabling dangerous consumption environments |

---

## Category 3: Labeling

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AL-3.1 | **Ingredient Non-Disclosure**                 | Failing to list additives, colorants, flavorings, or allergens present in the product | Withholding information critical to consumer health and informed choice |
| AL-3.2 | **Calorie Concealment**                       | Omitting nutritional and caloric information from packaging or digital listings | Preventing consumers from managing dietary intake and health conditions |
| AL-3.3 | **Origin Misrepresentation**                  | Implying products originate from a specific region, distillery, or tradition when they do not | Deceiving consumers who rely on origin for quality and authenticity |
| AL-3.4 | **Craft Branding for Mass-Produced Goods**    | Using small-batch, handcrafted, or artisanal language for industrially manufactured products | Misleading consumers about production methods and product quality |
| AL-3.5 | **Age Statement Ambiguity**                   | Using vague or misleading age statements (e.g., "aged up to" without minimum) | Creating false impressions of product maturity and value |

---

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AL-4.1 | **Purchase Data Sale**                        | Selling consumer alcohol purchase histories to third parties without meaningful consent | Monetizing sensitive consumption data that could affect employment, insurance, or reputation |
| AL-4.2 | **Delivery App Age Verification Gaps**        | Operating alcohol delivery services with inadequate or easily circumvented age verification | Enabling underage access through negligent digital gatekeeping |
| AL-4.3 | **Loyalty Program Addiction Enabling**        | Designing rewards programs that incentivize frequent or high-volume purchases without consumption monitoring | Using gamification and rewards to reinforce potentially harmful drinking patterns |
| AL-4.4 | **Behavioral Profiling for Targeted Marketing** | Building consumer profiles based on drinking frequency, brand preference, or spending to deliver hyper-targeted promotions | Exploiting consumption patterns to deepen dependency |

---

## Category 5: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AL-5.1 | **Warning Label Inadequacy**                  | Providing insufficient, poorly placed, or obscured health warnings on products | Undermining the consumer's right to be informed about health risks |
| AL-5.2 | **Serving Size Manipulation**                 | Presenting non-standard serving sizes to obscure actual alcohol content per serving | Deceiving consumers about how much alcohol they are consuming |
| AL-5.3 | **Contamination Concealment**                 | Failing to promptly disclose known contamination, recall, or quality control failures | Endangering public health by withholding critical safety information |
| AL-5.4 | **Overserving for Profit**                    | Continuing to serve visibly intoxicated patrons to maximize revenue | Prioritizing revenue over patron safety and legal serving obligations |
| AL-5.5 | **Lack of Intervention Protocols**             | Operating without staff training or protocols for identifying and responding to intoxication | Neglecting duty of care to patrons and the broader community |

---

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Establish age-gating standards** — exceed minimum regulatory requirements across all marketing channels, including social media, influencer content, and delivery platforms.
2. **Implement responsible pricing policies** — prohibit below-cost selling, limit time-based pressure promotions, and ensure pricing transparency.
3. **Adopt full-disclosure labeling** — include ingredients, nutritional information, ABV, origin, and production methods in accessible formats.
4. **Govern consumer data ethically** — prohibit the sale of purchase data, require robust age verification for delivery, and audit loyalty programs for addiction-enabling design.
5. **Prioritize safety in operations** — mandate staff training, intervention protocols, contamination disclosure procedures, and visible warning labels.
