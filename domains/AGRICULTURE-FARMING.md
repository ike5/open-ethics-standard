# Agriculture & Farming — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Agriculture & Farming (Agribusiness, Growers, Supply Chains)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to agricultural enterprises, farming cooperatives, and food supply chains. Agriculture is foundational to human survival, and unethical practices in this domain exploit workers, degrade ecosystems, and undermine food security for communities worldwide.

---

## Category 1: Land & Resource Use

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AG-1.1 | **Water Rights Exploitation**     | Diverting or hoarding water resources beyond sustainable needs, depleting community water supplies in drought-prone regions | Resource hoarding; community harm    |
| AG-1.2 | **Pesticide Drift Onto Communities** | Applying aerial or broad-acre pesticides in conditions that cause drift onto neighboring communities, schools, or organic farms without adequate warning | Environmental justice violation; negligence |
| AG-1.3 | **Soil Degradation Concealment**  | Continuing intensive monoculture practices known to degrade topsoil while publicly promoting sustainability commitments | Greenwashing; long-term harm concealment |

## Category 2: Labor Practices

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AG-2.1 | **Farmworker Wage Suppression**   | Paying below living wages, using piece-rate systems that incentivize dangerous speed, or avoiding overtime obligations | Exploitative labor; wage theft        |
| AG-2.2 | **Hazardous Condition Exposure**  | Failing to provide protective equipment, shade, or safe conditions for workers handling pesticides, operating machinery, or working extreme heat | Endangering worker health            |
| AG-2.3 | **Child Labor in Supply Chains**  | Sourcing from suppliers that employ underage workers, or using subcontracting structures designed to obscure child labor involvement | Child exploitation; accountability avoidance |
| AG-2.4 | **Housing & Camp Conditions**      | Providing substandard, overcrowded, or unsanitary housing for seasonal or migrant workers | Worker dignity violation             |

## Category 3: Environmental Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AG-3.1 | **Deforestation for Farmland**    | Clearing forested or ecologically significant land for agricultural expansion without environmental review | Ecosystem destruction; carbon harm   |
| AG-3.2 | **Antibiotic Overuse in Livestock** | Administering antibiotics prophylactically at scale to compensate for crowded conditions, accelerating antimicrobial resistance | Public health endangerment           |
| AG-3.3 | **Fertilizer Runoff Concealment** | Downplaying or concealing the extent of nutrient pollution (dead zones, algal blooms, groundwater contamination) caused by fertilizer overapplication | Environmental harm concealment       |
| AG-3.4 | **Biodiversity Monoculture Push** | Promoting or enforcing single-crop systems that destroy pollinator habitats, erode genetic diversity, and increase vulnerability to pests | Undermining ecological resilience    |

---

## Category 4: Seed & Input Monopoly

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AG-4.1 | **Patent-Protected Seed Lock-In** | Prohibiting seed saving through patent enforcement or technology agreements, forcing repurchase each season | Restricting farmer autonomy; anti-competitive lock-in |
| AG-4.2 | **Herbicide Bundling**            | Bundling herbicide-resistant seeds with proprietary herbicides, creating chemical dependency cycles | Coercive commercial tying             |
| AG-4.3 | **GMO Labeling Opposition**        | Lobbying against mandatory labeling or transparency requirements for genetically modified ingredients | Consumer right-to-know suppression   |
| AG-4.4 | **Input Price Manipulation**      | Using market dominance to artificially inflate seed, fertilizer, or chemical prices for smallholder farmers | Monopolistic exploitation            |

---

## Category 5: Market & Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AG-5.1 | **Commodity Price Manipulation**  | Using market position or insider information to manipulate commodity prices at the expense of producers and consumers | Market manipulation; unfair trading   |
| AG-5.2 | **Supply Chain Opacity**          | Obscuring origin, labor conditions, and environmental practices through complex multi-tier supply chains | Accountability avoidance              |
| AG-5.3 | **Farmer Debt Trapping**          | Extending credit, equipment leases, or input loans with terms designed to create perpetual financial dependency | Predatory lending; indentured economics |
| AG-5.4 | **Contract Farming Exploitation** | Imposing one-sided contracts that transfer production risk to farmers while locking in below-market purchase prices | Power imbalance exploitation         |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Protect water and land** — conduct environmental impact assessments before expansion; disclose pesticide application schedules to neighboring communities; invest in soil regeneration.
2. **Ensure fair labor** — pay living wages; provide protective equipment and safe housing; audit supply chains for child labor with independent verification.
3. **Reduce environmental harm** — publish antibiotic usage data; implement nutrient runoff management plans; commit to zero deforestation with traceable verification.
4. **Respect farmer autonomy** — allow seed saving where legally possible; unbundle inputs; support mandatory GMO labeling in markets where consumers request it.
5. **Increase supply chain transparency** — disclose multi-tier sourcing; publish fair contract terms; eliminate predatory lending and debt-trapping practices.
