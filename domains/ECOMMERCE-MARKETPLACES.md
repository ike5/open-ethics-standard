# E-Commerce Marketplaces — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** E-Commerce Marketplaces (Online Retail Platforms, Third-Party Marketplaces)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to online retail platforms and third-party marketplaces. E-commerce marketplaces serve as digital town squares where consumers and sellers meet; unethical practices—counterfeit goods, seller exploitation, data harvesting, and deceptive pricing—undermine trust and distort markets that billions depend on.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Fees

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EC-1.1 | **Seller Fee Escalation**         | Progressively increasing referral fees, fulfillment fees, or storage fees without proportional service improvements, squeezing seller margins | Rent extraction; monopolistic abuse   |
| EC-1.2 | **Buy Box Manipulation for Own Products** | Designing buy box algorithms to favor the platform's private-label products over identical or superior third-party offerings | Self-preferencing; market distortion  |
| EC-1.3 | **Dynamic Pricing on Essentials** | Algorithmically raising prices on essential goods (medicine, baby formula, emergency supplies) during demand surges | Price gouging on necessities           |
| EC-1.4 | **Hidden Checkout Fees**           | Adding shipping, handling, or service fees late in the checkout flow after the user has committed to the purchase | Bait-and-add; cost concealment        |
| EC-1.5 | **Price Parity Enforcement**       | Contractually prohibiting sellers from offering lower prices on other platforms, artificially inflating consumer prices | Anti-competitive price fixing          |

## Category 2: Counterfeit & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EC-2.1 | **Failure to Remove Counterfeit Goods** | Allowing counterfeit products to remain listed despite verified reports, prioritizing listing volume over authenticity | Endangering consumers; enabling fraud |
| EC-2.2 | **Review Manipulation and Fake Reviews** | Tolerating or incentivizing fake reviews, merged product listings that combine reviews across different items, or review hijacking | Deceptive consumer representation      |
| EC-2.3 | **Unsafe Product Listing Delays**  | Taking weeks or months to delist products flagged as safety hazards, citing bureaucratic process while consumers remain at risk | Public safety negligence              |
| EC-2.4 | **Product Category Misclassification** | Deliberately miscategorizing products to avoid category-specific safety reviews or regulations | Regulatory evasion                     |
| EC-2.5 | **Recalled Product Resale**       | Failing to prevent the relisting of recalled products by third-party sellers after removal | Enabling sale of dangerous goods      |

## Category 3: Seller Exploitation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EC-3.1 | **Search Ranking Pay-to-Play**    | Requiring sellers to purchase advertising or pay for placement to achieve organic search visibility, blurring the line between ads and results | Monetizing fair access; deceptive ranking |
| EC-3.2 | **Forced Advertising**            | Making seller advertising spend mandatory through reduced visibility for non-participants, effectively taxing sellers for discoverability | Coercive commercial terms             |
| EC-3.3 | **Account Suspension Without Recourse** | Suspending seller accounts without transparent explanation, prompt appeal process, or human review, freezing inventory and funds | Due process violation; asset seizure   |
| EC-3.4 | **Contractual Unilateral Changes** | Modifying seller agreements, fee structures, or policies unilaterally with minimal notice, forcing take-it-or-leave-it acceptance | Unequal bargaining power              |
| EC-3.5 | **Seller Data Extraction**         | Requiring sellers to share detailed product, pricing, and sales data that the platform then uses to compete against them | Conflicts of interest; data extraction |

---

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EC-4.1 | **Purchase History Monetization** | Selling or sharing aggregated and individual purchase histories with advertisers, data brokers, or affiliated businesses | Privacy violation; data trafficking   |
| EC-4.2 | **Third-Party Seller Data Mining for Private Label** | Analyzing individual seller performance data—sales volume, margins, customer reviews—to identify and replicate profitable products as platform-owned alternatives | Intellectual property appropriation; unfair competition |
| EC-4.3 | **Cross-Device Tracking**          | Linking user activity across devices and sessions to build shopping behavior profiles without clear, affirmative consent | Surveillance overreach                |
| EC-4.4 | **Wish List & Cart Surveillance**  | Monitoring wish lists, carts, and browsing patterns to trigger targeted advertising or dynamic pricing based on intent signals | Manipulative profiling                |

---

## Category 5: Return & Refund

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EC-5.1 | **Return Window Erosion**         | Shortening return windows, adding conditional restrictions, or requiring original packaging that is discarded during use | Undermining consumer remedy           |
| EC-5.2 | **Restocking Fee Abuse**           | Applying restocking fees to all returns regardless of item condition, or inflating fees beyond actual processing cost | Punitive cost recovery                |
| EC-5.3 | **Refund Delay Tactics**          | Delaying refunds through processing hold periods, requiring multiple contacts, or issuing store credit instead of original payment method | Financial hostage-taking              |
| EC-5.4 | **Return Fraud Accusation Without Evidence** | Flagging or banning loyal customers for "return fraud" based on opaque algorithmic thresholds without human review or transparent appeal | Guilt by algorithm; consumer punishment |
| EC-5.5 | **Warranty Transfer Barriers**    | Restricting warranty claims to original purchasers only, denying coverage for legitimate second-hand buyers | Unfair warranty restriction           |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Maintain product authenticity** — implement proactive counterfeit detection, respond to verified reports within 48 hours, and publish quarterly takedown statistics.
2. **Ensure fair seller treatment** — provide transparent account suspension reasons, human-reviewed appeals within 5 business days, and 60-day notice for contract changes.
3. **Separate organic results from paid placement** — clearly label sponsored listings; do not reduce organic visibility as leverage for advertising spend.
4. **Protect consumer data** — prohibit use of seller data for competitive private-label development; obtain affirmative consent before cross-device tracking.
5. **Honor return and refund rights** — maintain minimum 30-day return windows, limit restocking fees to actual cost, and process refunds to the original payment method within 10 business days.
