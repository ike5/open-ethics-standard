# Domain: Human Resources — Recruitment

**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This standard defines ethical practices and violations within human resources recruitment, covering the hiring lifecycle from job posting through onboarding. It addresses systemic biases, data misuse, compensation inequity, worker misclassification, and invasive surveillance that undermine fair employment. Organizations operating under this domain standard commit to transparent, equitable, and privacy-respecting recruitment and employment practices.

---

## Hiring Bias

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| HR-1.1 | AI screening bias mitigation | Ensure automated resume-screening and candidate-ranking tools are audited for disparate impact across protected classes before deployment | Deploying AI screening tools that systematically disadvantage candidates based on race, gender, age, or other protected characteristics without audit or remediation |
| HR-1.2 | Salary history prohibition | Refrain from requesting or using a candidate's prior salary to set compensation, perpetuating historical pay gaps | Exploiting salary history data to suppress offers below market rate, reinforcing systemic pay inequities |
| HR-1.3 | Inclusive job description language | Use neutral, inclusive language in job postings and avoid unnecessary requirements that disproportionately exclude underrepresented groups | Publishing job descriptions containing gendered terms, unnecessary physical requirements, or arbitrary credential thresholds that deter qualified diverse applicants |
| HR-1.4 | Structured interview consistency | Apply uniform interview questions and evaluation rubrics across all candidates for the same role | Using unstructured or inconsistent interview processes that allow subjective bias to influence hiring decisions |
| HR-1.5 | Diverse candidate pipeline | Actively source candidates from underrepresented communities and maintain diversity metrics for candidate slates | Filling pipelines exclusively through referral networks that replicate existing workforce demographics |

## Data & Privacy

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| HR-2.1 | Resume data retention limits | Define and enforce clear retention schedules for candidate resumes and application materials, deleting data after the defined period | Retaining candidate personal data indefinitely without consent or legitimate business purpose |
| HR-2.2 | Candidate data sale prohibition | Never sell, share, or transfer candidate personal data to third parties for commercial purposes unrelated to the hiring process | Selling candidate resume data to data brokers, marketing firms, or other employers without explicit informed consent |
| HR-2.3 | Background check proportionality | Limit background checks to information directly relevant to the specific role, disclose scope to candidates, and obtain explicit consent | Conducting overly broad background investigations into financial history, social media, or personal relationships unrelated to job requirements |
| HR-2.4 | Consent-based data collection | Collect only the personal data necessary for the hiring decision and clearly inform candidates of what is collected and why | Gathering personal data beyond job requirements—such as social media profiles, political affiliations, or health records—without candidate knowledge |
| HR-2.5 | Data breach notification | Notify affected candidates promptly when their personal data held from the recruitment process is compromised in a breach | Failing to inform candidates of data breaches involving their application materials within a reasonable timeframe |

## Compensation

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| HR-3.1 | Pay transparency | Disclose salary ranges, compensation structures, and benefits information in job postings or upon candidate request | Deliberately withholding pay range information to suppress offer negotiations or maintain below-market compensation |
| HR-3.2 | Equity grant fair vesting | Structure equity grants with reasonable vesting schedules that do not disproportionately forfeit employee earnings | Implementing cliff vesting schedules that cause employees to lose significant earned equity upon departure just before the cliff date |
| HR-3.3 | Non-compete reasonableness | Use non-compete agreements only where legitimately necessary to protect trade secrets, limited in scope and duration | Requiring overbroad non-compete clauses that restrict employee mobility far beyond what is necessary to protect legitimate business interests |
| HR-3.4 | Internal pay equity auditing | Regularly audit compensation across role, tenure, and demographic groups to identify and correct unjustified pay disparities | Ignoring known pay inequities between employees performing substantially similar work based on gender, race, or other protected characteristics |
| HR-3.5 | Benefits parity | Offer equivalent benefits to all employees in comparable roles regardless of employment classification | Providing reduced or no benefits to certain employee classes performing the same work through structural loopholes |

## Worker Classification

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| HR-4.1 | Accurate worker classification | Classify workers according to the true nature of the working relationship, applying legal and ethical tests for employee versus contractor status | Misclassifying employees as independent contractors to avoid payroll taxes, benefits obligations, and labor protections |
| HR-4.2 | Gig worker benefit access | Provide gig and platform workers with pro-rated access to benefits, protections, and recourse mechanisms proportionate to their work contribution | Denying gig workers access to health insurance, injury coverage, or dispute resolution mechanisms despite dependence on platform income |
| HR-4.3 | Temp-to-perm pathway clarity | Clearly disclose the terms, likelihood, and timeline for temp-to-permanent conversion at the outset of temporary engagements | Using temp-to-perm promises as a recruitment lure while having no genuine intention or process for permanent conversion |
| HR-4.4 | Contract scope honesty | Define contractor engagements with clear deliverables, timelines, and autonomy expectations that reflect genuine contractor relationships | Exerting employee-level control over contractors while denying the corresponding employment benefits and protections |

## Surveillance

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| HR-5.1 | Monitoring proportionality | Limit employee monitoring to what is necessary for legitimate business purposes, disclosed transparently in advance | Deploying pervasive employee monitoring software that tracks activity, location, and communications far beyond legitimate business needs |
| HR-5.2 | Keystroke logging prohibition | Do not log keystrokes, screen captures, or other granular employee inputs except where legally required for regulated industries | Capturing keystrokes, clipboard contents, or screen images without employee knowledge to measure productivity or detect dissent |
| HR-5.3 | Off-hours behavior respect | Do not surveil, discipline, or penalize employees for lawful off-duty conduct unless directly relevant to job duties | Policing employees' personal social media, political activity, or lawful off-hours behavior through overt or covert surveillance |
| HR-5.4 | Monitoring data minimization | Collect only the minimum monitoring data necessary, retain it briefly, and restrict access to authorized personnel | Aggregating surveillance data into employee behavioral profiles used for promotion, termination, or retaliation decisions |
| HR-5.5 | Transparency and consent | Fully disclose all monitoring practices, tools, and data usage to employees before implementation, and provide opt-out where feasible | Implementing surveillance tools secretly or through ambiguous policy language that employees cannot meaningfully consent to |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this standard should:

1. **Establish a Recruitment Ethics Board** — Convene a cross-functional body responsible for reviewing hiring practices, monitoring technology, and compensation equity against this standard.
2. **Conduct Annual Audits** — Perform yearly audits of AI screening tools, compensation equity, worker classification accuracy, and surveillance scope, with results reported to leadership.
3. **Publish Transparency Reports** — Release annual public reports covering hiring diversity metrics, pay equity findings, data retention practices, and monitoring tool usage.
4. **Implement Grievance Mechanisms** — Provide confidential channels for candidates and employees to report ethical violations without fear of retaliation.
5. **Train Hiring Teams** — Require regular training on bias mitigation, data privacy, fair compensation, proper classification, and responsible monitoring for all personnel involved in recruitment.
6. **Review Vendor Contracts** — Ensure third-party recruitment platforms, background check providers, and monitoring tool vendors comply with this standard's requirements.
