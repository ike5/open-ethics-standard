# Beauty & Cosmetics — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Beauty & Cosmetics (Brands, Manufacturers, Retailers)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to beauty and cosmetics brands, manufacturers, and retailers. Personal care products are applied directly to the body, and unethical practices in this domain expose consumers to health risks, exploit insecurities, and generate significant environmental harm through opaque ingredient lists and deceptive marketing.

---

## Category 1: Ingredient Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BC-1.1 | **Toxic Chemical Concealment**   | Using known or suspected harmful ingredients (parabens, phthalates, formaldehyde releasers) while obscuring them under unfamiliar chemical names or omitting them from marketing | Consumer health endangerment; deception |
| BC-1.2 | **Fragrance Loophole Abuse**     | Hiding dozens of unlisted chemicals behind the single label "fragrance" or "parfum," preventing consumers from identifying allergens, endocrine disruptors, or sensitizers | Ingredient opacity; allergy risk     |
| BC-1.3 | **Greenwashing Natural Claims**   | Marketing products as "clean," "natural," or "non-toxic" without standardized definitions, while containing synthetic or harmful ingredients | Misleading health claims; false natural positioning |
| BC-1.4 | **Undisclosed Nano-Ingredients** | Using nanoparticles without disclosure or safety assessment, exploiting regulatory gaps that treat nano-forms identically to bulk materials | Unknown risk; labeling evasion       |

## Category 2: Marketing & Claims

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BC-2.1 | **Photoshopped Results Without Disclosure** | Displaying digitally altered before-and-after images or retouched model photography to exaggerate product effectiveness | Deceptive advertising               |
| BC-2.2 | **Unrealistic Beauty Standards**  | Promoting unattainable appearance ideals through filters, editing, and selective casting while claiming the product achieves these results | Psychological harm; false promises   |
| BC-2.3 | **Cultural Appropriation in Branding** | Co-opting traditional beauty practices, indigenous ingredients, or cultural aesthetics without attribution, compensation, or community benefit | Cultural exploitation; erasure      |
| BC-2.4 | **Anti-Aging Fear Marketing**      | Positioning normal aging as a problem to be solved, using fear-based messaging to drive product sales | Exploiting insecurity; ageism        |

## Category 3: Pricing & Packaging

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BC-3.1 | **Shrinkflation in Cosmetics**    | Reducing product volume or weight while maintaining or increasing price, relying on opaque packaging to conceal the change | Hidden price inflation               |
| BC-3.2 | **Refill Program Lock-In**        | Offering sustainability-branded refill systems that only accept proprietary cartridges, preventing use of lower-cost alternatives under the guise of eco-consciousness | Anti-competitive greenwashing        |
| BC-3.3 | **Sample-to-Subscription Traps**  | Converting free trial or sample offers into recurring subscriptions with difficult cancellation processes and opaque auto-renewal terms | Predatory subscription practices     |
| BC-3.4 | **Luxury Ingredient Markup**       | Charging extreme markups for trace amounts of trending ingredients (hyaluronic acid, retinol) that cost fractions of the retail price to formulate | Price exploitation; value deception  |

---

## Category 4: Testing & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BC-4.1 | **Animal Testing in Unregulated Markets** | Selling products tested on animals in jurisdictions where it is not prohibited, or sourcing from suppliers who test on animals while claiming cruelty-free status in regulated markets | Regulatory arbitrage; animal cruelty  |
| BC-4.2 | **Patch Test Omission**           | Failing to recommend or provide patch test guidance for products containing known sensitizers, allergens, or active ingredients | Consumer safety negligence           |
| BC-4.3 | **Long-Term Safety Data Gaps**    | Bringing products to market without long-term dermal or systemic safety studies, relying on general ingredient safety rather than finished-formulation testing | Incomplete safety assurance          |
| BC-4.4 | **Contaminant Concealment**       | Failing to disclose batch-level contamination with heavy metals, microbials, or 1,4-dioxane, only revealing issues after regulatory enforcement | Withholding safety information       |

---

## Category 5: Sustainability

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BC-5.1 | **Plastic Overpackaging**         | Using excessive or non-recyclable plastic packaging, multi-material wraps, and outer boxes that dwarf the product size for premium shelf presence | Environmental waste; packaging deception |
| BC-5.2 | **Microplastic Ingredients**      | Formulating with microbeads, glitter, or synthetic polymers that enter waterways despite known ecological damage, simply shifting to unregulated alternatives | Ecosystem contamination              |
| BC-5.3 | **Empty Recycling Promises**      | Marketing collection programs or recyclable claims while knowing the packaging is not recyclable in practice or the collection program processes negligible volumes | Greenwashing; misleading consumers   |
| BC-5.4 | **Palm Oil Deforestation Link**   | Sourcing palm oil or derivatives without verified deforestation-free supply chains while marketing products as sustainable or eco-friendly | Supply chain opacity; habitat destruction |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Disclose all ingredients** — eliminate the fragrance loophole by listing all constituent chemicals; flag known allergens and sensitizers clearly; disclose nano-ingredients.
2. **Market honestly** — label digitally altered images; cease fear-based anti-aging messaging; attribute cultural inspirations and share benefits with source communities.
3. **Price and package transparently** — disclose net weight and volume changes; decouple refill systems from proprietary lock-in; make subscription cancellation straightforward.
4. **Ensure safety accountability** — commit to global cruelty-free standards regardless of jurisdiction; provide patch test guidance on all sensitizing products; publish formulation safety data.
5. **Commit to real sustainability** — set measurable packaging reduction targets; eliminate microplastic ingredients; verify deforestation-free sourcing with independent audits.
