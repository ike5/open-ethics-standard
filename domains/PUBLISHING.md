# Publishing — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Publishing (Books, Academic, News)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to the publishing industry, encompassing trade book publishers, academic journal publishers, and news organizations. Publishing is the primary mechanism by which knowledge, culture, and information reach the public. When ethical standards falter in this domain, the consequences extend beyond individual creators to the integrity of public discourse, the accessibility of knowledge, and the diversity of voices in society.

---

## Category 1: Author Compensation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PB-1.1 | **Work-for-Hire Rights Grabs**    | Classifying book projects as work-for-hire to acquire full copyright ownership from authors, denying them ongoing royalties and creative control over their own work | Creator rights appropriation; ownership stripping |
| PB-1.2 | **Below-Subsistence Advances**    | Offering advances so low that authors cannot survive during the writing period, forcing financial hardship or abandonment of projects | Economic coercion; livelihood undermining |
| PB-1.3 | **Royalty Rate Opacity**           | Using convoluted royalty calculations, varying rates across formats, and opaque escalator thresholds that prevent authors from understanding or predicting their earnings | Financial transparency violation; contractual deception |
| PB-1.4 | **Return Reserve Manipulation**    | Withholding excessive royalty payments against future returns that never materialize, or holding reserves far beyond industry norms without releasing them | Revenue withholding; cash flow exploitation |
| PB-1.5 | **Out-of-Print Suppression**      | Keeping books technically in print through print-on-demand at token quantities to prevent rights reversion, locking works in contractual limbo | Rights reversion obstruction; catalog control |

## Category 2: Academic Publishing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PB-2.1 | **Taxpayer-Funded Research Paywall** | Charging exorbitant subscription fees for access to research that was funded by public grants and produced using public resources | Public knowledge enclosure; double-charging |
| PB-2.2 | **Peer Review Free Labor Exploitation** | Relying on unpaid academic peer reviewers while generating billions in subscription revenue from their volunteer contributions | Labor exploitation; value extraction    |
| PB-2.3 | **Journal Price Escalation**       | Implementating annual subscription price increases far above inflation, particularly for bundled "big deal" packages that force institutional libraries to pay for unwanted titles | Price gouging; monopolistic leverage   |
| PB-2.4 | **Editorial Board Replacement for Profit** | Replacing dissenting editorial boards who advocate for open access or fair pricing with compliant boards willing to maintain exploitative business models | Academic freedom suppression; editorial independence violation |
| PB-2.5 | **Article Processing Charge Inflation** | Charging authors thousands of dollars in article processing charges for open-access publication, shifting costs from readers to authors while maintaining high profit margins | Cost shifting; access inequality       |

## Category 3: Market Consolidation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PB-3.1 | **Imprint Opacity Hiding Monopoly** | Operating dozens of imprints under a single corporate parent while presenting them as independent publishers, obscuring the true extent of market concentration | Market transparency violation; monopoly concealment |
| PB-3.2 | **Independent Bookstore Disadvantage** | Negotiating exclusive terms, preferential pricing, and early release windows with large retailers that independent bookstores cannot match | Anti-competitive practice; small business suppression |
| PB-3.3 | **Advance Auction Excluding Diverse Voices** | Using high-advance bidding wars that favor already-established and connected authors, systematically excluding debut and underrepresented voices from major publishing deals | Access inequality; systemic bias      |
| PB-3.4 | **Backlist Suppression**           | Allowing mid-list and diverse backlist titles to go out of print or receive no marketing support while front-list titles receive disproportionate investment | Cultural devaluation; catalog neglect  |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PB-4.1 | **Reader Behavior Tracking via Ebooks** | Collecting granular reading data — page turns, reading speed, highlights, annotations, and completion rates — through ebook platforms without meaningful disclosure or consent | Privacy violation; surveillance without consent |
| PB-4.2 | **Library Lending Restriction**    | Imposing restrictive licensing terms on ebook lending that limit library checkouts, impose per-use fees, or expire licenses after arbitrary periods | Public access restriction; library undermining |
| PB-4.3 | **DRM Preventing Accessibility**   | Applying digital rights management that prevents screen readers, text-to-speech, and other accessibility tools from functioning, excluding disabled readers | Disability exclusion; access barrier  |
| PB-4.4 | **Purchase Data Sale**             | Selling aggregated reader purchase and browsing data to advertisers, data brokers, or third parties without explicit, informed consent | Data exploitation; privacy violation  |

## Category 5: Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PB-5.1 | **Ebook Pricing Above Physical**  | Setting ebook prices at or above the cost of physical books despite dramatically lower production and distribution costs | Price gouging; digital access barrier |
| PB-5.2 | **Geographic Restriction**         | Implementing territorial rights and geo-blocking that prevent readers in certain regions from purchasing legally available books | Geographic discrimination; access denial |
| PB-5.3 | **Public Domain Misappropriation** | Repackaging public domain works with minor additions and claiming new copyright protection to restrict access to works that belong to the public | Public domain enclosure; false claims |
| PB-5.4 | **Textbook Price Gouging**         | Releasing unnecessary new editions to kill the used book market, bundling access codes that expire, and charging prices far exceeding production costs for required educational materials | Educational exploitation; cost manipulation |
| PB-5.5 | **Print Disability Access Denial** | Failing to provide accessible formats simultaneously with standard publication, leaving disabled readers waiting months or years for equivalent access | Disability exclusion; equal access violation |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Compensate authors fairly** — limit work-for-hire classification to genuinely commissioned works; provide advances that reflect realistic living costs; use transparent royalty calculations; release return reserves promptly; honor contractual rights reversion clauses.
2. **Reform academic publishing** — support open access for taxpayer-funded research; compensate peer reviewers or reduce APCs proportionally; cap annual subscription increases; respect editorial board independence.
3. **Reduce market consolidation harm** — disclose corporate ownership of all imprints; offer equivalent terms to independent retailers; invest in debut and underrepresented author development; actively market backlist titles.
4. **Protect reader data** — obtain explicit consent before collecting reading behavior data; negotiate fair library lending terms; remove DRM that blocks accessibility tools; prohibit sale of reader data without informed consent.
5. **Expand access** — price ebooks below physical editions; minimize geographic restrictions; respect public domain boundaries; adopt simultaneous accessible format release; cap textbook pricing relative to production costs.
