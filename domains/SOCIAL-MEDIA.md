# Social Media — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Social Media  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to social media platforms, content networks, and algorithm-driven recommendation systems. Social media companies operate at unprecedented scale, shaping public discourse, mental health, and democratic processes. The asymmetry between platform knowledge and user understanding demands heightened ethical accountability.

---

## Category 1: Algorithmic Manipulation

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SM-1.1 | **Engagement Addiction Design**  | Engineering feeds, notifications, and rewards to maximize time-on-platform through variable reinforcement schedules | Exploiting psychological vulnerability for profit |
| SM-1.2 | **Outrage Amplification**        | Designing algorithms that prioritize anger-inducing or divisive content because it drives higher engagement | Fueling social fragmentation for engagement metrics |
| SM-1.3 | **Political Polarization Optimization** | Optimizing content delivery to reinforce existing beliefs and suppress cross-cutting viewpoints | Undermining democratic discourse; radicalizing users |
| SM-1.4 | **Infinite Scroll Exploitation** | Removing natural stopping cues—pagination, episode boundaries—to prevent users from disengaging | Intentionally impairing user self-regulation |
| SM-1.5 | **Downranking by Emotion**        | Algorithmically demoting content that does not provoke strong emotional responses, regardless of informational quality | Marginalizing calm, accurate information |

## Category 2: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SM-2.1 | **Shadow Profiling of Non-Users** | Building behavioral and demographic profiles of people who have never created accounts, using pixels, contact uploads, or third-party data | Surveillance of non-consenting individuals |
| SM-2.2 | **Data Sale to Brokers**          | Selling user data—interests, connections, locations—to data brokers, advertisers, or analytics firms without clear informed consent | Monetizing personal information without meaningful consent |
| SM-2.3 | **Location History Exploitation** | Using precise location data for ad targeting, trend analysis, or law enforcement sharing beyond what users reasonably expect | Exceeding scope of consent for sensitive data |
| SM-2.4 | **Cross-Platform Tracking**      | Following users across apps, websites, and devices through shared login, SDKs, or fingerprinting to build unified profiles | Circumventing platform-specific privacy choices |
| SM-2.5 | **Retention Beyond Purpose**     | Storing data indefinitely or beyond the functional need for which it was collected | Data hoarding beyond justified use |
| SM-2.6 | **Emotional Inference**          | Inferring mental health states—depression, anxiety, grief—from posting patterns and targeting ads accordingly | Weaponizing emotional vulnerability |

## Category 3: Content Moderation

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SM-3.1 | **Inconsistent Enforcement**    | Applying community standards unevenly across languages, regions, or account types—especially protecting high-revenue accounts | Undermining rule of law on the platform |
| SM-3.2 | **Government Censorship Compliance** | Removing or suppressing content at government request without transparency reporting or legal challenge | Enabling authoritarian censorship |
| SM-3.3 | **Hate Speech Tolerance for Engagement** | Allowing hate speech, harassment, or incitement to remain when it generates significant engagement or ad revenue | Prioritizing revenue over user safety |
| SM-3.4 | **Moderator Well-Being Neglect** | Exposing content moderators to graphic, violent, or traumatic material without adequate mental health support | Endangering worker psychological health |
| SM-3.5 | **Appeal Process Opacity**       | Providing no meaningful mechanism to challenge content removals, account suspensions, or demonetization decisions | Denying due process on platform-governed spaces |

## Category 4: Youth Safety

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SM-4.1 | **Addictive Design for Minors**  | Deploying engagement-maximizing features—streaks, likes, notifications—specifically targeted at or disproportionately affecting users under 18 | Exploiting developmental vulnerability |
| SM-4.2 | **Inadequate Age Verification**  | Allowing self-reported age with no verification, enabling minors to access age-restricted features or content | Facilitating regulatory circumvention |
| SM-4.3 | **Bullying Tool Gaps**           | Providing insufficient reporting, blocking, or moderation tools for harassment, body-shaming, or coordinated pile-ons | Failing to protect users from systematic abuse |
| SM-4.4 | **Predator Contact Enablement**   | Designing discovery and messaging features that facilitate unsolicited contact between adults and minors | Enabling grooming and exploitation |
| SM-4.5 | **Self-Harm Content Amplification** | Algorithmically recommending or clustering self-harm, eating disorder, or suicide content to vulnerable users | Amplifying dangerous content to at-risk populations |

## Category 5: Monetization

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SM-5.1 | **Creator Revenue Share Opacity** | Opaque or unilaterally changeable revenue-sharing formulas that leave creators unable to predict or verify earnings | Exploiting creator dependency |
| SM-5.2 | **Forced Advertising Participation** | Requiring personal ad targeting as a non-negotiable condition of platform use, even in paid tiers | Denying genuine opt-out from surveillance advertising |
| SM-5.3 | **Pay-for-Visibility**           | Reducing organic reach to force creators and businesses into paid promotion or boosted posts | Rent-seeking on community-built audiences |
| SM-5.4 | **Virtual Economy Exploitation**  | Designing in-app currencies, loot boxes, or tipping mechanisms that obscure real costs and encourage overspending | Enabling unregulated microtransactions |
| SM-5.5 | **Ad Metrics Fraud**            | Inflating impressions, clicks, or engagement metrics through bot traffic, accidental clicks, or misattributed conversions | Billing advertisers for non-genuine engagement |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish algorithmic impact assessments** — disclose ranking signals, engagement optimization objectives, and known harms; commission independent audits annually.
2. **Implement data minimization** — delete data when its collection purpose has ended; prohibit shadow profiling; offer meaningful deletion for all user and non-user data.
3. **Enforce standards consistently** — publish enforcement reports by language, region, and account tier; apply rules uniformly regardless of commercial value.
4. **Protect minors by design** — default accounts under 18 to private; disable engagement-maximizing notifications for minors; require age assurance for age-restricted features.
5. **Disclose monetization terms** — publish creator revenue-share formulas; provide 90-day notice before changes; offer non-targeted ad options at no extra cost.
6. **Support moderator welfare** — limit shifts to 4 hours for traumatic content; provide ongoing counseling; publish moderator well-being metrics.
