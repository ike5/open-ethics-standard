# Event Ticketing — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Event Ticketing (Primary, Secondary, Resale Platforms)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to primary ticketing platforms, secondary marketplaces, and resale services for live events. Event ticketing is a gatekeeper to shared cultural experiences; unethical practices—fee stacking, scalping tolerance, and access manipulation—transform public events into pay-to-play markets that price out fans and concentrate profits among intermediaries.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ET-1.1 | **Dynamic Pricing Abuse on Cultural Events** | Applying surge pricing algorithms to concert, theater, and sporting event tickets that far exceed demand elasticity, treating cultural events as pure commodities | Price gouging; cultural access denial  |
| ET-1.2 | **Fee Stacking Beyond Face Value** | Adding service fees, facility charges, processing fees, and delivery fees that cumulatively exceed the ticket's face value | Hidden cost; fee exploitation         |
| ET-1.3 | **Platinum Ticket Insider Hoarding** | Reserving premium ticket inventory for insiders, affiliates, or corporate partners before public on-sale, restricting genuine fan access | Insider access manipulation           |
| ET-1.4 | **Mandatory Bundle Pricing**       | Requiring purchase of unwanted additional tickets, merchandise, or experiences to access high-demand events | Forced purchase; cost inflation       |
| ET-1.5 | **Charity Markup Capture**         | Marketing tickets with charity add-ons while directing the majority of inflated prices to platform and promoter fees rather than the stated cause | Misrepresenting charitable intent     |

## Category 2: Access & Fairness

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ET-2.1 | **Bot-Enabled Scalping Tolerance** | Failing to implement effective bot detection and prevention, allowing automated bulk purchases that redirect tickets to secondary markets at markup | Enabling market manipulation          |
| ET-2.2 | **Venue Presale Code Manipulation** | Distributing presale codes through sponsor channels, credit card partnerships, or email lists that advantage privileged groups over general fans | Unequal access; systemic favoritism   |
| ET-2.3 | **ADA Seat Withholding**          | Under-providing accessible seating, releasing ADA seats to general inventory, or requiring phone-based verification that delays access | Disability discrimination             |
| ET-2.4 | **Sold-Out Misrepresentation**     | Declaring events "sold out" while holding back significant inventory for resale at premium prices | Manufactured scarcity                 |
| ET-2.5 | **Geographic Access Barriers**    | Restricting ticket sales to specific regions or requiring in-person purchase for events with national or global fan bases | Arbitrary access restriction          |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ET-3.1 | **Ticket Buyer Data Sale**         | Selling purchaser data—names, demographics, event preferences, purchase histories—to advertisers, data brokers, or affiliated businesses | Privacy violation; data trafficking    |
| ET-3.2 | **Venue Entry Biometric Collection** | Requiring facial recognition, fingerprint, or other biometric scans for venue entry without providing alternative access or clear data retention policies | Biometric surveillance; consent coercion |
| ET-3.3 | **Dynamic Pricing Data Hoarding**  | Withholding the algorithms, demand data, and pricing models used to set ticket prices from regulators, artists, and consumers | Algorithmic transparency failure       |
| ET-3.4 | **Purchase History Profiling**    | Building behavioral profiles from ticket purchase history to target premium pricing, restrict access, or manipulate future offerings | Discriminatory profiling              |

## Category 4: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ET-4.1 | **Hold-Back Disclosure Failure**   | Failing to disclose the percentage of tickets held back from public sale for insiders, sponsors, or secondary market distribution | Inventory opacity                    |
| ET-4.2 | **Transfer Restriction Lock-In**   | Prohibiting or restricting ticket transfers to force use of the primary platform's resale system, limiting consumer choice | Anti-competitive lock-in              |
| ET-4.3 | **Resale Price Floor Manipulation** | Setting minimum resale prices or requiring resale at face value while simultaneously allowing dynamic primary pricing | Artificial price control             |
| ET-4.4 | **Total Cost Concealment**         | Withdrawing full pricing—including all fees—until late in the purchase flow, preventing price comparison | Cost transparency failure              |
| ET-4.5 | **Artist Revenue Disclosure Gap**  | Failing to disclose what portion of ticket revenue reaches the artist versus intermediary fees and platform charges | Revenue opacity; artist exploitation    |

## Category 5: Secondary Market

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ET-5.1 | **Platform-Owned Resale at Markup** | Operating or owning the secondary marketplace where primary tickets are resold at premium, capturing both primary and secondary revenue | Conflicts of interest; market manipulation |
| ET-5.2 | **Transfer Fee Exploitation**       | Charging substantial fees to transfer tickets between individuals, profiting from every change of hands | Extracting rent on peer transfers     |
| ET-5.3 | **Voiding Legitimate Resale Tickets** | Canceling tickets detected on secondary markets even when resold at or below face value, penalizing fans who cannot attend | Punitive anti-transfer policy          |
| ET-5.4 | **Speculative Listing**            | Allowing sellers to list tickets they do not yet possess, creating phantom inventory that inflates perceived demand | Fraudulent market signaling           |
| ET-5.5 | **Resale Bot Partnerships**         | Maintaining financial relationships with ticket brokers who use automated tools to acquire inventory, profiting from bot scalping indirectly | Enabling scalping ecosystem           |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Enforce total price transparency** — display all-in pricing (face value plus all mandatory fees) from the first step of the purchase flow; eliminate last-step fee surprises.
2. **Combat scalping effectively** — implement bot detection, enforce purchase limits, and require verified fan registration for high-demand events.
3. **Guarantee accessible seating** — provide ADA-compliant seating inventory in proportion to demand; offer alternative purchasing channels for accessibility needs.
4. **Disclose inventory allocation** — publish hold-back percentages for each event, including presale, insider, and secondary allocations.
5. **Separate primary and secondary markets** — prohibit platforms from operating or profiting from secondary resale of tickets they originally sold; cap transfer fees at actual processing cost.
6. **Protect buyer data** — prohibit sale of ticket purchaser data; require opt-in consent for any biometric collection with mandatory deletion after event entry.
