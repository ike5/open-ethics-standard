# Space & Aerospace Industry — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Space & Aerospace Industry (Launch Providers, Satellite Operators, Space Manufacturing, Defense Aerospace)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to the space and aerospace industry. As commercial activity in space accelerates, unethical practices in this domain threaten the long-term usability of orbital environments, exploit public investment for private gain, and create risks that fall disproportionately on communities and nations with no voice in orbital governance.

---

## Category 1: Orbital Debris

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AE-1.1 | **Satellite Constellation Debris Risk** | Deploying large satellite constellations without adequate debris mitigation planning, increasing collision probability for all orbital users | Collective orbital resource endangerment |
| AE-1.2 | **End-of-Life Disposal Failure** | Failing to deorbit or relocate defunct satellites within committed timelines, leaving derelict objects as collision hazards | Abandonment of orbital responsibility |
| AE-1.3 | **Collision Avoidance Opacity** | Withholding conjunction data, refusing to coordinate maneuver decisions, or concealing near-miss events from other operators and regulators | Concealing shared risk; coordination failure |
| AE-1.4 | **Megaconstellation Interference With Astronomy** | Deploying reflective satellites in orbits that degrade ground-based and space-based astronomical observations without meaningful mitigation | Scientific commons degradation |
| AE-1.5 | **Post-Mission Abandonment** | Designing missions without end-of-life disposal capability, effectively planning to leave hardware in orbit permanently | Intentional orbital littering |

## Category 2: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AE-2.1 | **Launch Emission Non-Disclosure** | Failing to measure, report, or disclose the atmospheric impact of launch emissions including alumina, soot, and ozone-depleting compounds | Environmental impact concealment |
| AE-2.2 | **Rocket Fuel Contamination** | Using or improperly storing toxic propellants such as hydrazine or perchlorates that contaminate soil and water near launch and test facilities | Toxic environmental release |
| AE-2.3 | **Ground Water Contamination From Testing** | Allowing propellant, solvent, or chemical runoff from static fire tests and launch operations to contaminate local aquifers without remediation | Community environmental harm |
| AE-2.4 | **Carbon Footprint of Frequent Launches** | Operating high-cadence launch schedules while dismissing or downplaying cumulative climate impact of repeated atmospheric injections | Climate impact dismissal; intergenerational harm |
| AE-2.5 | **Upper Atmosphere Alteration** | Ignoring research indicating that repeated reentry deposits of alumina and other particles alter stratospheric chemistry and radiative balance | Atmospheric commons degradation |

## Category 3: Market & Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AE-3.1 | **Spectrum Hoarding Preventing Competition** | Filing for vast spectrum allocations beyond intended use to block competitors from accessing orbital communications spectrum | Anti-competitive resource lock-up |
| AE-3.2 | **Launch Market Monopoly Behavior** | Using vertical integration, exclusive pad access, or predatory pricing to prevent new entrants from accessing launch infrastructure | Market foreclosure; competition suppression |
| AE-3.3 | **Government Subsidy Dependence While Privatizing Profits** | Accepting public development funding, infrastructure subsidies, or guaranteed contracts while structuring operations to ensure private profit without public return | Public wealth extraction; privatized gain, socialized risk |
| AE-3.4 | **Exclusion of Developing Nations From Orbit Allocation** | Opposing equitable orbital slot and spectrum allocation frameworks that would provide access to nations without established space programs | Orbital commons enclosure; global inequality reinforcement |

## Category 4: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AE-4.1 | **Crew Safety Corner-Cutting** | Pressuring flight readiness reviews to proceed despite unresolved anomalies, accepting elevated risk thresholds for crewed missions to meet schedule | Endangering human life for schedule compliance |
| AE-4.2 | **Rapid Iteration at Risk** | Using test-flight failure as a development methodology for crewed vehicles without adequately separating uncrewed test risk from human spaceflight risk | Treating crewed vehicles as expendable test platforms |
| AE-4.3 | **Reentry Risk to Populated Areas** | Planning uncontrolled or marginally controlled reentry trajectories that pose statistically significant risk to populated regions without adequate notification systems | Exposing ground populations to unconsented risk |
| AE-4.4 | **Workplace Safety in Manufacturing** | Maintaining inadequate safety protocols in rocket and satellite manufacturing facilities, leading to preventable industrial injuries and exposures | Worker endangerment; negligence |

## Category 5: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AE-5.1 | **Satellite Imagery Sale to Authoritarian Regimes** | Selling high-resolution earth observation imagery and analytics to governments known to use such data for surveillance, targeting, or repression of civilian populations | Enabling human rights violations |
| AE-5.2 | **Surveillance Capability Marketing** | Advertising satellite or airborne surveillance capabilities to law enforcement and intelligence agencies without transparency about civil liberties implications | Surveillance infrastructure enablement |
| AE-5.3 | **Earth Observation Data Hoarding** | Collecting commercially valuable climate, agricultural, or environmental data from public-domain orbits while restricting public and scientific access | Enclosing observational commons; hindering climate science |
| AE-5.4 | **GPS Dependency Exploitation** | Leveraging society's critical dependence on positioning, navigation, and timing services to extract preferential terms, avoid interoperability, or resist redundancy requirements | Critical infrastructure hostage-taking |
| AE-5.5 | **Dual-Use Technology Transfer Concealment** | Marketing space technology as civilian while designing capabilities that serve military or surveillance applications without disclosure | End-use deception; arms control circumvention |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Manage orbital responsibility** — commit to deorbiting defunct assets within five years of end-of-life; share conjunction data openly; fund debris remediation research proportional to constellation size.
2. **Account for environmental impact** — measure and publish launch emission data; remediate contaminated test sites; incorporate atmospheric impact into mission planning for high-cadence operations.
3. **Ensure equitable access** — return spectrum allocations not in active use; support orbital and spectrum allocation frameworks that reserve capacity for developing nations; disclose public subsidy reliance and return structures.
4. **Prioritize safety** — isolate crewed flight risk from test programs; provide advance notice and trajectory data for reentry over populated areas; maintain independently audited manufacturing safety programs.
5. **Govern data ethically** — refuse imagery contracts with entities under sanctions or documented human rights abusers; publish civil liberties impact assessments for surveillance-capable products; provide scientific access to earth observation data.
