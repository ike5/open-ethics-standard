# Veterinary Medicine — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Veterinary Medicine & Animal Health  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to veterinary clinics, animal hospitals, pet insurance providers, and pharmaceutical companies serving animal health. Veterinary care involves emotionally vulnerable clients making urgent decisions for dependent animals; unethical practices exploit this vulnerability for financial gain.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Cost Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| VE-1.1 | **Procedure Cost Opacity**       | Failing to provide written cost estimates before non-emergency procedures | Withholding information for informed consent |
| VE-1.2 | **Prescription Markups**          | Charging significantly above market rate for medications dispensed in-clinic when pharmacies offer lower prices | Exploitation of convenience and urgency |
| VE-1.3 | **Emergency Care Cost Exploitation** | Leveraging the urgency of after-hours emergencies to charge premium rates without upfront disclosure | Financial coercion in crisis          |
| VE-1.4 | **Unbundling Surprise**          | Performing bundled procedures and charging separately without prior agreement | Hidden cost practice                  |
| VE-1.5 | **Payment Plan Predatory Terms** | Offering high-interest payment plans for costly treatments without disclosing total interest paid | Predatory lending to vulnerable clients |

## Category 2: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| VE-2.1 | **Pet Health Data Sale**          | Selling animal health records and treatment data to pharmaceutical companies or data brokers | Violating client confidentiality      |
| VE-2.2 | **Pharmacy Data Sharing**         | Sharing prescription data with manufacturers for marketing purposes without client consent | Undisclosed data exploitation         |
| VE-2.3 | **Client Data Monetization**      | Selling client contact information and pet demographics to pet product marketers | Privacy violation; spam facilitation  |
| VE-2.4 | **Insurance Data Asymmetry**      | Sharing claims data with insurers that creates coverage disadvantages for clients | Undermining client insurance position |

## Category 3: Quality of Care

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| VE-3.1 | **Unnecessary Procedure Upselling** | Recommending procedures or diagnostics not clinically indicated to increase revenue | Medical fraud; animal stress for profit |
| VE-3.2 | **Specialist Referral Barriers**  | Discouraging referrals to specialists to keep revenue in-house, even when specialist care is warranted | Prioritizing revenue over patient welfare |
| VE-3.3 | **Equipment Quality Disparity**   | Using outdated diagnostic equipment in lower-income service areas while investing in premium tech for affluent clientele | Tiered care based on ability to pay    |
| VE-3.4 | **Antibiotic Overprescription**   | Prescribing antibiotics without proper diagnostics, contributing to resistance | Public health endangerment            |

## Category 4: Access & Equity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| VE-4.1 | **Insurance Claim Denial Patterns** | Systematically denying common claims through ambiguous policy language | Insurance bad faith                   |
| VE-4.2 | **Payment Plan Unavailability**   | Refusing to offer payment arrangements for low-income clients, leading to economic euthanasia | Financial barrier to animal welfare   |
| VE-4.3 | **Rural Vet Desert Creation**     | Concentrating practices in affluent urban areas while rural communities lose access | Geographic inequity in care           |
| VE-4.4 | **Breed-Specific Insurance Exclusion** | Denying coverage for common conditions in specific breeds while marketing policies as comprehensive | Discriminatory coverage practices      |

## Category 5: Drug & Pharmaceutical Practices

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| VE-5.1 | **Compounding Pharmacy Risks**   | Using unregulated compounding pharmacies for critical medications without disclosing quality risks | Patient safety risk                   |
| VE-5.2 | **Expired Medication Dispensing** | Dispensing medications near or past expiration dates, particularly in shelter or low-cost settings | Safety risk; informed consent failure |
| VE-5.3 | **Off-Label Use Without Disclosure** | Prescribing human medications off-label without explaining unapproved status and risks | Withholding material treatment information |
| VE-5.4 | **Vaccine Schedule Inflation**    | Recommending more frequent vaccinations than evidence-based guidelines require | Unnecessary treatment for revenue     |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish price lists** — provide written cost estimates for the top 30 most common procedures; guarantee estimates within 15% for non-emergency care.
2. **Implement data minimization** — collect only necessary clinical data; prohibit sale of animal health or client data; obtain explicit consent for any research use.
3. **Adopt referral transparency** — disclose financial interest in keeping cases in-house; provide specialist referrals when clinically indicated regardless of revenue impact.
4. **Offer payment accessibility** — provide zero-interest or low-interest payment plans for procedures exceeding $500; publish financial assistance criteria.
5. **Align vaccination protocols with evidence** — adopt AAHA/AAFP guidelines; disclose when recommendations exceed evidence-based schedules.
