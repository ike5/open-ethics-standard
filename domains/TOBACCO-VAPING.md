# Tobacco & Vaping — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Tobacco & Vaping Products  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to tobacco and vaping product manufacturers, distributors, and retailers. These products cause well-documented harm and carry a high risk of addiction; unethical practices in this domain recruit new users, sustain addiction, and obstruct public health efforts.

---

## Category 1: Marketing & Recruitment

| Ref   | Practice                                | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TO-1.1 | **Youth-Appealing Flavors**           | Developing sweet, candy, and dessert flavors that attract underage users | Targeting minors for addiction       |
| TO-1.2 | **Social Media Influencer Targeting**  | Deploying paid influencers to promote vaping products without disclosure on platforms popular with youth | Undisclosed advertising to minors     |
| TO-1.3 | **Health Risk Minimization**           | Marketing products as "safer alternatives" without contextualizing absolute risk | Misleading risk communication        |
| TO-1.4 | **Lifestyle Branding**                 | Associating vaping with fitness, outdoor recreation, and wellness culture | Rebranding addiction as self-care    |
| TO-1.5 | **Point-of-Sale Placement**           | Placing products at eye level of children near checkout in convenience stores | Impulse recruitment of minors        |

## Category 2: Product Design & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                                | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TO-2.1 | **Nicotine Optimization for Addiction** | Engineering e-liquid formulations for rapid nicotine absorption and maximum addictiveness | Intentional addiction design         |
| TO-2.2 | **Child-Resistant Packaging Failure**  | Producing refill containers and devices that can be easily opened by children | Endangering child safety              |
| TO-2.3 | **Emission Concealment**               | Withholding data on harmful chemicals produced during heating and vaporization | Withholding health-critical information |
| TO-2.4 | **Device Safety Neglect**              | Marketing devices with known battery explosion or overheating risks without recall | Known product danger concealment      |
| TO-2.5 | **Synthetic Nicotine Exploitation**    | Using synthetic nicotine to evade FDA regulation of tobacco-derived nicotine products | Regulatory evasion                    |

## Category 3: Pricing & Access

| Ref   | Practice                                | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TO-3.1 | **Tax Avoidance via Reclassification** | Reformulating or reclassifying products to fall into lower-tax categories | Undermining public health taxation    |
| TO-3.2 | **Discount Targeting in Low-Income Areas** | Concentrating promotions and price discounts in low-income and minority neighborhoods | Exploiting vulnerable populations    |
| TO-3.3 | **Loyalty Programs for Addiction**     | Offering rewards, points, or discounts for repeat purchases of addictive products | Incentivizing continued use           |
| TO-3.4 | **Online Age Verification Gaps**      | Using weak or bypassable age verification for online sales         | Facilitating underage access          |

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                                | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TO-4.1 | **Purchase Data Sale**                 | Selling or sharing consumer purchase data with third parties including marketers | Privacy violation; data exploitation  |
| TO-4.2 | **Connected Device Data Harvesting**   | Collecting usage frequency, puff duration, and location data from smart vaping devices without meaningful consent | Surveillance of addicted users        |
| TO-4.3 | **Age Verification Data Retention**   | Retaining identity documents and biometric data submitted for age verification beyond the verification event | Unnecessary data hoarding             |
| TO-4.4 | **Cessation Data Weaponization**       | Using data indicating a user is attempting to quit to target them with re-engagement offers | Sabotaging health decisions           |

## Category 5: Harm Reduction Claims

| Ref   | Practice                                | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TO-5.1 | **Reduced Risk Without FDA Authorization** | Marketing products as reduced-risk or modified-risk without FDA authorization | Unauthorized health claims            |
| TO-5.2 | **Cessation Claims Without Evidence**  | Implying vaping helps quit smoking without clinical evidence or FDA approval | Deceptive health marketing            |
| TO-5.3 | **Relative Risk Framing**              | Emphasizing relative risk compared to combustible cigarettes while encouraging initiation among non-smokers | Encouraging new addiction via comparison |
| TO-5.4 | **Independent Research Suppression**   | Funding research under conditions that prevent publication of unfavorable results | Scientific integrity violation        |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Cease youth-oriented marketing** — eliminate sweet/dessert flavors, cartoon imagery, and social media influencer campaigns; restrict advertising to adult-only channels.
2. **Publish full emission data** — disclose all chemicals produced during product use, verified by independent laboratories, updated with each product reformulation.
3. **Adopt robust age verification** — implement multi-factor age verification for all sales channels; delete verification data within 30 days of confirmation.
4. **Restrict loyalty programs** — prohibit rewards, points, or discounts tied to purchase volume of addictive products.
5. **Submit to FDA review** — obtain FDA authorization before making any reduced-risk or cessation claims; fund independent research with no publication restrictions.
