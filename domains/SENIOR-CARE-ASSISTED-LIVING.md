# Senior Care & Assisted Living — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Senior Care & Assisted Living (Nursing Homes, Assisted Living, Memory Care, In-Home Senior Services)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to senior care and assisted living facilities. Elderly residents are among the most vulnerable populations, and unethical practices in this domain exploit age-related dependencies, conceal neglect, and strip dignity from individuals who have entrusted their later years to institutional care.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SN-1.1 | **Facility Fee Stacking**                     | Adding layers of mandatory fees (activity, wellness, amenity) beyond the base rate without clear upfront disclosure | Concealing the true cost of residency until after commitment |
| SN-1.2 | **Medicaid Bed Hold Charges**                 | Billing residents for holding their bed during hospital stays or temporary absences beyond reasonable administrative costs | Profiting from residents' medical emergencies and recovery periods |
| SN-1.3 | **Level-of-Care Upselling**                   | Reclassifying residents to higher care levels without corresponding increases in services provided | Charging more for care that is not actually delivered |
| SN-1.4 | **Rate Escalation Without Notice**            | Increasing monthly fees without adequate advance notice or transparent justification | Preventing residents and families from making informed financial decisions |
| SN-1.5 | **Move-Out Cost Penalties**                   | Imposing excessive fees or forfeited deposits when residents transition to higher care or pass away | Exploiting vulnerable transitions for financial gain |

## Category 2: Quality

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SN-2.1 | **Staffing Ratio Violations**                 | Operating below required or advertised caregiver-to-resident ratios to reduce labor costs | Compromising resident safety and wellbeing for financial savings |
| SN-2.2 | **Neglect Concealment**                       | Failing to disclose or actively concealing incidents of resident neglect, malnutrition, or dehydration | Endangering residents by hiding systemic care failures |
| SN-2.3 | **Medication Error Underreporting**           | Not reporting or minimizing medication administration errors to families and regulators | Withholding critical health information that could prevent future harm |
| SN-2.4 | **Unlicensed Staff Performing Care**          | Allowing unlicensed personnel to perform medical or personal care tasks requiring certification | Placing residents in the hands of unqualified caregivers |
| SN-2.5 | **Inadequate Activity Programming**          | Advertising robust enrichment programs while providing minimal or token activities | Misrepresenting quality of life to prospective residents and families |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SN-3.1 | **Health Data Sale**                          | Selling or sharing resident health records, medication histories, or diagnostic information with third parties without consent | Monetizing deeply sensitive medical information of a vulnerable population |
| SN-3.2 | **Family Access Monitoring**                  | Tracking, logging, or restricting family members' visit patterns and communications without transparency | Creating surveillance environments that isolate residents from advocates |
| SN-3.3 | **Cognitive Assessment Data Misuse**          | Using cognitive evaluation results for marketing, insurance cross-selling, or level-of-care justification beyond care planning | Exploiting diagnostic data for financial or strategic advantage |
| SN-3.4 | **Wearable Data Collection Without Consent**   | Deploying health monitoring wearables that collect continuous biometric data without clear resident consent | Gathering sensitive health data under the guise of safety |

## Category 4: Rights

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SN-4.1 | **Involuntary Discharge**                     | Evicting residents without adequate notice, documented cause, or safe transition planning | Forcibly displacing vulnerable individuals without ensuring continuity of care |
| SN-4.2 | **Activity Restriction as Punishment**        | Withholding activities, social engagement, or privileges as a behavioral management tool | Using isolation and deprivation as coercion against residents |
| SN-4.3 | **Guardianship Overreach**                    | Encouraging or facilitating guardianship arrangements that strip residents of decision-making autonomy beyond necessity | Removing civil rights under the pretext of care |
| SN-4.4 | **Visitor Restriction Without Justification** | Limiting family, friend, or advocate access without documented safety or health reasons | Isolating residents from their support networks |
| SN-4.5 | **Communication Interception**                | Screening, monitoring, or restricting resident mail, phone calls, or digital communications | Violating residents' fundamental right to private communication |

---

## Category 5: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SN-5.1 | **Fall Prevention Negligence**                | Failing to implement assessed fall-prevention measures (grab bars, mats, monitoring) despite known risk | Ignoring preventable injury risks to reduce costs |
| SN-5.2 | **Wandering Incident Concealment**            | Not reporting elopement or wandering incidents that could indicate systemic supervision failures | Hiding evidence of dangerous supervision gaps |
| SN-5.3 | **Abuse Reporting Obstruction**               | Discouraging, delaying, or failing to facilitate the reporting of suspected abuse by staff, residents, or visitors | Enabling ongoing abuse by blocking accountability pathways |
| SN-5.4 | **Emergency Preparedness Inadequacy**         | Maintaining insufficient evacuation plans, emergency supplies, or staff training for natural disasters or medical emergencies | Placing residents at catastrophic risk during emergencies |
| SN-5.5 | **Restraint Misuse**                          | Using physical or chemical restraints beyond clinical necessity or without proper authorization | Violating resident dignity and safety through unlawful restraint |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Ensure pricing integrity** — disclose all fees at admission, eliminate unjust bed-hold charges, and base care-level charges on verified service delivery.
2. **Maintain care quality** — meet or exceed staffing ratios, promptly report neglect and medication errors, and employ only qualified caregivers for clinical tasks.
3. **Protect resident data** — prohibit the sale of health or cognitive data, secure monitoring systems, and obtain informed consent for any wearable or biometric tracking.
4. **Uphold resident rights** — implement transparent discharge policies, prohibit punitive restrictions, respect autonomy in guardianship, and guarantee open communication access.
5. **Prioritize safety** — implement fall prevention plans, report all wandering incidents, facilitate abuse reporting, and restrict restraint use to clinically justified cases.
