# Private Equity & Venture Capital — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Private Equity & Venture Capital Industry  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to private equity firms, venture capital funds, portfolio company operators, and associated financial advisors. Private equity and venture capital wield concentrated financial power over portfolio companies, their workers, and the communities those companies serve. The opaque, long-cycle nature of fund structures creates opportunities for value extraction that shifts costs to workers, communities, and limited partners while shielding general partners from accountability. Ethical violations in this domain amplify economic inequality and erode the social contract underlying investment markets.

---

## Category 1: Portfolio Company Exploitation

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PV-1.1 | **Dividend Recapitalization**    | Loading portfolio companies with debt to fund special dividends to the fund, leaving the company financially weakened | Extracting value at the expense of enterprise stability |
| PV-1.2 | **Layoffs to Service Debt**      | Cutting workforce primarily to meet debt service obligations imposed by the acquisition structure | Prioritizing debt obligations over worker livelihoods |
| PV-1.3 | **Asset Stripping Before Exit**  | Selling off valuable divisions, real estate, or intellectual property from portfolio companies prior to sale or wind-down | Dismantling productive capacity for short-term gain |
| PV-1.4 | **Pension Fund Underfunding**    | Reducing or suspending employer pension contributions to improve cash flow metrics before exit | Transferring retirement obligations to workers and government backstops |
| PV-1.5 | **Management Fee Loading**       | Charging excessive management, consulting, or monitoring fees to portfolio companies beyond reasonable service value | Self-dealing that drains portfolio company resources |

## Category 2: Market Impact

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PV-2.1 | **Consolidation Reducing Competition** | Acquiring and merging competitors within an industry roll-up to create local or regional monopolies | Eliminating market competition to extract monopoly rents |
| PV-2.2 | **Predatory Roll-Up Strategies**  | Systematically acquiring small competitors at below-fair-value prices using the threat of market dominance | Coercing acquisition terms through market power |
| PV-2.3 | **Community Impact After Acquisition** | Closing local facilities, outsourcing operations, and eliminating community-anchor employers post-acquisition | Disregarding community dependence on acquired enterprises |
| PV-2.4 | **Strategic Bankruptcy**          | Using Chapter 11 proceedings to shed labor contracts, pension obligations, and environmental liabilities while preserving fund returns | Weaponizing bankruptcy to escape social obligations |
| PV-2.5 | **Predatory Pricing to Capture Market** | Funding portfolio company price wars to drive competitors out of business before raising prices | Using financial firepower to eliminate competition unfairly |

## Category 3: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PV-3.1 | **Fund Fee Opacity**             | Failing to disclose all fees, expenses, and carried interest calculations to limited partners in a timely and comprehensible manner | Concealing true cost of fund participation |
| PV-3.2 | **Carried Interest Taxation Advantage** | Structuring compensation as carried interest to exploit lower tax rates while lobbying against policy changes | Exploiting tax treatment that does not reflect economic substance |
| PV-3.3 | **Portfolio Company Financial Concealment** | Withholding consolidated financial performance data from limited partners and regulators | Obscuring fund performance and portfolio health |
| PV-3.4 | **Valuation Manipulation**       | Inflating portfolio company valuations at fundraising milestones to present stronger track records | Misrepresenting investment performance to attract capital |
| PV-3.5 | **Co-Investment Disclosure Gaps** | Offering selective co-investment opportunities without disclosing conflicts of interest or preferential terms | Creating information asymmetries among limited partners |

## Category 4: Worker Impact

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PV-4.1 | **Mass Layoffs Post-Acquisition** | Implementing significant workforce reductions immediately after acquisition to improve short-term financial metrics | Treating workers as disposable cost units |
| PV-4.2 | **Benefit Stripping**            | Eliminating or reducing healthcare, retirement, and other benefits after taking control of portfolio companies | Eroding worker compensation to boost exit valuations |
| PV-4.3 | **Worker Misclassification in Portfolio Companies** | Pressuring portfolio companies to classify employees as contractors to reduce benefit obligations | Circumventing labor protections to reduce costs |
| PV-4.4 | **Non-Compete Enforcement**      | Imposing non-compete clauses on low-wage portfolio company workers to suppress turnover | Restricting worker mobility for cost control |
| PV-4.5 | **Workforce Reduction Without Transition Support** | Eliminating positions without adequate severance, retraining programs, or job placement assistance | Abandoning displaced workers without mitigation |

## Category 5: LP Relations

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PV-5.1 | **Return Calculation Manipulation** | Using IRR, subscription credit lines, or other mechanisms to inflate reported returns relative to actual cash-on-cash outcomes | Misleading limited partners about fund performance |
| PV-5.2 | **Fund Extension Without Consent** | Extending fund life beyond stated terms to avoid liquidating underperforming assets at unfavorable valuations | Trapping limited partner capital beyond agreed horizons |
| PV-5.3 | **Side Letter Favoritism**       | Granting preferential fee structures, co-investment rights, or information access to select limited partners without disclosure | Creating unequal treatment among investors in the same fund |
| PV-5.4 | **Re-Up Pressure Tactics**       | Conditioning access to future fund vintages on commitments to current funds, limiting genuine investor discretion | Coercing capital commitments through access leverage |
| PV-5.5 | **GP Commitment Shortfall**       | Failing to maintain general partner capital commitments at industry-standard levels, misaligning risk | Reducing skin-in-the-game while retaining full upside participation |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Limit leverage** — cap debt-to-EBITDA ratios on portfolio companies; disclose dividend recapitalization plans to limited partners before execution.
2. **Protect workers** — provide minimum transition support including severance, continued healthcare, and retraining for any post-acquisition layoffs exceeding 10% of workforce.
3. **Disclose fully** — publish itemized fee schedules, carried interest calculations, and portfolio company financials to limited partners on a quarterly basis.
4. **Maintain pension obligations** — prohibit reduction of employer pension contributions below pre-acquisition levels; honor all existing retirement commitments.
5. **Ensure LP consent** — require supermajority limited partner approval for fund term extensions beyond stated maturity.
6. **Standardize side letters** — disclose all side letter arrangements to the full LP base; prohibit terms that materially disadvantage non-preferred investors.
