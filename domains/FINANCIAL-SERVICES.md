# Financial Services — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Banking, Credit Cards, Investing, Fintech  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to banks, credit card issuers, investment platforms, payday lenders, and fintech companies. Financial services directly determine economic mobility; unethical practices in this domain trap people in debt cycles, strip wealth, and deepen systemic inequality.

---

## Category 1: Fee & Rate Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FS-1.1 | **Overdraft Cascading**          | Processing transactions largest-first to maximize overdraft fees, creating cascading charges from small purchases | Transaction reordering for profit     |
| FS-1.2 | **Maintenance Fee Traps**        | Charging monthly maintenance fees when balances dip below thresholds, pushing accounts further negative | Fee-driven debt spiral               |
| FS-1.3 | **Foreign Transaction Fee Obscurity** | Burying foreign transaction and conversion fees in fine print rather than displaying them at point of sale | Hidden cost practice                  |
| FS-1.4 | **Penalty APR Weaponization**    | Applying penalty interest rates to entire balances after a single late payment, even for new purchases | Disproportionate punishment            |
| FS-1.5 | **Minimum Payment Illusion**     | Displaying minimum payments prominently while burying total interest cost and time-to-payoff information | Encouraging debt prolongation          |

## Category 2: Lending & Credit

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FS-2.1 | **Payday Debt Traps**           | Offering short-term loans at effective APRs exceeding 300%, designed for rollover rather than repayment | Predatory lending                     |
| FS-2.2 | **Subprime Targeting**           | Marketing high-interest products specifically to low-income and minority communities | Discriminatory exploitation            |
| FS-2.3 | **Credit Score Gatekeeping**    | Using credit scores for non-credit decisions (employment, insurance, housing) beyond their predictive validity | Scope creep of scoring models         |
| FS-2.4 | **Hard Inquiry Abuse**           | Generating multiple hard credit inquiries from a single shopping event, each reducing the applicant's score | Penalizing rate-shopping              |
| FS-2.5 | **Forced Arbitration**           | Requiring arbitration clauses that prevent class-action suits for systemic lending violations | Denying legal recourse                |

## Category 3: Investment & Advisory

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FS-3.1 | **Payment for Order Flow**       | Routing customer orders to market makers who pay for the flow, potentially obtaining worse execution prices | Conflict of interest; hidden cost     |
| FS-3.2 | **Fiduciary Standard Evasion**   | Operating under suitability rather than fiduciary standards to recommend higher-commission products | Prioritizing advisor profit over client interest |
| FS-3.3 | **Hidden Fund Fees**             | Failing to clearly disclose 12b-1 fees, expense ratios, and fund-level trading costs | Cost concealment                      |
| FS-3.4 | **Gamification of Trading**      | Using confetti, push notifications, and social feeds to encourage frequent trading in speculative assets | Encouraging reckless financial behavior |
| FS-3.5 | **Proprietary Product Pushing**   | Steering clients toward in-house funds and products that carry higher fees than comparable external options | Self-dealing                          |

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FS-4.1 | **Data Breach Concealment**      | Delaying public disclosure of data breaches to avoid reputational damage beyond legally required timelines | Withholding critical security information |
| FS-4.2 | **Screen Scraping Without Consent** | Using customer login credentials to scrape data from other financial institutions without explicit authorization | Unauthorized data access              |
| FS-4.3 | **Behavioral Scoring Opacity**   | Using AI-driven behavioral scoring for credit decisions without disclosing factors or allowing appeals | Unaccountable algorithmic decisions    |
| FS-4.4 | **Cross-Sell Data Abuse**        | Using transaction data from one product to target customers for other, potentially unsuitable products | Misusing data for predatory targeting  |

## Category 5: Account Access & Closures

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FS-5.1 | **Account Freezing Without Notice** | Freezing accounts suspected of fraud without providing immediate explanation or recourse path | Due process denial                    |
| FS-5.2 | **Zombie Account Drain**          | Charging inactivity fees that slowly drain abandoned accounts to zero | Slow confiscation of dormant funds    |
| FS-5.3 | **Debanking Without Transparency** | Closing accounts based on internal risk models without providing specific reasons, preventing the customer from addressing the concern | Opaque exclusion; financial marginalization |
| FS-5.4 | **Deposit Hold Exploitation**    | Placing extended holds on deposits to float interest-free funds while customers cannot access their money | Unfair use of customer funds          |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish total cost of credit** — display total interest paid, time to payoff, and all fees before account opening or loan consummation, using standardized comparison formats.
2. **Adopt fiduciary standards** — require all investment advisors and robo-advisors to act in the client's best interest; disclose all conflicts of interest in writing.
3. **Implement fair lending audits** — conduct annual audits of lending decisions for disparate impact; publish aggregated approval and rate data by demographics.
4. **Commit to breach transparency** — notify affected customers within 72 hours of confirmed breach; provide free credit monitoring for at least 24 months.
5. **Provide account closure due process** — give specific reasons for involuntary closures; allow 30-day transition periods; provide written appeal procedures.
