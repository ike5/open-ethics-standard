# Domain: Media & Journalism

**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This standard defines ethical practices and violations within media and journalism, covering editorial independence, data stewardship, labor fairness, public access, and accuracy. It addresses practices that compromise journalistic integrity, exploit audiences and workers, restrict access to information, or erode truth in reporting. Organizations operating under this domain standard commit to independent, transparent, and accountable journalism that serves the public interest.

---

## Editorial Independence

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| ME-1.1 | Advertiser influence firewall | Maintain strict separation between advertising and editorial operations with written policies preventing advertiser pressure on coverage | Allowing advertisers to influence, suppress, or alter editorial coverage through explicit or implicit threats to withdraw advertising revenue |
| ME-1.2 | Ownership conflict disclosure | Disclose media ownership structures, financial interests, and any stakeholder relationships that could influence editorial decisions | Failing to disclose ownership conflicts when reporting on entities in which media owners hold financial interests |
| ME-1.3 | Sponsored content labeling | Clearly and prominently label all sponsored, native, or paid content as such, distinguishing it from independent editorial material | Publishing sponsored or paid content without clear labeling, enabling readers to mistake advertising for independent journalism |
| ME-1.4 | Editorial leadership autonomy | Ensure editors-in-chief and editorial leadership have final authority over editorial content without interference from business or ownership | Allowing business executives or owners to overrule editorial decisions, kill stories, or mandate coverage to protect commercial or political interests |
| ME-1.5 | Political endorsement transparency | Clearly distinguish news coverage from editorial opinion and disclose any institutional political endorsements or positions | Blurring the line between news reporting and opinion, or using news coverage to advance undisclosed institutional political positions |

## Data

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| ME-2.1 | Paywall data fairness | Collect only the data necessary for subscription management and clearly disclose how paywall registration data is used | Harvesting personal data through paywall registration—demographics, reading habits, contact information—for sale or secondary commercial use without informed consent |
| ME-2.2 | Subscriber list protection | Do not sell, share, or transfer newsletter subscriber lists or individual subscriber data to third parties | Selling newsletter subscriber email lists, reading preferences, or engagement data to data brokers, advertisers, or political organizations |
| ME-2.3 | Cookie consent honesty | Present cookie consent options with genuine choice, including a clearly accessible reject option, and honor user selections promptly | Employing cookie consent dark patterns—pre-selected options, confusing toggles, hidden reject buttons—to manipulate users into excessive tracking |
| ME-2.4 | Data breach notification | Notify readers and subscribers promptly of any data breach involving their personal information | Failing to inform affected subscribers of data breaches in a timely manner, minimizing breach scope, or delaying notification for reputational reasons |
| ME-2.5 | Anonymization of sources' data | Protect the identity and data of confidential sources with robust technical and operational safeguards | Storing source-identifying data in unencrypted or accessible systems, or failing to implement source protection protocols |

## Labor

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| ME-3.1 | Fair freelancer compensation | Pay freelance contributors at rates commensurate with their experience and the publication's budget, with timely payment terms | Suppressing freelance rates below living-wage thresholds, exploiting oversupply of freelance journalists, or imposing excessive rights grabs for minimal compensation |
| ME-3.2 | Byline and credit integrity | Attribute all published work to its rightful author, including freelance, staff, and collaborative contributions | Denying bylines to contributors, publishing work under staff bylines that was substantially produced by uncredited freelancers, or removing author attribution |
| ME-3.3 | AI disclosure and displacement safeguards | Disclose any use of AI in content production and ensure AI supplements rather than replaces journalistic staff | Replacing journalists with AI content generation without disclosure, or reducing editorial staff under the pretext of AI efficiency while degrading reporting quality |
| ME-3.4 | Contract fairness for contributors | Offer freelance contracts with clear terms on payment, rights, kill fees, and publication timelines | Imposing one-sided contracts that demand full rights, eliminate kill fees, allow indefinite publication delays, and prevent freelancers from negotiating terms |
| ME-3.5 | Newsroom diversity | Invest in hiring and retaining journalists from diverse backgrounds reflective of the communities covered | Maintaining homogeneous newsrooms that produce coverage reflecting limited perspectives and failing to address systemic barriers to diverse hiring |

## Access

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| ME-4.1 | Public interest open access | Make reporting on matters of significant public interest—emergencies, public health, elections—freely accessible regardless of paywall status | Placing critical public interest stories behind paywalls that prevent affected communities from accessing vital safety and governance information |
| ME-4.2 | Archive preservation | Maintain and make accessible the publication's archives as a matter of public record and historical integrity | Deleting or removing access to news archives, particularly coverage that reflects poorly on the publication or its interests |
| ME-4.3 | Geographic access equity | Avoid restricting access to news content based on geographic location, particularly for public interest reporting | Blocking access to news content by region or country, preventing audiences from accessing reporting relevant to their communities |
| ME-4.4 | Accessibility compliance | Ensure digital content meets accessibility standards for readers with visual, auditory, or cognitive disabilities | Failing to provide alt text, captions, transcripts, or accessible formatting, effectively excluding readers with disabilities from journalistic content |

## Accuracy

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| ME-5.1 | Headline-body accuracy | Ensure headlines accurately represent the substance of the article and do not mislead or exaggerate | Publishing clickbait headlines that misrepresent, exaggerate, or distort the actual content of the article |
| ME-5.2 | Correction prominence | Issue corrections prominently, in the same format and location as the original error, with clear explanation of what changed | Burying corrections in inconspicuous locations, omitting explanations, or quietly editing articles without acknowledging the original error |
| ME-5.3 | Source protection commitment | Protect the identity and safety of confidential sources and whistleblowers who provide information in the public interest | Eroding source protection through weak legal defense, cooperation with authorities without resistance, or revealing source identities under pressure |
| ME-5.4 | Verification standards | Verify information through multiple independent sources before publication and clearly attribute claims | Publishing unverified claims, single-source allegations, or unconfirmed rumors without disclosure of verification status |
| ME-5.5 | Contextual reporting | Report stories with sufficient context, background, and nuance to avoid misleading audiences | Stripping context from stories to produce sensationalized narratives that misrepresent the significance or nature of events |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this standard should:

1. **Adopt Editorial Independence Policies** — Publish and enforce written policies separating editorial decision-making from advertising, ownership, and political influence.
2. **Conduct Data Practices Audits** — Review data collection, retention, and sharing practices annually, including paywall registration, cookie consent, and subscriber data handling.
3. **Establish Contributor Standards** — Set minimum payment rates, rights terms, and credit standards for freelance contributors, published and publicly accessible.
4. **Create Public Interest Access Protocols** — Define criteria for public interest content that must remain freely accessible and ensure archives are preserved.
5. **Implement Accuracy Review Processes** — Establish formal correction policies, verification checklists, and editorial review standards with publicly accessible correction logs.
6. **Train Editorial and Business Staff** — Require regular ethics training for journalists, editors, advertising staff, and data teams on the standards in this document.
