# Cleaning Services — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Commercial & Residential Cleaning Services  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to commercial and residential cleaning service providers, including franchise operations, independent contractors, and platform-mediated cleaning businesses. Cleaning workers—disproportionately women, immigrants, and people of color—face systemic wage theft, chemical exposure, and privacy violations. Unethical pricing and safety practices exploit both workers and clients in an industry marked by low margins and high turnover.

---

## Category 1: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CL-1.1 | **Off-the-Clock Work**              | Requiring workers to perform setup, travel, or cleanup tasks without pay | Wage theft; labor exploitation         |
| CL-1.2 | **Worker Misclassification**         | Classifying employees as independent contractors to avoid benefits, overtime, and payroll taxes | Labor law evasion; benefit denial     |
| CL-1.3 | **Chemical Exposure Without Protection** | Providing inadequate or no personal protective equipment for workers handling hazardous cleaning agents | Worker health endangerment            |
| CL-1.4 | **Overtime Denial**                  | Requiring schedules that exceed 40 hours while refusing overtime compensation | Wage theft; labor law violation        |
| CL-1.5 | **Retaliation for Reporting**         | Punishing workers who report safety violations or wage complaints through reduced hours, termination, or blacklisting | Suppression of worker rights           |

## Category 2: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CL-2.1 | **Bait-and-Switch Quotes**           | Advertising low rates then inflating charges after on-site assessment | Deceptive pricing; consumer fraud     |
| CL-2.2 | **Mandatory Tip Suggestion Replacing Fair Wages** | Encouraging or requiring clients to tip instead of paying workers a living wage | Shifting fair compensation burden to consumers; wage suppression |
| CL-2.3 | **Per-Visit Fee Inflation**           | Increasing per-visit charges without proportional service improvement or transparent notification | Price gouging; contract bad faith     |
| CL-2.4 | **Contract Auto-Renewal with Escalation** | Automatically renewing contracts at higher rates with difficult cancellation processes | Consumer lock-in; deceptive billing   |
| CL-2.5 | **Hidden Supply Surcharges**          | Adding un disclosed charges for cleaning supplies, equipment, or travel fees | Hidden cost practice                  |

## Category 3: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CL-3.1 | **Chemical Hazard Non-Disclosure**    | Failing to inform clients and workers about hazardous chemicals used in their spaces | Withholding safety-critical information |
| CL-3.2 | **Wet Floor and Slip Risk Neglect**   | Leaving wet surfaces unmarked or failing to provide adequate drying time before space re-entry | Client and worker injury risk         |
| CL-3.3 | **Equipment Injury Training Absence** | Not training workers on safe operation of industrial cleaning equipment | Worker endangerment through negligence |
| CL-3.4 | **Biohazard Handling Without Proper Gear** | Assigning workers to clean blood, mold, sewage, or other biohazards without appropriate protective equipment and training | Worker health endangerment; regulatory violation |
| CL-3.5 | **Ventilation Failure**              | Requiring use of volatile chemicals in poorly ventilated spaces     | Respiratory hazard; worker and occupant endangerment |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CL-4.1 | **Home Security Code Collection**   | Retaining alarm codes, gate access credentials, or entry patterns beyond the service period | Unnecessary data retention; security risk |
| CL-4.2 | **Key Duplication Risk**             | Failing to implement key control protocols, enabling unauthorized duplication or retention of client keys | Client security breach facilitation   |
| CL-4.3 | **Schedule Data Sale**               | Selling or sharing client scheduling data revealing occupancy patterns, travel plans, or daily routines | Privacy violation; enabling targeted crimes |
| CL-4.4 | **Photo Documentation Privacy**      | Taking and retaining photos of client spaces without explicit consent or beyond service needs | Privacy violation; surveillance       |

## Category 5: Quality

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CL-5.1 | **Employee Substitution for Insured Staff** | Sending uninsured or unvetted workers to jobs sold on the basis of bonded and insured personnel | Consumer fraud; liability gap         |
| CL-5.2 | **Supply Cost Opacity**              | Using inferior or diluted products while charging clients for premium-grade supplies | Consumer fraud; quality deception     |
| CL-5.3 | **Damage Insurance Gaps**            | Failing to carry adequate liability coverage or making claims processes prohibitively difficult | Client financial exposure; accountability avoidance |
| CL-5.4 | **Green Cleaning Claims Without Verification** | Marketing services as eco-friendly or non-toxic while using conventional chemical products | Greenwashing; consumer deception      |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Guarantee wage transparency** — provide itemized pay statements showing hourly rates, overtime, and deductions; classify workers in compliance with applicable labor law.
2. **Disclose all chemicals** — maintain and share Safety Data Sheets for all products used; provide appropriate PPE at no cost to workers.
3. **Publish clear pricing** — offer binding quotes before service begins; disclose all fees, surcharges, and auto-renewal terms in plain language.
4. **Implement key and access controls** — use auditable key management systems; destroy or return all access credentials upon contract termination.
5. **Verify green claims** — obtain third-party certification for any environmental or non-toxic claims; disclose product ingredients upon request.
6. **Maintain adequate insurance** — carry liability coverage proportional to operational risk; publish claims procedures accessible to clients.
