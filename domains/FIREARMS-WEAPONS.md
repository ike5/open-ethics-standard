# Firearms & Weapons — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Firearms & Weapons Manufacturing and Sales  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to firearms and weapons manufacturers, distributors, retailers, and online sellers. Firearms present a unique ethical challenge: they are lawful products whose misuse causes irreversible harm at scale. The industry's choices in marketing, sales practices, safety design, data handling, and community engagement directly influence rates of homicide, suicide, accidental death, and trafficking. Ethical conduct in this domain requires balancing lawful commerce with an affirmative duty to prevent diversion and harm.

---

## Category 1: Marketing

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FW-1.1 | **Militaristic Advertising Appealing to Fear** | Marketing firearms using military imagery, combat scenarios, or fear-based messaging suggesting personal safety requires armed response | Exploiting fear; normalizing violence  |
| FW-1.2 | **Social Media Presence Reaching Minors** | Placing firearm advertisements or promotional content on platforms with significant underage audiences | Marketing dangerous products to children |
| FW-1.3 | **Loophole Product Marketing**        | Advertising firearms or accessories specifically designed to exploit legal loopholes (e.g., pistol braces redefining short-barreled rifles) | Deliberate regulatory circumvention    |
| FW-1.4 | **Accessory Marketing Enabling Modification** | Promoting accessories whose primary purpose is converting legal firearms into more dangerous or restricted configurations | Enabling unlawful modification; public safety risk |

## Category 2: Sales

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FW-2.1 | **Background Check Evasion Facilitation** | Selling firearm components, kits, or parts that allow assembly without background checks, or directing customers toward private-sale exemptions | Undermining public safety law; enabling prohibited access |
| FW-2.2 | **Straw Purchase Tolerance**          | Completing sales when indicators suggest a buyer is purchasing on behalf of a prohibited person without reporting or refusing the sale | Enabling illegal firearms trafficking |
| FW-2.3 | **Gun Show Sale Opacity**             | Conducting sales at gun shows without the same record-keeping and background check standards applied in stores | Exploiting regulatory gaps; undermining traceability |
| FW-2.4 | **Online Sale Jurisdiction Shopping** | Facilitating or encouraging purchasers to exploit varying state laws by directing sales through permissive jurisdictions | Regulatory circumvention; enabling unlawful possession |
| FW-2.5 | **Bulk Purchase Without Reporting**  | Failing to implement internal reporting protocols for suspicious bulk or rapid-fire purchases beyond legal thresholds | Trafficking indicator disregard; public safety negligence |

## Category 3: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FW-3.1 | **Recall Delay**                     | Delaying product recalls for known defects or safety issues to avoid financial impact | Public endangerment for profit        |
| FW-3.2 | **Child Safety Mechanism Resistance** | Opposing or failing to incorporate built-in child safety features such as loaded chamber indicators or manual safeties | Preventable child injury and death    |
| FW-3.3 | **Smart Gun Technology Opposition**  | Actively lobbying against or refusing to develop personalized firearm technology that prevents unauthorized use | Blocking life-saving innovation       |
| FW-3.4 | **Storage Safety Omission from Sales** | Failing to provide or recommend secure storage devices and practices at point of sale | Neglecting preventable unauthorized access; suicide and accident risk |

---

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FW-4.1 | **Purchase Record Retention Beyond Requirement** | Maintaining detailed purchase databases beyond legally required periods, creating centralized records vulnerable to misuse | Unnecessary data retention; privacy risk |
| FW-4.2 | **Ballistic Data Opposition**         | Lobbying against ballistic fingerprinting or microstamping programs that aid crime investigation | Obstructing law enforcement; accountability avoidance |
| FW-4.3 | **Serial Number Traceability Resistance** | Opposing serialization requirements or creating products deliberately difficult to trace | Enabling criminal anonymity; undermining investigations |
| FW-4.4 | **Trafficking Pattern Non-Cooperation** | Refusing to share internal sales data with law enforcement investigating trafficking patterns despite valid legal process | Obstructing public safety investigations |

---

## Category 5: Community

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FW-5.1 | **Dealer Concentration in High-Crime Areas** | Saturating communities already experiencing high violence with retail firearm outlets | Compounding community harm; violence amplification |
| FW-5.2 | **Community Violence Impact Non-Acknowledgment** | Refusing to acknowledge or address the correlation between industry practices and community gun violence rates | Responsibility evasion; community harm denial |
| FW-5.3 | **Suicide Prevention Omission**      | Failing to incorporate suicide awareness resources, waiting periods, or safe storage messaging in sales processes | Missing preventable suicide intervention opportunities |
| FW-5.4 | **Irresponsible Dealer Practices**   | Operating dealerships with repeated regulatory violations, lost inventory, or documented trafficking connections without corrective action | Enabling criminal market supply; regulatory indifference |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Conduct responsible marketing** — eliminate fear-based and militaristic advertising; restrict promotional content from platforms with significant minor audiences; market only lawful uses.
2. **Enforce sales integrity** — implement universal background checks for all sales including private and gun show transactions; report and refuse suspicious purchases; maintain traceable records.
3. **Prioritize product safety** — incorporate available safety technologies; issue recalls promptly for known defects; include secure storage recommendations with every sale.
4. **Cooperate with investigations** — share trafficking-relevant data with lawful law enforcement requests; support ballistic identification and serialization programs; maintain records only as long as legally required.
5. **Engage communities responsibly** — assess retail location impact on community violence; fund suicide prevention and safe storage education; correct or close dealers with repeated violations.
