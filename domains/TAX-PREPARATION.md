# Tax Preparation & Filing — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Tax Preparation & Filing Services  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to tax preparation companies, filing platforms, and related service providers. Tax preparation is a mandatory interaction with government for most working adults, and the industry's practices directly affect whether people can fulfill their civic obligations affordably and accurately. When tax preparers prioritize profit over accuracy, lobby against simplification, or exploit the complexity they help create, they extract wealth from the public while undermining trust in the tax system itself.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TX-1.1 | **Refund Anticipation Loan Interest Concealment** | Marketing refund anticipation loans or advances as "instant refunds" while obscuring the effective interest rates, which can exceed 100% APR | Predatory lending; rate concealment   |
| TX-1.2 | **Fee Opacity Until Filing Complete** | Declining to disclose total preparation fees until after the return is filed, preventing consumers from comparing prices or withdrawing without penalty | Pricing deception; lock-in exploitation |
| TX-1.3 | **Upselling Unnecessary Audit Protection** | Pressure-selling audit defense, identity theft protection, and extended service plans that provide minimal value relative to their cost, particularly for simple returns | Unnecessary upselling; fear-based sales |
| TX-1.4 | **Per-Form Pricing Inflation**    | Charging separately for each form or schedule, resulting in total fees far exceeding advertised base prices — disproportionately affecting taxpayers with more complex situations | Price inflation; complexity exploitation |
| TX-1.5 | **Add-On Fee Stacking**            | Adding fees for e-filing, direct deposit, state returns, and other services presented as standard features, creating final bills that bear little relation to advertised prices | Fee concealment; bait-and-switch pricing |

## Category 2: Lobbying

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TX-2.1 | **Lobbying Against IRS Free Filing** | Spending millions lobbying against the expansion of IRS free filing capabilities, preventing the government from offering a service that would save taxpayers billions in preparation fees | Anti-public-interest lobbying; market capture |
| TX-2.2 | **Intentional Complexity Perpetuation** | Lobbying against tax code simplification measures that would reduce the need for paid preparation, directly profiting from the complexity that burdens taxpayers | Self-serving regulation; public harm  |
| TX-2.3 | **Legislative Opposition to Pre-Filled Returns** | Opposing pre-filled or pre-populated return systems — proven to work in dozens of countries — because they would eliminate the market for paid preparation services | Innovation obstruction; rent-seeking  |
| TX-2.4 | **Revolving Door with Tax Agency** | Employing former IRS officials and tax policymakers in lobbying and advisory roles to influence regulations in favor of the preparation industry | Regulatory capture; conflict of interest |

## Category 3: Accuracy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TX-3.1 | **Error Rate Non-Disclosure**      | Refusing to publish or disclose internal accuracy rates, leaving consumers unable to assess whether a preparer's error rate poses a financial or legal risk | Transparency violation; consumer risk |
| TX-3.2 | **Penalty Guarantee Fine Print**   | Advertising accuracy guarantees or penalty coverage that is voided by extensive exclusions, short claim windows, and burdensome documentation requirements | Misleading guarantee; fine-print evasion |
| TX-3.3 | **Preparer Qualification Opacity**  | Failing to disclose preparer credentials, training levels, or PTIN status, allowing seasonal or unqualified workers to handle complex returns without client awareness | Qualification concealment; competency misrepresentation |
| TX-3.4 | **Data Entry Outsourcing Without Disclosure** | Sending taxpayer returns to offshore data entry operations without informing clients that their sensitive financial information will be processed outside the country | Transparency violation; security risk concealment |
| TX-3.5 | **Algorithmic Error Shifting**     | Using software that shifts liability for calculation errors to the taxpayer while presenting the preparer's role as merely data entry | Liability evasion; accountability avoidance |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TX-4.1 | **Tax Return Data Sale to Brokers** | Selling anonymized or pseudonymized tax return data — including income, deductions, and filing patterns — to data brokers and financial institutions | Privacy violation; data exploitation  |
| TX-4.2 | **Financial Product Targeting from Return Data** | Using tax return information to target clients for refund advances, high-interest loans, and financial products, profiting from the very financial vulnerability revealed by their returns | Conflict of interest; data misuse    |
| TX-4.3 | **Identity Theft Vulnerability Through Data Retention** | Retaining tax return data, Social Security numbers, and financial records for longer than necessary or beyond legal requirements, creating targets for data breaches and identity theft | Data over-retention; security negligence |
| TX-4.4 | **SSN Handling Insecurity**         | Storing, transmitting, or processing Social Security numbers without encryption, access controls, or audit trails proportionate to the sensitivity of the data | Security violation; breach risk      |
| TX-4.5 | **Third-Party Data Sharing Without Consent** | Sharing taxpayer information with affiliated or unaffiliated third parties for marketing, analytics, or product development without explicit, informed, opt-in consent | Consent violation; data exploitation  |

## Category 5: Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| TX-5.1 | **Low-Income Service Withdrawal**  | Discontinuing free or low-cost filing options for low-income taxpayers while simultaneously lobbying against government-provided free alternatives | Access restriction; dual exploitation |
| TX-5.2 | **Refund Delay for Product Profit** | Deliberately slowing refund processing for taxpayers who decline ancillary products, or steering filers toward paid products by making the free option slower or less convenient | Coercive steering; access manipulation |
| TX-5.3 | **Credit Product Targeting at Refund Time** | Marketing refund advances, prepaid cards, and high-fee financial products to taxpayers at the moment of maximum financial need, when they are least able to evaluate terms | Predatory targeting; vulnerability exploitation |
| TX-5.4 | **Language Barrier Exploitation**   | Providing inadequate multilingual support while charging premium prices for non-English speakers, or using complex English-only agreements that non-native speakers cannot fully evaluate | Language discrimination; consent inequality |
| TX-5.5 | **Free File Program Undermining**  | Offering Free File programs with income caps, form restrictions, and hidden upsells that divert eligible taxpayers to paid products, undermining the program's intended purpose | Program subversion; bait-and-switch   |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Price transparently** — disclose all fees before service begins; present refund anticipation loan terms as loans with clear APR disclosure; eliminate hidden add-on charges; advertise prices that reflect the total cost for typical returns.
2. **Cease anti-competitive lobbying** — end opposition to IRS free filing expansion; support tax code simplification; stop blocking pre-filled return implementations; institute cooling-off periods for former government employees in lobbying roles.
3. **Guarantee accuracy** — publish error rates publicly; honor accuracy guarantees without exclusionary fine print; disclose preparer qualifications and outsourcing practices; assign clear accountability for calculation errors.
4. **Protect taxpayer data** — prohibit the sale of tax return data to brokers and marketers; obtain explicit opt-in consent for all data sharing; retain data only as long as legally required; encrypt and secure all SSN handling.
5. **Expand access equitably** — maintain free filing options for eligible taxpayers without restrictions or upsell pressure; process refunds without delay based on product selection; offer full multilingual support at no additional cost; eliminate steering toward unnecessary financial products.
