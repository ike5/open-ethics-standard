# Grocery Stores — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Grocery Retail  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to the grocery retail industry. Grocery stores occupy a unique position as providers of essential goods; unethical practices in this domain directly threaten food security, public health, and consumer trust.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Cost Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                       | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GS-1.1 | **Shrinkflation Concealment** | Reducing product quantity while keeping price and packaging the same without clear disclosure | Deceptive packaging; misrepresentation of value |
| GS-1.2 | **Unit Price Manipulation**   | Displaying unit prices in inconsistent units (e.g., per ounce vs. per pound) to hinder comparison | Obstructing informed choice           |
| GS-1.3 | **Loss Leader Exploitation**  | Selling staples below cost to draw customers, then marking up adjacent essentials disproportionately | Bait-and-switch pricing pattern       |
| GS-1.4 | **Dynamic Pricing on Essentials** | Algorithmically raising prices on essential items during emergencies, weather events, or supply disruptions | Price gouging; exploitation of necessity |
| GS-1.5 | **Checkout Price Discrepancy** | Shelf price differs from scanned price at register, consistently in the store's favor | Systematic overcharging               |
| GS-1.6 | **Membership Price Gating**   | Restricting fair prices behind paid memberships for essential goods | Creating artificial barriers to affordable food |

## Category 2: Food Advertising & Labeling

| Ref   | Practice                        | Description                                                         | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GS-2.1 | **Health Claim Misrepresentation** | Making misleading health claims (e.g., "all natural," "supports immunity") without substantiation | Deceptive health marketing            |
| GS-2.2 | **Origin Washing**              | Misleading consumers about the geographic origin or sourcing of products (e.g., "local" without clear definition) | False provenance claims               |
| GS-2.3 | **Sustainability Greenwashing**  | Labeling products as "sustainable" or "eco-friendly" without verifiable standards | Environmental deception               |
| GS-2.4 | **Placement Deception**         | Placing paid-placement products at eye level or end-caps while presenting them as organically recommended | Undisclosed native advertising        |
| GS-2.5 | **Allergen Obscurity**          | Using ambiguous ingredient names or tiny font sizes to obscure allergen information | Withholding safety-critical data      |
| GS-2.6 | **Expiration Date Manipulation** | Re-labeling or obscuring expiration/best-by dates on perishable goods | Endangering consumer health           |

## Category 3: Supply Chain & Labor

| Ref   | Practice                        | Description                                                         | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GS-3.1 | **Supplier Squeeze**            | Using market dominance to impose unfair terms on small and local suppliers | Exploitation of power asymmetry       |
| GS-3.2 | **Labor Opaque Sourcing**       | Selling products from suppliers with known labor violations without disclosure | Complicity in labor exploitation       |
| GS-3.3 | **Food Waste Concealment**      | Discarding edible food rather than donating, while claiming community commitment | Hypocrisy; community harm             |
| GS-3.4 | **Wage Suppression**           | Scheduling practices (on-call shifts, unpredictable hours) that prevent workers from earning a living wage or seeking other employment | Labor exploitation                    |

---

## Category 4: Data & Surveillance

| Ref   | Practice                        | Description                                                         | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GS-4.1 | **Loyalty Data Harvesting**     | Requiring loyalty program enrollment to access fair prices while collecting and selling purchasing data | Coerced consent; data exploitation    |
| GS-4.2 | **Behavioral Pricing**          | Using purchase history to offer personalized pricing that disadvantages certain demographics | Discriminatory pricing                |
| GS-4.3 | **Surveillance Checkout**      | Deploying facial recognition or biometric tracking in stores without clear, opt-in consent | Privacy violation; coerced surveillance |

---

## Category 5: Product Quality & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                        | Description                                                         | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GS-5.1 | **Diluted Store Brands**        | Private-label products formulated to closely mimic brand names but with inferior ingredients | Copycat deception                     |
| GS-5.2 | **Freshness Illusion**          | Red lighting in meat sections, misting systems for produce that create false appearance of freshness | Sensory manipulation                  |
| GS-5.3 | **Recall Non-Disclosure**       | Failing to prominently notify customers of product recalls carried in-store | Withholding safety information        |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish a pricing transparency statement** — disclose unit pricing methodology and commit to consistent units of measure.
2. **Implement a labeling integrity policy** — all health, origin, and sustainability claims must reference verifiable, third-party standards.
3. **Maintain a food donation program** — establish partnerships with food banks and publish annual food waste and donation metrics.
4. **Adopt fair scheduling practices** — provide workers with schedules at least two weeks in advance and offer minimum hour guarantees.
5. **Disclose data practices** — publish a clear data collection notice at store entrances and on loyalty sign-up forms; offer price-matching without data collection.
