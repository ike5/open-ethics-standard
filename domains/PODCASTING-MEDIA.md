# Podcasting & Media — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Podcasting & Media (Podcast Networks, Hosting Platforms, Ad Networks, Media Companies)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to podcasting platforms, networks, and media companies. Podcasting occupies an intimate position in listeners' lives, and unethical practices in this domain exploit that intimacy through covert advertising, data surveillance, creator exploitation, and content manipulation.

---

## Category 1: Advertising

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PO-1.1 | **Host-Read Ad Disclosure Failure**           | Failing to clearly distinguish host-read advertisements from organic editorial content | Blurring the line between personal endorsement and paid promotion |
| PO-1.2 | **Dynamically Inserted Ad Opacity**           | Inserting or replacing advertisements into archived episodes without disclosing that content has been altered | Modifying historical content and listener expectations without notice |
| PO-1.3 | **Ad Targeting via Listening Data**           | Using detailed listening behavior (completion rates, skip patterns, topic affinity) to serve hyper-targeted advertisements | Exploiting intimate listening data for advertising precision |
| PO-1.4 | **Native Advertising as Editorial Content**   | Producing advertising segments that mimic the show's editorial format without clear labeling | Deceiving listeners into consuming marketing as content |
| PO-1.5 | **Political Ad Disclosure Gaps**             | Failing to disclose the source, sponsorship, or political affiliation of issue-based or electoral advertising | Undermining transparency in political communication |

## Category 2: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PO-2.1 | **Download Analytics Sale**                   | Selling podcast download counts, listening duration, and episode-level engagement data to third parties without listener consent | Monetizing audience behavior data that was collected for operational purposes |
| PO-2.2 | **Listener Profiling**                        | Building detailed psychographic or demographic profiles based on subscription patterns, listening habits, and device data | Creating surveillance profiles that listeners would not expect from audio consumption |
| PO-2.3 | **IP-Based Geographic Tracking**              | Using IP addresses to determine listener location for targeted advertising, content restriction, or resale | Tracking physical location without explicit consent under the guise of service delivery |
| PO-2.4 | **Cross-Platform Listener Tracking**          | Correlating listening data across multiple platforms, devices, or services to build unified listener profiles | Building comprehensive profiles without aggregate informed consent |
| PO-2.5 | **Children's Listening Data Collection**      | Gathering listening data from content known to be consumed by minors without parental consent | Violating child privacy by collecting data through content that attracts underage audiences |

## Category 3: Creator Treatment

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PO-3.1 | **Platform Revenue Share Imbalance**          | Retaining a disproportionate share of advertising or subscription revenue relative to the value created by creators | Extracting value from creative work without fair compensation |
| PO-3.2 | **Content Ownership Grabs**                   | Requiring creators to transfer intellectual property rights, licensing control, or derivative works as a condition of distribution | Stripping creators of ownership over their own work |
| PO-3.3 | **Algorithmic Discovery Pay-to-Play**        | Requiring payment or exclusive terms for favorable algorithmic placement, chart positioning, or editorial featuring | Making visibility a paid privilege rather than a meritocratic outcome |
| PO-3.4 | **Contract Opacity and Lock-In**             | Presenting creators with lengthy, complex contracts with hidden exclusivity clauses, auto-renewals, or non-compete provisions | Trapping creators in unfavorable terms through asymmetric information |
| PO-3.5 | **Unilateral Content Removal**               | Removing or demonetizing creator content without transparent criteria, prior notice, or meaningful appeal | Exercising arbitrary control over creator livelihoods |

## Category 4: Content Integrity

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PO-4.1 | **Advertiser Influence on Topics**            | Allowing advertisers to dictate, suppress, or influence editorial topics or guest selection | Surrendering editorial independence to commercial interests |
| PO-4.2 | **Sponsored Episode Non-Disclosure**         | Producing episodes entirely funded by a brand or interest group without disclosing the financial relationship | Presenting paid content as independent editorial work |
| PO-4.3 | **Review Manipulation**                       | Coordinating, incentivizing, or fabricating reviews or ratings to inflate perceived quality or popularity | Deceiving potential listeners about the authentic reception of content |
| PO-4.4 | **Misinformation Amplification**             | Prioritizing engagement metrics over accuracy, leading to algorithmic amplification of false or harmful claims | Valuing attention over truth and public harm prevention |
| PO-4.5 | **Source Concealment**                       | Failing to disclose conflicts of interest when presenting information from or about affiliated entities | Undermining audience trust through hidden relationships |

---

## Category 5: Access

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PO-5.1 | **Paywall on Public Interest Content**        | Placing content of significant public interest behind paywalls that limit access to paying subscribers | Restricting access to information that serves the public good |
| PO-5.2 | **Accessibility Gaps for Deaf Listeners**     | Failing to provide transcripts, captions, or text alternatives for audio content | Excluding deaf and hard-of-hearing audiences from participation |
| PO-5.3 | **Geographic Content Restrictions**           | Imposing regional restrictions on content availability without clear justification or listener recourse | Arbitrarily limiting access based on location |
| PO-5.4 | **Platform Exclusivity for Public Discourse** | Restricting content that addresses civic, political, or social issues to a single platform | Limiting the reach of content essential to informed citizenship |
| PO-5.5 | **Economic Barriers to Creation**             | Requiring expensive hosting, production tools, or platform fees that prevent under-resourced creators from participating | Creating economic gatekeeping that limits diversity of voices |

---

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Enforce advertising transparency** — clearly label all paid content, disclose dynamic ad insertion, and maintain separation between editorial and commercial interests.
2. **Protect listener privacy** — prohibit the sale of analytics and profiling data, minimize geographic tracking, and obtain consent before cross-platform data correlation.
3. **Treat creators fairly** — offer balanced revenue shares, preserve creator ownership, make discovery algorithms transparent, and provide clear and cancellable contracts.
4. **Maintain content integrity** — reject advertiser influence on editorial decisions, disclose all sponsorships, and establish policies against misinformation amplification.
5. **Expand access** — provide transcripts for accessibility, limit paywalls on public interest content, and reduce barriers to creator entry.
