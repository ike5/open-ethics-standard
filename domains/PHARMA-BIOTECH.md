# Pharmaceutical & Biotechnology — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Pharmaceutical & Biotechnology  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to pharmaceutical manufacturers, biotechnology firms, contract research organizations, and related entities. The pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries occupy a position of profound public trust: unethical practices can delay life-saving treatments, inflate costs beyond reach, and directly endanger patients who have no alternative recourse.

---

## Category 1: Drug Pricing & Access

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PH-1.1 | **Evergreening Patents**         | Making minor modifications to existing drugs to extend patent life and block generic entry | Anti-competitive behavior; restricts access to affordable medicine |
| PH-1.2 | **Pay-for-Delay Blocking Generics** | Paying generic manufacturers to delay market entry of affordable alternatives | Anti-competitive; inflates drug costs  |
| PH-1.3 | **Price Gouging on Essential Medications** | Raising prices dramatically on off-patent or acquired essential drugs with no competitive alternative | Exploitation of medical necessity     |
| PH-1.4 | **Orphan Drug Exploitation**     | Securing orphan drug designations and pricing incentives for drugs that serve large populations or were already developed | Misuse of public incentive programs; diverting resources from true rare diseases |
| PH-1.5 | **Therapeutic Similarity Blocking** | Using patent thickets and litigation to block biosimilar and generic entry beyond legitimate intellectual property protection | Prolonged monopoly; denying patient access |

## Category 2: Clinical Trials

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PH-2.1 | **Selective Result Publication** | Publishing only favorable trial results while suppressing negative or inconclusive findings | Scientific fraud; patient endangerment |
| PH-2.2 | **Testing in Vulnerable Populations Without Full Consent** | Conducting trials in populations with limited capacity for informed consent or limited access to alternative care | Exploitation of vulnerability; consent violation |
| PH-2.3 | **Placebo Use When Effective Treatment Exists** | Using placebo controls in trials where established effective treatment exists, exposing participants to unnecessary risk | Violation of ethical research standards; patient harm |
| PH-2.4 | **Compassionate Use Denial**     | Refusing expanded access or compassionate use for terminally ill patients with no treatment alternatives, particularly when safety data exists | Denying last-resort access; prioritizing trial enrollment over patient welfare |
| PH-2.5 | **Post-Trial Access Failure**    | Failing to provide continued access to beneficial investigational treatments after trial completion for participants who benefited | Abandoning participants who relied on treatment |
| PH-2.6 | **Adverse Event Underreporting** | Delaying or downgrading adverse event reports to regulatory authorities during ongoing trials | Concealing safety risks; endangering current and future patients |

## Category 3: Marketing & Influence

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PH-3.1 | **Direct-to-Consumer Disease Awareness Advertising** | Running campaigns that medicalize normal conditions to drive demand for branded treatments | Creating false health anxiety; pathologizing normal experience |
| PH-3.2 | **Ghostwritten Studies**          | Commissioning ghostwritten clinical or review articles attributed to academic authors to lend credibility to product claims | Scientific fraud; deceptive authorship |
| PH-3.3 | **Kickbacks to Prescribers**     | Providing payments, speaking fees, travel, or gifts to influence prescribing behavior | Bribery; corrupting medical decision-making |
| PH-3.4 | **Off-Label Promotion**          | Marketing drugs for unapproved uses without sufficient clinical evidence, exposing patients to unvetted risks | Patient endangerment; regulatory evasion |
| PH-3.5 | **Key Opinion Leader Capture**    | Cultivating and compensating influential physicians to serve as de facto spokespeople while obscuring financial relationships | Undisclosed conflict of interest; deceptive influence |

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PH-4.1 | **Genetic Data Monetization**    | Selling or licensing genetic data collected from trial participants or patients for secondary commercial use without explicit consent | Privacy violation; exploitation of sensitive biological data |
| PH-4.2 | **Trial Participant Data Retention Beyond Consent** | Retaining identifiable participant data beyond the consented period or using it for unstated secondary purposes | Consent violation; data overreach     |
| PH-4.3 | **Breach Notification Delay**   | Delaying notification to participants and regulators when personal health or genetic data is compromised | Concealing harm; preventing affected individuals from taking protective action |
| PH-4.4 | **Cross-Border Data Exploitation** | Transferring clinical trial data to jurisdictions with weaker privacy protections to avoid regulatory constraints | Regulatory arbitrage; circumventing participant protections |

## Category 5: Safety & Quality

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PH-5.1 | **Manufacturing Quality Degradation** | Reducing manufacturing standards or oversight to cut costs, particularly for generic or established products | Patient safety risk; regulatory violation |
| PH-5.2 | **Contamination Concealment**    | Failing to promptly disclose manufacturing contamination or quality failures to regulators and the public | Concealing known danger; endangering patients |
| PH-5.3 | **Fast-Track Drug Safety Gap**   | Leveraging expedited approval pathways without committing to or completing required post-marketing safety studies | Prioritizing speed over safety; incomplete efficacy and safety data |
| PH-5.4 | **Recall Reluctance**           | Delaying voluntary product recalls when internal data indicates safety concerns, pending regulatory enforcement | Endangering patients; evading accountability |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Commit to transparent pricing** — publish list prices and justification for price increases above inflation; disclose orphan drug designation usage and associated pricing.
2. **Adopt open clinical trial reporting** — register all trials publicly; post results within 12 months of completion regardless of outcome; submit all adverse event data to regulators without delay.
3. **Disclose all financial relationships** — publicly report all payments to healthcare professionals, academic institutions, and patient advocacy groups.
4. **Implement data sovereignty controls** — obtain explicit, separate consent for secondary use of genetic and trial data; notify affected individuals of breaches within 72 hours.
5. **Establish independent safety oversight** — empower internal safety committees with authority to halt distribution and initiate recalls independent of commercial leadership.
