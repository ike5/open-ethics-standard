# Energy & Utilities — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Energy & Utilities (Electric, Gas, Water Utilities)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to electric, gas, and water utilities and energy service providers. Utilities provide essential services that no household can forgo; unethical practices—regressive pricing, service shutoffs, environmental deception—directly endanger health, safety, and economic survival, disproportionately harming low-income and marginalized communities.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EU-1.1 | **Rate Structure Complexity**      | Designing tariff structures with multiple tiers, demand charges, and time-of-use rates so complex that consumers cannot predict or control their bills | Obfuscatory billing                   |
| EU-1.2 | **Regressive Fixed Charges**      | Increasing fixed monthly charges that consume a larger share of low-usage and low-income customers' bills, reducing the benefit of conservation | Disproportionate burden on low-income  |
| EU-1.3 | **Peak Pricing Without Smart Meter Opt-In** | Imposing mandatory time-of-use or dynamic pricing without providing smart meters or offering opt-in alternatives, penalizing customers who cannot shift usage | Mandatory pricing exposure            |
| EU-1.4 | **Estimated Billing Overcharges** | Systematically estimating bills on the high side rather than based on actual meter readings, requiring customers to identify and dispute overcharges | Systematic overcollection              |
| EU-1.5 | **Deposit Requirements Targeting Vulnerable Customers** | Requiring disproportionately high deposits from customers with low credit scores or prior arrears, blocking service access for those least able to pay | Barrier to essential service          |

## Category 2: Service Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EU-2.1 | **Shutoffs Without Adequate Notice** | Disconnecting electric, gas, or water service with insufficient notice, no grace period, or during extreme weather | Endangering health and life           |
| EU-2.2 | **Medical Baseline Denial**        | Rejecting or delaying medical baseline allowances that provide discounted rates for households with life-sustaining medical equipment | Endangering medically vulnerable      |
| EU-2.3 | **Utility Monopoly Abuse**         | Leveraging monopoly position to resist competition, block community choice aggregation, or prevent customer-owned generation | Anti-competitive; consumer lock-in    |
| EU-2.4 | **Disproportionate Shutoff Impact** | Disconnecting service in predominantly low-income or minority neighborhoods at higher rates without offering adequate payment plan alternatives | Discriminatory enforcement            |
| EU-2.5 | **Reconnection Fee Exploitation** | Charging high reconnection fees and requiring full balance payment before restoring service after shutoff | Profiting from hardship               |

## Category 3: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EU-3.1 | **Green Tariff Greenwashing**      | Marketing renewable energy tariffs that do not result in additional renewable generation, relying on existing hydro or repurchased certificates | Deceptive environmental claims        |
| EU-3.2 | **Renewable Certificate Double-Counting** | Selling the same renewable energy certificates to both retail green tariff customers and wholesale compliance markets | Double-selling environmental attributes |
| EU-3.3 | **Fossil Fuel Investment Concealment** | Lobbying against clean energy policy while publicly promoting sustainability commitments, without disclosing the gap between rhetoric and capital allocation | Deceptive public positioning          |
| EU-3.4 | **Emission Reporting Manipulation** | Underreporting methane leaks, line losses, or generation emissions through favorable accounting methods or selective monitoring | Environmental accounting fraud        |

## Category 4: Infrastructure

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EU-4.1 | **Grid Modernization Lag**         | Delaying investment in grid upgrades, storage, and distributed generation interconnection despite collecting modernization surcharges from ratepayers | Collecting fees without delivering     |
| EU-4.2 | **Safety Deferment**               | Postponing infrastructure maintenance and safety upgrades to protect short-term financial returns, increasing risk of outages, fires, or explosions | Public safety negligence              |
| EU-4.3 | **Smart Meter Surveillance**       | Collecting granular energy usage data through smart meters—revealing occupancy, appliance use, and daily patterns—without transparent data governance | Surveillance overreach; privacy violation |
| EU-4.4 | **Reliability Cost Shifting**      | Blaming outage costs on ratepayers through rate cases while failing to invest in vegetation management, line hardening, or backup systems | Cost externalization                  |

## Category 5: Community Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EU-5.1 | **Community Solar Manipulation**   | Designing community solar programs that provide minimal bill credits, impose long lock-in periods, or assign low-income subscribers to underperforming projects | Exploiting clean energy demand        |
| EU-5.2 | **Low-Income Program Barriers**    | Creating energy assistance programs with onerous application requirements, narrow eligibility windows, or inadequate outreach to the communities that need them | Systemic exclusion from benefits      |
| EU-5.3 | **Political Lobbying Against Distributed Generation** | Funding opposition to rooftop solar, battery storage, or net metering policies that would reduce customer dependence on utility service | Anti-competitive; anti-democratic     |
| EU-5.4 | **Siting Environmental Injustice**  | Concentrating new infrastructure—power plants, substations, pipelines—in low-income or minority communities without equitable community benefit agreements | Environmental racism; inequitable burden |
| EU-5.5 | **Public Comment Suppression**      | Using legal maneuvers, complex filing requirements, or packed hearing schedules to limit meaningful public participation in rate cases and siting decisions | Undermining democratic participation   |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Ensure affordable access** — maintain lifeline rates, medical baseline programs, and prohibit shutoffs during extreme weather or before adequate notice and payment plan options are offered.
2. **Simplify pricing** — provide clear, plain-language rate summaries; offer fixed-rate options where smart meter opt-in is not available; eliminate estimated billing overcharges.
3. **Substantiate environmental claims** — ensure green tariffs fund additional renewable generation; prohibit double-counting of certificates; disclose fossil fuel investment percentages annually.
4. **Protect smart meter data** — collect granular usage data only with opt-in consent; prohibit sale to third parties; provide customers with real-time access to their own data.
5. **Invest in equity** — remove barriers to low-income assistance programs; support community solar with fair credit structures; publish annual reports on shutoff demographics and restoration timelines.
