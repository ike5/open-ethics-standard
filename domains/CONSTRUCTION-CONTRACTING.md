# Construction & Contracting — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Construction & Contracting (Builders, General Contractors, Subcontractors)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to construction firms, general contractors, subcontractors, and developers. Construction is a high-value, high-risk industry where unethical practices compromise structural safety, exploit vulnerable workers, and leave property owners financially exposed on projects that define communities for decades.

---

## Category 1: Bidding & Contracts

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CO-1.1 | **Bid Shopping**                  | Sharing a contractor's bid with competitors to drive down prices, or soliciting bids with no intent to award in good faith | Undermining fair competition; bid exploitation |
| CO-1.2 | **Change Order Abuse**            | Deliberately underbidding the base contract with the intention of recovering profit through excessive or unjustified change orders once the project is underway | Bait-and-switch; contract manipulation |
| CO-1.3 | **Phantom Lowball Bidding**       | Submitting unrealistically low bids to win contracts, then cutting corners, using substandard materials, or inflating change orders to recover margins | Fraudulent bidding; quality compromise |
| CO-1.4 | **Unilateral Contract Terms**     | Imposing one-sided contract clauses that shift all risk to property owners or subcontractors, including unlimited indemnification and forced arbitration | Power imbalance exploitation          |
| CO-1.5 | **Bid Suppression**               | Coordinating with competitors to refrain from bidding or to submit intentionally non-competitive proposals, ensuring a predetermined winner | Collusion; market rigging            |

## Category 2: Labor Practices

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CO-2.1 | **Misclassification of Workers as Contractors** | Classifying employees as independent contractors to avoid paying benefits, workers' compensation, overtime, and payroll taxes | Wage theft; benefit denial; tax evasion |
| CO-2.2 | **Safety Violation Concealment**  | Hiding or failing to report workplace injuries, OSHA violations, or hazardous conditions to avoid fines, delays, or premium increases | Endangering worker safety; regulatory evasion |
| CO-2.3 | **Wage Theft on Public Projects** | Failing to pay prevailing wages, withholding final paychecks, or deducting unauthorized amounts from worker pay on taxpayer-funded projects | Public fund fraud; worker exploitation |
| CO-2.4 | **Subcontractor Non-Payment**     | Withholding or delaying payment to subcontractors and suppliers to improve cash flow, using retainage as leverage | Supply chain exploitation; financial coercion |
| CO-2.5 | **Migrant Worker Exploitation**  | Recruiting migrant or temporary workers through labor brokers who charge fees, confiscate documents, or enforce substandard living and working conditions | Human trafficking indicators; labor abuse |

## Category 3: Material & Quality

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CO-3.1 | **Substandard Material Substitution** | Replacing specified materials with cheaper, inferior alternatives without disclosure or engineering approval, compromising structural integrity | Safety compromise; contract fraud    |
| CO-3.2 | **Code Compliance Shortcuts**     | Performing work that does not meet building code requirements, relying on overburdened inspectors to miss deficiencies or on post-insulation concealment | Regulatory evasion; safety risk     |
| CO-3.3 | **Inspection Bribery**           | Offering payments, gifts, or favors to building inspectors to overlook deficiencies or approve work that does not meet code | Corruption; public safety endangerment |
| CO-3.4 | **Counterfeit Material Use**     | Installing counterfeit or uncertified building materials (rebar, fasteners, fireproofing) with falsified mill certifications | Life safety fraud                    |
| CO-3.5 | **Structural Engineering Bypass** | Modifying structural designs or load calculations without engineer approval to reduce material costs or accelerate schedules | Engineering integrity violation     |

## Category 4: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CO-4.1 | **Illegal Dumping**               | Disposing of construction debris, hazardous materials, or contaminated soil at unpermitted sites to avoid disposal fees | Environmental violation; public health risk |
| CO-4.2 | **Asbestos Removal Negligence**   | Performing asbestos abatement without proper containment, worker protection, or disposal procedures, exposing workers and occupants to carcinogenic fibers | Health endangerment; regulatory violation |
| CO-4.3 | **LEED Certification Fraud**      | Falsifying documentation or misrepresenting building features to achieve LEED or green building certifications that have not been genuinely earned | Greenwashing; certification fraud    |
| CO-4.4 | **Stormwater & Runoff Violations** | Failing to implement required erosion and sediment controls, allowing contaminated runoff into waterways and storm systems during construction | Water contamination; regulatory negligence |

---

## Category 5: Consumer Protection

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CO-5.1 | **Project Abandonment**           | Walking away from projects after receiving substantial payments, leaving owners with unfinished structures and no realistic recourse | Consumer fraud; financial devastation |
| CO-5.2 | **Lien Abuse**                    | Filing mechanic's liens punitively or prematurely to pressure owners into paying disputed or unjustified amounts, leveraging the threat to property title | Coercive collection; property threat |
| CO-5.3 | **Warranty Evasion**              | Using corporate restructuring, shell entities, or vague contract language to avoid honoring construction warranties and defect obligations | Accountability avoidance; consumer harm |
| CO-5.4 | **Scope Creep Without Consent**  | Expanding project scope or adding work without written change orders or owner approval, then billing for unauthorized additions | Unauthorized billing; contract breach |

---

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Compete fairly** — prohibit bid shopping and collusion; bid realistically with transparent change order processes; use balanced contract terms that distribute risk appropriately.
2. **Protect workers** — classify workers correctly; report all workplace injuries; pay prevailing wages on public projects; pay subcontractors within contract terms.
3. **Ensure material integrity** — use specified materials with traceable documentation; welcome independent inspections; reject counterfeit supply chains.
4. **Respect environmental obligations** — comply fully with disposal, asbestos, and stormwater regulations; pursue genuine sustainability certifications with verified documentation.
5. **Honor consumer commitments** — complete contracted work; resolve disputes through fair processes; honor warranty obligations without entity gamesmanship; obtain written consent for scope changes.
