# Oil & Gas Extraction & Refining — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Oil & Gas Extraction & Refining  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to oil and gas companies involved in exploration, extraction, refining, transport, and distribution. The fossil fuel industry operates at the intersection of environmental stewardship, community welfare, and global climate stability: unethical practices cause irreversible ecological damage, displace communities, and undermine the transition to a sustainable energy future.

---

## Category 1: Environmental Impact

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| OG-1.1 | **Fracking Fluid Disclosure Refusal** | Withholding the chemical composition of hydraulic fracturing fluids from regulators and affected communities | Concealing environmental and health risks; denying right to know |
| OG-1.2 | **Methane Emission Underreporting** | Systematically underreporting methane emissions to regulators and investors through selective measurement or accounting methods | Climate deception; regulatory evasion |
| OG-1.3 | **Oil Spill Response Delay**     | Delaying containment, cleanup, or public notification after spills to minimize perceived liability | Endangering ecosystems and communities; concealing harm |
| OG-1.4 | **Produced Water Contamination** | Discharging or improperly storing produced water containing toxic substances, contaminating groundwater and soil | Environmental degradation; public health risk |
| OG-1.5 | **Site Abandonment Without Remediation** | Decommissioning wells and facilities without adequate environmental remediation, leaving contamination for public entities to address | Externalizing cleanup costs; community abandonment |

## Category 2: Climate & Transition

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| OG-2.1 | **Greenwashing Fossil Investments** | Marketing fossil fuel projects or minimal green initiatives as major climate commitments while continuing expansion | Deceptive communication; undermining climate action |
| OG-2.2 | **Lobbying Against Climate Policy** | Funding or directing lobbying efforts to obstruct, weaken, or delay climate legislation and regulation while publicly claiming climate commitment | Hypocritical obstruction; climate disinformation |
| OG-2.3 | **Stranded Asset Concealment from Investors** | Failing to disclose climate-related financial risks and potential asset stranding in financial reporting | Investor deception; fiduciary failure |
| OG-2.4 | **Community Transition Failure** | Withdrawing operations from communities without investing in economic diversification, retraining, or transition support | Abandoning communities dependent on industry |

## Category 3: Community Impact

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| OG-3.1 | **Forced Displacement**          | Acquiring land through coercion, inadequate compensation, or legal pressure, displacing communities for extraction or pipeline projects | Violation of property and human rights; community destruction |
| OG-3.2 | **Indigenous Rights Violation**   | Proceeding with projects on indigenous lands without free, prior, and informed consent | Violation of sovereignty; cultural destruction |
| OG-3.3 | **Health Impact Concealment**    | Suppressing or failing to investigate evidence of health impacts on nearby communities from emissions, flaring, or contamination | Endangering public health; concealing known harm |
| OG-3.4 | **Community Benefit Agreement Failure** | Failing to deliver on negotiated community benefit agreements after securing project approvals | Breach of good faith; exploiting community trust |
| OG-3.5 | **Water Rights Appropriation**   | Securing disproportionate water access for operations at the expense of community drinking water and agricultural needs | Resource exploitation; threatening water security |

## Category 4: Labor & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| OG-4.1 | **Worker Safety Violation Concealment** | Underreporting workplace injuries, near-misses, or fatalities to maintain safety records and avoid regulatory scrutiny | Concealing danger; endangering workers |
| OG-4.2 | **Contractor Misclassification** | Classifying workers as independent contractors to avoid safety obligations, benefits, and liability | Labor exploitation; evading safety and benefit obligations |
| OG-4.3 | **Chemical Exposure Non-Disclosure** | Failing to inform workers of chemical exposure risks or denying access to exposure monitoring data | Withholding critical safety information; endangering health |
| OG-4.4 | **Fatigue Risk Normalization**   | Scheduling practices that create chronic fatigue risk in safety-critical operations without adequate mitigation | Endangering workers and communities through impaired operations |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Disclose all operational emissions** — publish verified methane and CO₂ emissions data using independent third-party measurement, updated annually.
2. **Publish chemical disclosure** — fully disclose all chemicals used in extraction processes, including fracking fluid compositions, to regulators and affected communities.
3. **Commit to free, prior, and informed consent** — obtain documented consent from indigenous communities before project initiation; publish community benefit agreement progress annually.
4. **Implement transparent safety reporting** — report all workplace injuries, near-misses, and fatalities to a publicly accessible database; provide workers real-time access to exposure monitoring data.
5. **Adopt transition planning** — publish climate transition plans with measurable milestones; disclose stranded asset risk in financial filings; fund community transition programs proportional to operational footprint.
