# Consulting & Management Advisory — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Consulting & Management Advisory  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to management consulting firms, strategy advisors, and advisory service providers. Consulting firms occupy a privileged position of influence: they shape major strategic and operational decisions for corporations, governments, and institutions while operating behind confidentiality walls that resist scrutiny. Unethical practices redirect client resources, create dependencies, and amplify harms across entire sectors through cascading advice.

---

## Category 1: Conflicts of Interest

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CN-1.1 | **Simultaneous Advising of Competing Clients** | Serving direct competitors in the same market on the same strategic question without disclosure and informed consent | Conflict of interest; betraying client trust |
| CN-1.2 | **Recommending Own Implementation Services** | Designing recommendations that require the firm's implementation services, creating self-referential revenue | Self-dealing; corrupting advisory objectivity |
| CN-1.3 | **Equity Stakes in Recommended Vendors** | Holding undisclosed financial interests in technology vendors, platforms, or service providers recommended to clients | Undisclosed self-enrichment; compromised advice |
| CN-1.4 | **Government Advisory to Regulated Industry** | Advising government on policy formulation while simultaneously advising regulated clients on navigating that same policy | Dual loyalty; regulatory capture      |
| CN-1.5 | **Investment Conflicts**          | Recommending strategic actions that benefit the firm's internal investment arm or portfolio companies | Self-dealing; misaligned incentives    |

## Category 2: Value Delivery

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CN-2.1 | **Scope Creep Without Value Addition** | Expanding engagement scope and billing beyond agreed deliverables without proportional client benefit | Revenue extraction; exploiting client trust |
| CN-2.2 | **Recycled Generic Frameworks**  | Presenting recycled, off-the-shelf frameworks and playbooks as bespoke, client-specific analysis | Misrepresentation; selling generic work as custom insight |
| CN-2.3 | **Deliverable Opacity**           | Delivering reports and recommendations without transparent methodology, data sources, or assumptions, preventing client verification | Withholding accountability; preventing informed challenge |
| CN-2.4 | **Implementation Failure Distance** | Designing strategies that are impractical to implement, then blaming the client for execution failure | Avoiding accountability; shifting risk to client |
| CN-2.5 | **Success Metric Manipulation**  | Defining engagement success metrics after outcomes are known, or choosing metrics that flatter the engagement regardless of client impact | Misrepresenting value; retrospective goal-setting |

## Category 3: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CN-3.1 | **Billable Hour Inflation**       | Padding billable hours through unnecessary meetings, research, or iteration to maximize revenue under time-based billing | Billing fraud; exploiting fee structure |
| CN-3.2 | **Junior Billing at Senior Rates** | Assigning junior staff to execution work while billing at senior or partner rates without disclosure | Rate misrepresentation; overcharging  |
| CN-3.3 | **Outcome-Independent Pricing**   | Pricing engagements without any linkage to client outcomes, removing firm accountability for advice quality | Misaligned incentives; no accountability for results |
| CN-3.4 | **Expenses Padding**             | Inflating travel, accommodation, and operational expenses passed through to clients beyond actual costs | Expense fraud; hidden cost inflation   |
| CN-3.5 | **Fee Escalation Without Scope Change** | Raising fees mid-engagement without corresponding scope or value changes, leveraging client switching costs | Exploiting client lock-in; coercive pricing |

## Category 4: Data & Confidentiality

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CN-4.1 | **Using One Client's Data to Serve Competitors** | Leveraging confidential data, patterns, or insights from one client to advise or benefit competitors | Breach of confidentiality; competitive harm |
| CN-4.2 | **Proprietary Methodology Claims on Public Knowledge** | Claiming proprietary ownership over methodologies, frameworks, or benchmarks built from public domain knowledge or aggregated client data | Intellectual overreach; misleading claims of originality |
| CN-4.3 | **NDAs That Silence Wrongdoing**  | Using non-disclosure agreements to prevent clients from reporting unethical, illegal, or harmful practices encountered during engagements | Suppressing accountability; obstructing justice |
| CN-4.4 | **Data Retention Beyond Engagement** | Retaining client data, models, and deliverables after engagement completion for reuse or competitive advantage | Data overreach; exceeding consented use |

## Category 5: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CN-5.1 | **Up-or-Out Culture Burnout**     | Maintaining up-or-out promotion systems that demand unsustainable hours, normalizing burnout and mental health deterioration as conditions of employment | Systemic exploitation; endangering worker health |
| CN-5.2 | **H1-B Dependency for Cost Arbitrage** | Structuring staffing around H1-B visa holders to achieve cost savings through visa-dependent workers unable to negotiate or leave | Labor exploitation; immigration status coercion |
| CN-5.3 | **Intern and Analyst Exploitation** | Expecting unpaid or underpaid interns and junior analysts to work extended hours on client billable work without fair compensation or development opportunity | Unpaid labor extraction; exploiting career aspiration |
| CN-5.4 | **Non-Compete Overreach**         | Imposing overly broad non-compete and non-solicitation agreements that restrict career mobility beyond legitimate business interests | Restricting labor freedom; coercive employment terms |
| CN-5.5 | **Performance Review Weaponization** | Using opaque performance review processes to justify predetermined attrition targets rather than genuine development | Manipulative employment practices; manufacturing justification for dismissal |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Disclose all conflicts** — publish a conflicts register identifying simultaneous client relationships in the same sector; obtain informed written consent before accepting potentially conflicting engagements.
2. **Tie pricing to value** — offer outcome-linked pricing options alongside traditional models; disclose staffing levels and billing rates by seniority; prohibit expense padding through published expense policies.
3. **Protect client data** — destroy client data within 90 days of engagement completion unless legally required; prohibit cross-client data reuse without explicit consent; audit internal data access annually.
4. **Adopt ethical NDA standards** — ensure NDAs do not prevent reporting of illegal activity or regulatory cooperation; publish a commitment to not enforce NDAs that suppress lawful whistleblowing.
5. **Reform labor practices** — cap weekly billable expectations at sustainable levels; provide transparent promotion criteria; eliminate non-competes for non-partner employees; audit H1-B practices for equitable treatment.
