# Renewable Energy — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Renewable Energy  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to renewable energy developers, utilities, offset providers, and clean technology manufacturers. The renewable energy sector carries a social license built on environmental responsibility; ethical violations here not only harm communities and workers but also undermine public trust in the energy transition itself.

---

## Category 1: Greenwashing

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RN-1.1 | **Overstating Renewable Content** | Claiming higher percentages of renewable energy in supply than verified generation or contractual entitlements support | Misleading consumers about environmental impact |
| RN-1.2 | **REC Double-Counting**          | Counting the same Renewable Energy Certificate toward both regulatory compliance and voluntary customer claims | Fraudulently inflating green credentials |
| RN-1.3 | **Offset Reliance Instead of Reduction** | Marketing "net-zero" or "carbon neutral" status primarily through purchased offsets rather than genuine emissions reduction | Shifting responsibility; delaying real decarbonization |
| RN-1.4 | **Fossil Fuel Portfolio Concealment** | Promoting renewable offerings while the parent company's generation portfolio remains majority fossil-fuel-based | Creating a misleading corporate sustainability image |
| RN-1.5 | **Future Pledge Misrepresentation** | Announcing distant net-zero targets without interim milestones, accountability mechanisms, or capital allocation plans | Making commitments that lack enforceable delivery paths |

## Category 2: Land & Community

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RN-2.1 | **Indigenous Land Rights Violation** | Developing renewable projects on or adjacent to indigenous territories without free, prior, and informed consent | Violating sovereignty and cultural rights |
| RN-2.2 | **Community Benefit Agreement Failure** | Promising local jobs, tax revenue, or infrastructure improvements during permitting and then failing to deliver | Exploiting community goodwill for project approval |
| RN-2.3 | **NIMBY Exploitation**           | Siting projects in low-income or minority communities with less political resistance while avoiding affluent opposition | Environmental justice violation; disproportionate burden |
| RN-2.4 | **Agricultural Land Displacement** | Converting productive farmland to solar or wind installations without food-security impact assessment | Undermining local food systems for energy development |
| RN-2.5 | **Noise and Visual Impact Minimization** | Downplaying or concealing projected noise, shadow flicker, or visual impacts on nearby residents during permitting | Withholding information needed for informed community consent |

## Category 3: Supply Chain

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RN-3.1 | **Rare Earth Mining Abuse**      | Sourcing materials from mines with documented child labor, forced labor, or toxic environmental practices | Enabling human rights violations through supply chain neglect |
| RN-3.2 | **Panel Manufacturing Labor Conditions** | Procuring from factories with unsafe working conditions, excessive hours, or wage theft | Complicity in worker exploitation |
| RN-3.3 | **End-of-Life Panel Disposal**   | Failing to plan for or fund the recycling and safe disposal of decommissioned panels, blades, or batteries | Creating future environmental liabilities |
| RN-3.4 | **Cobalt and Lithium Sourcing**  | Purchasing battery materials without tracing origin or verifying compliance with labor and environmental standards | Willful blindness to extraction harms |
| RN-3.5 | **Supply Chain Audit Obstruction** | Limiting audit scope, providing advance notice, or selecting auditors with conflicts of interest | Undermining verification of ethical sourcing claims |

## Category 4: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RN-4.1 | **PPA Complexity**               | Structuring Power Purchase Agreements with embedded escalators, obscure fees, or termination penalties that obscure true long-term costs | Hiding costs in contract complexity |
| RN-4.2 | **Rate Escalation Hidden Terms**  | Including annual rate increases or demand charges in fine print rather than upfront pricing disclosures | Exploiting information asymmetry in long-term contracts |
| RN-4.3 | **Community Solar Lock-In**       | Binding subscribers to long community solar contracts with steep early exit fees and limited transfer options | Restricting consumer freedom; trapping participants |
| RN-4.4 | **Net Metering Credit Reduction** | Adjusting net metering credit rates downward after installation without grandfathering existing customers | Bait-and-switch on investment return expectations |
| RN-4.5 | **Add-On Fee Stacking**           | Adding grid connection, metering, or administrative fees that erode advertised savings | Misleading total cost of adoption |

## Category 5: Grid & Storage

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RN-5.1 | **Battery Sourcing Ethics**       | Deploying grid-scale storage using batteries with unverified supply chain labor and environmental practices | Shifting ethical burden downstream |
| RN-5.2 | **Grid Reliability Claims**       | Advertising renewable or storage solutions as baseload-equivalent without disclosing intermittency or backup requirements | Misrepresenting technical capabilities |
| RN-5.3 | **Curtailment Concealment**       | Failing to disclose when renewable generation is curtailed due to grid constraints, effectively overstating actual clean energy delivered | Inflating environmental benefit claims |
| RN-5.4 | **Fire and Safety Risk Opacity**  | Underreporting or downplaying battery fire risks, chemical exposure, or thermal runaway incidents at storage facilities | Concealing safety information from communities |
| RN-5.5 | **Grid Interconnection Delay Exploitation** | Using interconnection queue delays strategically to block competitor projects while advancing own portfolio | Anti-competitive manipulation of grid access |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Verify green claims independently** — obtain third-party certification for renewable content claims; publish REC and offset registries with unique serial numbers.
2. **Obtain free, prior, and informed consent** — engage indigenous communities before site selection; document consent processes; halt development where consent is withheld.
3. **Audit supply chains annually** — require supplier compliance with ILO core conventions; publish audit summaries including findings and remediation actions.
4. **Simplify pricing disclosures** — provide PPA cost calculators; disclose all fees, escalators, and termination terms in plain-language summaries; offer standard-form contracts.
5. **Plan for end-of-life** — fund decommissioning and recycling programs at project inception; publish panel, blade, and battery disposal plans.
6. **Report actual generation** — publish curtailment data alongside rated capacity; disclose grid reliability limitations in marketing materials.
