# Domain: Mobile Carriers & Telecom

**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This standard defines ethical practices and violations within mobile carrier and telecommunications services, covering pricing integrity, data stewardship, coverage honesty, device subsidy fairness, and consumer rights. It addresses practices that exploit customer lock-in, obscure true costs, misuse communications data, misrepresent service availability, and obstruct customer autonomy. Organizations operating under this domain standard commit to transparent, fair, and customer-respecting telecommunications services.

---

## Pricing

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| MC-1.1 | Device financing transparency | Clearly disclose total device cost, financing terms, interest rates, and early payoff conditions in all promotional materials | Designing device financing plans with hidden interest, balloon payments, or penalties that lock customers into long-term obligations exceeding the device's value |
| MC-1.2 | Plan clarity and comparability | Present service plans with standardized pricing components that enable straightforward comparison across plans and carriers | Creating deliberately complex plan structures—layered data buckets, mixed-unit throttling thresholds, and opaque surcharges—that prevent customers from comparing true costs |
| MC-1.3 | Bill cramming prohibition | Ensure all charges on customer bills are for services explicitly authorized by the customer | Adding unauthorized charges—third-party services, premium SMS, or mystery fees—to customer bills without clear opt-in consent |
| MC-1.4 | Promotional rate honesty | Disclose the full-term cost of promotional rates, including the post-promotional regular price and the timing of rate increases | Advertising low promotional rates without equally prominent disclosure of the regular price and date the rate increases |
| MC-1.5 | Fee disclosure completeness | Disclose all mandatory and optional fees—activation, regulatory, administrative—before service commitment | Concealing mandatory fees until after the customer has committed, or presenting optional fees as required |

## Data

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| MC-2.1 | Metadata retention limits | Retain customer metadata—call records, cell tower data, IP assignments—only as long as necessary for service delivery or legal obligation | Retaining and selling customer metadata—including location, call patterns, and browsing metadata—to third parties for commercial purposes without explicit informed consent |
| MC-2.2 | DNS integrity | Resolve DNS queries faithfully without redirecting, modifying, or injecting responses for advertising or tracking purposes | Hijacking DNS queries to inject ads, redirect mistyped URLs to advertising pages, or modify resolution for carrier revenue |
| MC-2.3 | Bandwidth throttling honesty | Disclose all throttling policies clearly, including thresholds, speeds, and circumstances, before customer commitment | Throttling bandwidth during peak usage, for specific applications, or after undisclosed data thresholds while marketing plans as unlimited |
| MC-2.4 | Data breach notification | Notify affected customers promptly of any breach involving personal data, call records, or account information | Failing to notify customers of data breaches involving their call records, location data, or account information in a timely manner |
| MC-2.5 | Third-party data sharing consent | Obtain explicit opt-in consent before sharing any customer data with third parties for marketing, analytics, or profiling | Sharing customer usage data, location history, or account information with advertisers, data brokers, or analytics firms without explicit customer consent |

## Coverage

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| MC-3.1 | Coverage map accuracy | Publish coverage maps based on verified, current measurement data with clear methodology disclosures | Overstating coverage areas on maps without current measurement data, or using aspirational rather than actual coverage projections |
| MC-3.2 | Rural service commitment | Maintain and invest in rural and underserved area coverage proportionate to customer base and spectrum obligations | Neglecting rural network investment while concentrating upgrades exclusively in profitable urban markets, widening the digital divide |
| MC-3.3 | Roaming fee fairness | Disclose roaming rates clearly before travel and offer reasonable roaming packages for common travel destinations | Exploiting roaming fees with exorbitant per-MB rates, inadequate roaming packages, or failure to notify customers of roaming charges in real time |
| MC-3.4 | Outage transparency | Report network outages promptly and accurately, including expected restoration timelines and alternative service options | Concealing or minimizing network outages, failing to report outage scope, or providing inaccurate restoration estimates |
| MC-3.5 | Service level commitments | Define and publish realistic service level expectations for speed, latency, and reliability by area type | Marketing service levels that are achievable only under ideal conditions that do not reflect typical customer experience |

## Device Subsidy

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| MC-4.1 | Upgrade cycle honesty | Provide device upgrade recommendations based on the customer's actual needs and device condition rather than artificial replacement pressure | Creating upgrade trap cycles through software slowdowns, premature obsolescence messaging, or upgrade pressure tactics that push customers to replace functional devices |
| MC-4.2 | Trade-in valuation fairness | Offer trade-in valuations based on objective device condition assessments with transparent pricing methodology | Lowballing trade-in valuations below fair market value, applying arbitrary condition deductions, or offering promotional credits that expire before customers can use them |
| MC-4.3 | Carrier unlocking timeliness | Unlock devices promptly upon contract fulfillment or device payoff, without requiring additional fees or excessive processing time | Maintaining carrier locks beyond the contract period, imposing excessive unlock fees, or creating bureaucratic obstacles to device unlocking after contract completion |
| MC-4.4 | Device compatibility disclosure | Clearly disclose any device limitations, pre-installed software, or compatibility restrictions before sale | Selling devices with undisclosed carrier-specific restrictions, pre-installed bloatware, or compatibility limitations that reduce device functionality or portability |

## Consumer Rights

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| MC-5.1 | Contract renewal consent | Require explicit customer consent for contract auto-renewals with advance notification of terms and the right to decline | Auto-renewing contracts without meaningful notification, changing terms during renewal, or making cancellation impractical through complex procedures |
| MC-5.2 | Early termination fee proportionality | Set early termination fees proportionate to the carrier's remaining device subsidy or actual cost, declining over the contract term | Scaling early termination fees to remain excessive throughout the contract term, penalizing customers far beyond the carrier's actual cost or subsidy recovery |
| MC-5.3 | Number porting facilitation | Enable seamless number porting in compliance with regulatory timelines without retention obstacles or delays | Obstructing number porting through delays, incomplete transfers, retention department pressure, or technical barriers that discourage customers from switching carriers |
| MC-5.4 | Dispute resolution accessibility | Provide accessible, timely, and fair dispute resolution channels including escalation to independent arbitration when needed | Steering customers into binding arbitration with limited discovery, imposing forum restrictions, or making complaint processes so cumbersome that customers abandon legitimate claims |
| MC-5.5 | Cooling-off period | Offer a reasonable cooling-off period after contract signing during which customers can cancel without penalty | Eliminating or failing to offer any post-sale cancellation window, locking customers into contracts before they have assessed actual service quality |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this standard should:

1. **Publish Complete Pricing Guides** — Make full rate cards, fee schedules, and financing terms publicly available in standardized formats enabling easy comparison.
2. **Conduct Data Practices Audits** — Review metadata retention, DNS handling, throttling policies, and third-party data sharing arrangements annually.
3. **Verify Coverage Claims** — Regularly test and update coverage maps with real-world measurement data, publishing methodology and confidence levels.
4. **Implement Device Fairness Policies** — Establish transparent trade-in valuation criteria, unlock timelines, and upgrade recommendation standards.
5. **Establish Customer Advocacy Channels** — Provide accessible dispute resolution, number porting support, and cancellation processes with published timelines.
6. **Train Customer-Facing Staff** — Require regular training on pricing disclosure, data consent, coverage accuracy, device terms, and consumer rights for all sales and support personnel.
