# Public Transportation & Transit — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Public Transportation & Transit  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to public transit agencies, bus and rail operators, and transportation authorities. Public transit is an essential service that determines whether people can access jobs, education, and healthcare; unethical practices disproportionately harm those with the fewest alternatives.

---

## Category 1: Access & Equity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PT-1.1 | **Service Cuts in Low-Income Areas** | Reducing service frequency or routes in communities that depend on transit most while maintaining service in affluent areas | Transit equity violation              |
| PT-1.2 | **Disability Accommodation Failures** | Failing to maintain elevators, provide working audio announcements, or ensure vehicle accessibility on advertised routes | ADA violation; exclusion of disabled riders |
| PT-1.3 | **Fare Increases Without Service Improvement** | Raising fares without corresponding service upgrades, effectively taxing transit-dependent riders | Regressive pricing                    |
| PT-1.4 | **Language Barrier Exclusion**     | Providing service information, safety announcements, and alerts only in English in multilingual communities | Excluding non-English speakers from critical information |
| PT-1.5 | **Last-Mile Coverage Gaps**       | Discontinuing feeder routes and last-mile connections that complete transit trips for suburban and rural riders | Creating transit deserts              |

## Category 2: Safety & Maintenance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PT-2.1 | **Deferred Infrastructure Maintenance** | Postponing track, signal, and vehicle maintenance to balance budgets, increasing derailment and breakdown risk | Safety deferred for cost savings      |
| PT-2.2 | **Operator Fatigue & Overwork**    | Scheduling operators for extended shifts with minimal recovery time between runs, increasing accident risk | Endangering riders and operators      |
| PT-2.3 | **Accessibility Feature Neglect**  | Failing to repair broken elevators, non-functioning ramps, and inoperable lift equipment for weeks or months | Stranding disabled riders             |
| PT-2.4 | **Station Security Inequality**    | Concentrating security personnel and cameras in affluent stations while leaving high-ridership low-income stations underserved | Unequal safety provision              |
| PT-2.5 | **Incident Reporting Suppression**  | Discouraging or undercounting crime and safety incidents to maintain ridership statistics | Hiding safety problems               |

## Category 3: Data & Surveillance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PT-3.1 | **Fare Card Tracking & Data Sale** | Using reloadable fare card data to track individual travel patterns and selling aggregated data without meaningful consent | Privacy violation; surveillance        |
| PT-3.2 | **Surveillance Camera Overreach**   | Deploying facial recognition and pervasive CCTV in transit systems without clear retention limits or oversight | Mass surveillance of commuters       |
| PT-3.3 | **Algorithmic Service Cuts**        | Using ridership data to justify cutting low-ridership routes, creating a downward spiral of service and ridership loss | Data-driven disinvestment             |
| PT-3.4 | **Law Enforcement Data Sharing**    | Sharing real-time location and travel data with law enforcement without warrants or transparency | Overreach; chilling effect on riders  |

## Category 4: Pricing & Fares

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PT-4.1 | **Disproportionate Fare Evasion Penalties** | Issuing criminal citations and heavy fines for fare evasion that disproportionately impact low-income riders | Criminalizing poverty                 |
| PT-4.2 | **Dynamic Pricing on Essential Transit** | Implementing surge pricing during peak hours on routes that commuters have no alternative for | Regressive pricing on necessity       |
| PT-4.3 | **Transfer Fee Exploitation**       | Charging for transfers that effectively penalize riders who must use multiple routes due to poor network design | Penalizing inadequate service         |
| PT-4.4 | **Discount Program Barriers**        | Making reduced-fare programs for seniors, students, and low-income riders difficult to apply for and maintain | Access barrier to entitled benefits   |
| PT-4.5 | **Revenue Grafting to Non-Transit** | Diverting fare revenue to non-transit purposes while cutting service and raising fares | Misuse of transit funding            |

## Category 5: Environmental & Community Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PT-5.1 | **Diesel Bus Retention in Polluted Areas** | Continuing to operate diesel buses in communities already burdened by poor air quality while electrifying affluent routes | Environmental injustice              |
| PT-5.2 | **Electric Bus Procurement Greenwashing** | Announcing electric bus purchases while delaying actual deployment and continuing diesel operations | Environmental performance deception   |
| PT-5.3 | **Construction Displacement**       | Building transit infrastructure that displaces long-term residents without adequate relocation or community benefit agreements | Community disruption without redress  |
| PT-5.4 | **Transit-Oriented Development Gentrification** | Using transit investment to justify luxury development that displaces the transit-dependent residents the system was built to serve | Displacement via transit investment   |
| PT-5.5 | **Noise & Vibration Impact Neglect** | Failing to mitigate noise and vibration from elevated rail and bus depots in residential communities | Environmental quality degradation    |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Conduct equity audits** — analyze service levels, fare burden, and accessibility by income, race, and disability status; publish results annually and adjust service accordingly.
2. **Implement zero-fare or reduced-fare programs** — provide fare-free transit for low-income riders, students, and seniors; eliminate fare evasion criminalization.
3. **Maintain accessibility standards** — repair broken accessibility features within 48 hours; provide alternative accessible transportation during outages.
4. **Protect rider privacy** — prohibit sale of fare card data; require warrants for law enforcement access; delete individual travel data within 90 days.
5. **Prioritize environmental justice** — deploy zero-emission vehicles first in communities with the worst air quality; publish fleet electrification timelines with binding commitments.
