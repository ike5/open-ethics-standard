# Defense & Military Contracting — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Defense & Military Contracting  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to defense contractors, arms manufacturers, private military companies, and firms providing goods and services to military and security forces. The defense industry occupies a unique position where unethical practices can enable armed conflict, endanger service members, and divert enormous public resources from civilian needs — all while operating behind classifications and procurements that resist public scrutiny.

---

## Category 1: Procurement & Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DM-1.1 | **Cost-Plus Contract Exploitation** | Leveraging cost-plus contract structures to inflate costs, knowing higher spending yields higher profit | Taxpayer exploitation; perverse incentives |
| DM-1.2 | **Cost Overrun Tolerance**        | Treating chronic cost overruns as routine rather than escalating, absorbing public funds without accountability | Fiscal irresponsibility; public trust violation |
| DM-1.3 | **Revolving Door Between Pentagon and Contractors** | Executives and officials cycling between government procurement roles and industry positions, creating conflicts of interest | Corrupting fair procurement; institutional capture |
| DM-1.4 | **Unnecessary System Proliferation** | Developing or promoting redundant weapons systems and platforms to sustain revenue streams regardless of strategic necessity | Wasting public resources; prioritizing revenue over defense needs |
| DM-1.5 | **Sole-Source Contract Manipulation** | Engineering requirements or timelines to justify non-competitive sole-source contracts, limiting oversight and fair pricing | Anti-competitive behavior; undermining procurement integrity |

## Category 2: Arms Sales

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DM-2.1 | **Weapons Sales to Human Rights Violators** | Selling arms to governments or entities with documented patterns of human rights abuses | Enabling repression; complicity in human rights violations |
| DM-2.2 | **End-Use Monitoring Failure**    | Failing to verify or enforce end-use restrictions, allowing weapons diversion to unauthorized parties | Enabling illicit arms flows; complicity in misuse |
| DM-2.3 | **Conflict Fueling Through Arms Proliferation** | Supplying arms to regions with active or imminent conflicts, intensifying violence and instability | Profiting from conflict; endangering civilian populations |
| DM-2.4 | **Arms Lobbying Under Humanitarian Cover** | Lobbying for arms deals using framed national security or humanitarian rationales that obscure commercial motivations | Deceptive influence; corrupting policy |
| DM-2.5 | **Autonomous Weapons Ethical Avoidance** | Developing or selling autonomous lethal systems without meaningful human control safeguards or international legal frameworks | Endangering civilians; evading accountability for lethal decisions |

## Category 3: Safety & Testing

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DM-3.1 | **Equipment Failure Concealment** | Suppressing or delaying disclosure of known equipment failures and safety defects that endanger service members | Endangering lives; prioritizing contract preservation over troop safety |
| DM-3.2 | **Rushed Testing Endangering Troops** | Delivering systems to the field with incomplete testing to meet contract milestones or political deadlines | Deploying unsafe equipment; risking service member lives |
| DM-3.3 | **Defective Body Armor Supply**  | Providing body armor or protective equipment that fails to meet established threat protection standards | Endangering service members; fraudulent specification compliance |
| DM-3.4 | **Simulation Substitution**      | Replacing live operational testing with simulations that have not been validated against real-world conditions | Misrepresenting readiness; compromising safety assurance |

## Category 4: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DM-4.1 | **Classified Budget Opacity**    | Using classification to shield programs from congressional oversight and public accountability beyond legitimate security needs | Undermining democratic oversight; enabling waste |
| DM-4.2 | **Audit Failure and Unaccountable Spending** | Failing to achieve clean financial audits while expending billions in public funds, year after year | Fiscal irresponsibility; violating public trust |
| DM-4.3 | **Lobbying Disclosure Gaps**     | Channeling influence through trade associations, think tanks, or indirect lobbying to avoid direct disclosure requirements | Circumventing transparency laws; corrupting policy |
| DM-4.4 | **Program Cost Concealment**     | Withholding or obscuring total program lifecycle costs during congressional testimony and budget requests | Misleading lawmakers; undermining informed appropriation |

## Category 5: Labor & Ethics

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DM-5.1 | **Private Military Contractor Accountability Gaps** | Operating private military forces in legal gray zones that evade military justice, host-nation law, and domestic prosecution | Impunity for misconduct; evading rule of law |
| DM-5.2 | **War Zone Labor Exploitation**  | Recruiting third-country nationals for base operations and logistics under coercive conditions with inadequate pay and protections | Labor trafficking; exploiting vulnerable workers |
| DM-5.3 | **Whistleblower Retaliation**    | Retaliating against employees who report fraud, safety failures, or legal violations through termination, revocation of clearances, or blacklisting | Silencing accountability; violating legal protections |
| DM-5.4 | **Security Clearance Coercion**  | Using security clearance requirements to suppress employee complaints, limit job mobility, or enforce silence on misconduct | Abuse of authority; restricting lawful dissent |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish pricing transparency** — disclose cost-plus margins and cost overrun data by major program; commit to independent cost estimation for programs exceeding $1 billion.
2. **Implement end-use verification** — establish and publish end-use monitoring results for all international arms sales; suspend sales where diversion is detected.
3. **Adopt human rights due diligence** — conduct and publish human rights impact assessments before arms sales to any government; refuse sales where risks are unmitigable.
4. **Establish independent safety reporting** — report all equipment failures and safety defects to a publicly accessible database within 30 days; protect whistleblowers from retaliation through binding policy.
5. **Commit to financial auditability** — achieve and maintain clean financial audit opinions; reduce classified budget compartments to the minimum necessary for legitimate security.
