# Airlines & Aviation — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Airlines & Aviation (Carriers, Operators, Booking Platforms)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to airlines, aviation operators, and related booking platforms. Air travel is an essential service where unethical practices exploit captive consumers, endanger passengers, and erode public trust in a safety-critical industry.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Fee Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AA-1.1 | **Drip Pricing on Fares**        | Advertising low base fares while progressively adding mandatory fees (seat selection, carry-on, booking) during checkout | Deliberate price obfuscation         |
| AA-1.2 | **Seat Selection Fees**           | Charging for standard seat assignment that was previously included, effectively raising the real price of travel | Hidden cost inflation                |
| AA-1.3 | **Baggage Fee Escalation**        | Raising checked and carry-on baggage fees without proportional cost justification, exploiting the inability of booked passengers to switch carriers | Exploitative pricing on essentials    |
| AA-1.4 | **Change Fee Exploitation**       | Imposing disproportionate change or cancellation fees that exceed the actual cost of rebooking | Punitive fee extraction               |

## Category 2: Customer Service & Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AA-2.1 | **Denied Boarding Compensation Avoidance** | Using loopholes, technicalities, or aggressive tactics to avoid paying legally mandated compensation for involuntary denied boarding | Denying passenger legal rights        |
| AA-2.2 | **Delay & Cancellation Opacity** | Failing to provide timely, honest explanations for delays and cancellations, or withholding information about passenger entitlements | Withholding passenger rights information |
| AA-2.3 | **Accessibility Failures for Disabled Passengers** | Failing to provide required accessibility accommodations, damaged mobility aids, or consistent service for passengers with disabilities | Discrimination; duty of care failure |
| AA-2.4 | **Overbooking Without Disclosure** | Selling more tickets than available seats without transparently disclosing overbooking policies at purchase | Concealing material risk to consumer  |

## Category 3: Data & Surveillance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AA-3.1 | **Frequent Flyer Data Monetization** | Selling or sharing loyalty program member data (travel patterns, spending habits) to third-party marketers without meaningful consent | Privacy violation; data exploitation |
| AA-3.2 | **Biometric Boarding Coercion**   | Requiring facial recognition or biometric boarding as default, making opt-out processes burdensome or unclear | Coercive surveillance; consent violation |
| AA-3.3 | **Inflight WiFi Data Harvesting**  | Collecting, analyzing, and selling browsing data, messaging metadata, and device information from passengers using inflight connectivity | Undisclosed data collection           |

## Category 4: Safety & Maintenance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AA-4.1 | **Maintenance Deferral for Schedule** | Postponing non-critical but required maintenance to preserve on-time performance metrics and avoid flight cancellations | Prioritizing metrics over safety      |
| AA-4.2 | **Safety Report Opacity**         | Withholding or minimizing internal safety reports, incident data, or whistleblower findings from regulators and the public | Suppressing safety-critical information |
| AA-4.3 | **Regulatory Lobbying Against Passenger Protections** | Lobbying against passenger rights regulations, delay compensation rules, or safety mandates using industry influence | Undermining public interest for profit |

---

## Category 5: Loyalty Program Manipulation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| AA-5.1 | **Mile Devaluation**             | Unilaterally reducing the value of accumulated miles or points, diminishing the real purchasing power of loyal customers | Unilateral contract degradation      |
| AA-5.2 | **Elite Status Erosion**          | Quietly raising qualification thresholds or removing benefits previously promised to elite-tier members | Retroactive promise breaking          |
| AA-5.3 | **Award Availability Manipulation** | Restricting the release of award seats to artificially low levels, making redemption difficult despite marketed availability | Misleading loyalty program value     |
| AA-5.4 | **Expiration Without Notice**     | Allowing miles or status to expire without adequate advance notice, especially when earned through significant spending | Forfeiture through silence            |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Enforce all-in pricing** — display the total fare including all mandatory fees at first presentation; eliminate drip pricing in booking flows.
2. **Honor passenger rights** — proactively communicate delay, cancellation, and denied boarding entitlements; provide accessible services without advance request hurdles.
3. **Protect passenger data** — require opt-in consent for biometric processing; prohibit sale of loyalty and inflight data; publish clear data retention policies.
4. **Prioritize safety transparency** — report maintenance deferrals publicly; protect safety whistleblowers; cease lobbying against established passenger protections.
5. **Maintain loyalty integrity** — give 12 months' notice before devaluing miles or status; release award seats proportionally to program size; notify members before expiration.
