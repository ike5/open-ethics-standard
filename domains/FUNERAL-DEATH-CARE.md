# Funeral & Death Care — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Funeral & Death Care Services  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to funeral homes, crematories, cemeteries, and death care service providers. The funeral industry serves people at their most vulnerable — grieving families making urgent decisions under emotional distress. This power asymmetry creates extraordinary risk for exploitation, and ethical failures in this domain compound trauma during the most difficult moments in a person's life. This standard addresses the pricing, transparency, data, and dignity practices that must govern services for the deceased and their families.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FU-1.1 | **Casket Markup Exceeding 300%** | Marking up casket and merchandise prices to three or more times wholesale cost while presenting them as standard retail pricing in a market where comparison shopping is nearly impossible | Price gouging; vulnerability exploitation |
| FU-1.2 | **Package Bundling to Prevent Comparison** | Offering only bundled service packages that mix required and optional items, making it impossible for families to compare individual service costs or decline unwanted components | Price opacity; informed consent violation |
| FU-1.3 | **Required Item Misrepresentation** | Misrepresenting state or local legal requirements to families — claiming embalming, outer burial containers, or expensive caskets are legally mandated when they are not | Deception; regulatory misrepresentation |
| FU-1.4 | **Price List Withholding**         | Refusing to provide a General Price List over the phone, online, or before an in-person meeting, forcing families into high-pressure in-person sales environments | Transparency violation; FTC non-compliance |
| FU-1.5 | **Hidden Facility and Staffing Fees** | Adding mandatory facility fees, staff charges, or overhead costs not included in quoted service prices, resulting in final bills far exceeding initial estimates | Bait-and-switch pricing; fee concealment |

## Category 2: Vulnerability Exploitation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FU-2.1 | **Upselling During Grief**        | Pressuring grieving families to upgrade caskets, vaults, services, or memorials through implied judgments about love or respect for the deceased | Emotional manipulation; exploitative sales |
| FU-2.2 | **Emotional Manipulation for Premium Services** | Using guilt, dignity language, or implied social expectations to steer families toward the most expensive options regardless of their stated budget or wishes | Coercion; dignity exploitation        |
| FU-2.3 | **Pre-Need Contract Opacity**     | Selling pre-need funeral contracts with unclear terms, hidden fees, non-guaranteed price provisions, and confusing cancellation policies that lock consumers into long-term financial obligations | Contractual deception; long-term financial entrapment |
| FU-2.4 | **Cemetery Plot Investment Misrepresentation** | Marketing cemetery plots as appreciating financial investments rather than prepaid funeral services, misleading consumers about resale value and liquidity | Financial misrepresentation; investment fraud |
| FU-2.5 | **Immediate Need Premium Pricing** | Applying surge pricing or premium rates when families contact providers after a death has occurred, exploiting the urgency and emotional state of the moment | Crisis exploitation; discriminatory pricing |

## Category 3: Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FU-3.1 | **General Price List Non-Disclosure** | Failing to proactively provide the General Price List as required by the FTC Funeral Rule, or providing it only after services have already been selected | Regulatory non-compliance; transparency violation |
| FU-3.2 | **Outer Container Requirement Confusion** | Conflating grave liners (optional for ground maintenance) with vaults (far more expensive) and implying either is legally required when the cemetery's own policy is the only mandate | Deceptive bundling; consumer confusion |
| FU-3.3 | **Embalming Requirement Misrepresentation** | Falsely stating or implying that embalming is required by law, when in reality it is rarely mandated and refrigeration or rapid disposition are alternatives | Legal misrepresentation; unnecessary service pressure |
| FU-3.4 | **Cremation Process Opacity**      | Failing to disclose the cremation process, the right to witness, the handling of personal effects, or the commingling of cremated remains, preventing informed decision-making | Process concealment; informed consent failure |
| FU-3.5 | **Third-Party Provider Markup Concealment** | Marking up third-party services — flowers, obituaries, transit permits — without disclosing that families can purchase these services independently at lower cost | Provider conflict; cost concealment    |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FU-4.1 | **Pre-Need Contract Data Sale**     | Selling personal, financial, and health information collected during pre-need arrangements to third parties including insurance companies and marketers | Privacy violation; data exploitation  |
| FU-4.2 | **Next-of-Kin Contact Sale**        | Selling or sharing contact information of bereaved family members to grief counselors, florists, monument companies, and other service providers | Contact exploitation; grief monetization |
| FU-4.3 | **Estate Information Harvesting**   | Collecting information about estate size, life insurance, and asset values during arrangement consultations to target upselling or sell to financial service providers | Financial data harvesting; conflict of interest |
| FU-4.4 | **Grief Counseling Upsell Data**    | Using information shared during vulnerable grief counseling conversations to identify additional sales opportunities or profile families for targeted marketing | Consent violation; therapeutic exploitation |

## Category 5: Dignity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FU-5.1 | **Discriminatory Pricing by Demographics** | Charging higher prices or providing inferior services to families based on race, ethnicity, neighborhood, or perceived socioeconomic status | Discrimination; equal access violation |
| FU-5.2 | **Religious Accommodation Failure** | Failing to accommodate or actively obstructing religious, cultural, or spiritual practices during funeral services, viewings, or disposition | Religious freedom violation; cultural disrespect |
| FU-5.3 | **Gender-Based Service Pricing**    | Charging different prices for identical services based on the gender of the deceased, such as different casket or preparation costs | Sex-based discrimination; dignity violation |
| FU-5.4 | **Cultural Practice Disrespect**    | Dismissing, ridiculing, or refusing to accommodate culturally specific mourning practices, body preparation rituals, or disposition preferences | Cultural insensitivity; service denial |
| FU-5.5 | **Indigent Burial Neglect**         | Providing minimal or disrespectful services for indigent or unclaimed remains, treating government-reimbursed cases as low-priority afterthoughts | Dignity violation; class-based devaluation |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Price fairly and transparently** — provide General Price Lists proactively in all channels; unbundle services so families can choose individually; never misrepresent legal requirements; disclose all fees before selection.
2. **Protect vulnerable families** — prohibit upselling tactics that exploit grief; present options at every price point without judgment; ensure pre-need contracts are clear, cancellable, and portable; market plots only as services, not investments.
3. **Ensure full transparency** — comply with all FTC Funeral Rule requirements; clearly distinguish between legal requirements and cemetery policies; disclose embalming alternatives; explain the cremation process fully and offer witnessing; disclose third-party service markups.
4. **Safeguard personal data** — prohibit the sale of pre-need contract data, next-of-kin contacts, estate information, or any data collected during grief counseling; obtain explicit, informed consent for all data sharing.
5. **Uphold dignity equally** — audit pricing for demographic bias; accommodate all religious and cultural practices; eliminate gender-based pricing disparities; provide respectful care for all remains regardless of ability to pay.
