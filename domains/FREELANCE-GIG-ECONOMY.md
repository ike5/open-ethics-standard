# Freelance & Gig Economy — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Freelance & Gig Economy (Ride-Hailing, Delivery Platforms, Freelance Marketplaces, Task Apps)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to platforms and businesses operating in the freelance and gig economy. Gig workers often lack the protections of traditional employment, and unethical practices in this domain exploit that vulnerability through misclassification, algorithmic control, pricing manipulation, and data extraction.

---

## Category 1: Worker Classification

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FR-1.1 | **Forced Contractor Status**                  | Classifying workers as independent contractors when their role meets the legal definition of employment | Denying workers employment protections, benefits, and legal rights |
| FR-1.2 | **Benefits Denial**                           | Structuring engagements to avoid providing health insurance, retirement contributions, or paid leave | Shifting the full cost of social safety nets onto individual workers |
| FR-1.3 | **Tax Burden Shifting**                       | Requiring workers to manage and bear the full tax burden (self-employment tax, quarterly estimates) without adequate support or disclosure | Transferring employer tax obligations to workers without informed consent |
| FR-1.4 | **Exclusivity Without Employment**            | Imposing non-compete or exclusivity clauses on contractors who lack employment protections | Restricting worker freedom without providing employment security |
| FR-1.5 | **Misclassification Feedback Loops**          | Using worker misclassification as a baseline to benchmark industry norms, pressuring competitors to follow | Normalizing illegal classification practices across an industry |

## Category 2: Platform Control

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FR-2.1 | **Algorithmic Pay Manipulation**              | Adjusting pay rates via opaque algorithms that reduce earnings without notice or justification | Eroding worker income through unaccountable automated decisions |
| FR-2.2 | **Deactivation Without Due Process**           | Terminating or suspending worker access to a platform without transparent investigation or appeal | Removing livelihoods without procedural fairness |
| FR-2.3 | **Rating System Opacity**                      | Using rating algorithms that penalize workers without disclosing calculation methods, weighting, or correction options | Holding workers accountable to metrics they cannot understand or challenge |
| FR-2.4 | **Behavioral Nudging Through Penalties**      | Designing penalty structures (acceptance rate, response time) that coerce workers into unsafe or undesirable conditions | Using algorithmic pressure to override worker autonomy |
| FR-2.5 | **Arbitrary Policy Changes**                  | Modifying platform terms unilaterally with minimal notice, forcing workers to accept or lose access | Leveraging platform dependency to impose unfavorable conditions |

## Category 3: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FR-3.1 | **Race-to-the-Bottom Bidding**                | Allowing or encouraging clients to select workers solely on lowest price, driving unsustainable rates | Creating economic environments where workers cannot earn a living wage |
| FR-3.2 | **Platform Commission Escalation**             | Increasing platform fees or commission percentages without providing additional value or transparent justification | Extracting growing revenue from worker earnings without commensurate return |
| FR-3.3 | **Surge Pricing That Suppresses Worker Share** | Implementing surge or priority pricing where the platform captures a disproportionate share of the increase | Profiting from demand spikes while minimizing worker benefit |
| FR-3.4 | **Unpaid Scope Creep Facilitation**           | Providing no mechanism to enforce agreed scope, allowing clients to expand requirements without additional compensation | Enabling systematic underpayment for actual work performed |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FR-4.1 | **Worker Location Tracking**                  | Continuously tracking worker location beyond what is necessary for service delivery | Collecting disproportionate personal data under the guise of operational necessity |
| FR-4.2 | **Customer Data Hoarding from Workers**       | Preventing workers from accessing client contact information or project history they need to build independent careers | Using data asymmetry to maintain platform dependency and prevent worker independence |
| FR-4.3 | **Behavioral Scoring**                        | Building internal performance or risk scores based on work patterns, refusal rates, or earnings without worker access or consent | Creating hidden dossiers that affect worker opportunities without their knowledge |
| FR-4.4 | **Profile Data Portability Blocking**         | Preventing workers from exporting their reviews, portfolios, or professional history when leaving a platform | Using data as a lock-in mechanism to prevent worker mobility |
| FR-4.5 | **Data Sale to Third Parties**               | Selling aggregated or individualized worker data to recruiters, insurers, or analytics firms without explicit consent | Monetizing worker information that could be used to their detriment |

---

## Category 5: Access

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FR-5.1 | **Algorithmic Schedule Instability**          | Assigning or enabling unpredictable work schedules that prevent financial or personal planning | Creating chronic uncertainty that undermines worker stability |
| FR-5.2 | **Geographic Lock-Out**                       | Restricting workers from accepting work in adjacent areas or zones without transparent justification | Artificially limiting earning opportunities through arbitrary boundaries |
| FR-5.3 | **Equipment Cost Shifting to Worker**        | Requiring workers to purchase, maintain, or insure equipment (vehicles, tools, devices) as a condition of engagement | Transferring capital costs of business operations onto individual workers |
| FR-5.4 | **Skill Gating Without Pathway**              | Requiring certifications or qualifications for access to higher-paying work without providing affordable development pathways | Creating closed opportunity structures that entrench earning inequality |
| FR-5.5 | **Digital Access Barriers**                   | Requiring smartphone ownership, high-speed internet, or payment processing capabilities as implicit prerequisites | Excluding workers who lack the resources to meet unstated technology requirements |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Classify workers accurately** — apply legal employment tests transparently, provide benefits to qualifying workers, and support tax compliance.
2. **Ensure platform accountability** — publish algorithmic criteria for pay, ratings, and deactivation, and offer meaningful appeal processes.
3. **Practice pricing fairness** — cap commission structures, distribute surge premiums equitably, and enforce project scope agreements.
4. **Protect worker data** — limit location tracking, enable data portability, prohibit unauthorized data sales, and grant access to behavioral scores.
5. **Guarantee equitable access** — stabilize scheduling algorithms, remove unjustified geographic restrictions, and provide pathways for skill advancement.
