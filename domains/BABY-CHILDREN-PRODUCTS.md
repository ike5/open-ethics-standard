# Baby & Children Products — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Baby & Children Products  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to manufacturers and retailers of products for babies and children. Children are uniquely vulnerable consumers who cannot evaluate safety or value; unethical practices in this domain exploit parental anxiety and directly endanger child health and development.

---

## Category 1: Product Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BA-1.1 | **Recall Delay**                 | Delaying product recalls after internal identification of safety defects to avoid financial impact | Endangering children for profit       |
| BA-1.2 | **Inadequate Testing Standards** | Bringing products to market with testing protocols insufficient for the developmental stage of intended users | Insufficient safety verification      |
| BA-1.3 | **Used Product Safety Risks**    | Selling or facilitating resale of products (car seats, cribs) without verifying compliance with current safety standards | Endangering children via expired standards |
| BA-1.4 | **Choking Hazard Labeling Gaps** | Failing to provide age-appropriate choking hazard warnings on toys and accessories | Safety information withholding        |
| BA-1.5 | **Sleep Product Misrepresentation** | Marketing inclined sleepers, soft bedding, or bumper pads as safe despite contraindication from pediatric guidelines | Endangering infant safety             |

## Category 2: Marketing & Advertising

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BA-2.1 | **Fear-Based Safety Marketing**  | Using scare tactics about safety threats to sell premium products (monitors, sanitized products) | Exploiting parental anxiety           |
| BA-2.2 | **Developmental Milestone Claims** | Making unsubstantiated claims that products accelerate cognitive or physical development | Deceptive health and development claims |
| BA-2.3 | **Gendered Product Marketing**   | Creating artificial gender distinctions in toys, clothing, and accessories to drive dual purchases | Gender stereotyping for profit         |
| BA-2.4 | **Character Licensing on Unhealthy Products** | Using popular children's characters to market sugary foods, low-nutrition snacks, and excessive-screen-time apps | Exploiting child recognition for unhealthy choices |
| BA-2.5 | **In-App Purchase Targeting**    | Designing children's apps with predatory microtransaction mechanics and manipulative UX | Exploiting children's inability to evaluate cost |

## Category 3: Pricing & Access

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BA-3.1 | **Safety-Essential Item Premiums** | Charging premium prices for safety-critical items (car seats, helmets, baby gates) beyond reasonable cost | Exploiting parental necessity         |
| BA-3.2 | **Subscription Diaper Cost Escalation** | Offering low introductory diaper subscription prices that escalate beyond retail cost | Subscription bait-and-switch          |
| BA-3.3 | **Secondhand Market Destruction** | Using product registration requirements and one-time-use designs to prevent safe resale | Artificial consumption; waste generation |
| BA-3.4 | **Formula Pricing Exploitation**  | Increasing infant formula prices during supply shortages for products deemed medically necessary | Price gouging on essential nutrition  |

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BA-4.1 | **Smart Monitor Data Collection** | Collecting sleep patterns, movement, and vital signs from baby monitors beyond device function | Over-collection of infant biometric data |
| BA-4.2 | **Developmental Data Sale**       | Selling aggregated developmental milestone and growth data from parenting apps | Profiting from children's health data |
| BA-4.3 | **Family Profiling**              | Building consumer profiles from baby registry, purchase, and app data for long-term marketing | Surveillance of families              |
| BA-4.4 | **Children's App Data Harvesting** | Collecting identifying information from children under 13 in violation of COPPA | Illegal data collection from minors   |

## Category 5: Ingredients & Materials

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| BA-5.1 | **Toxic Chemical Presence**      | Using known endocrine disruptors, heavy metals, or carcinogens in products that contact children's skin or mouths | Child health endangerment             |
| BA-5.2 | **Flame Retardant Necessity Claims** | Applying chemical flame retardants to products where they provide no meaningful safety benefit while claiming enhanced safety | Unnecessary chemical exposure; deceptive safety claims |
| BA-5.3 | **Organic/Natural Misrepresentation** | Labeling products as organic or natural when only minor components meet those standards | Greenwashing; material misrepresentation |
| BA-5.4 | **Microplastic in Feeding Products** | Manufacturing bottles, utensils, and plates from materials that shed microplastics during normal use | Long-term exposure risk concealment   |
| BA-5.5 | **Food Additive Concealment**    | Using artificial colors, flavors, and preservatives in baby food while marketing products as "clean" | Ingredient deception                 |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Exceed minimum safety standards** — conduct independent third-party testing beyond regulatory minimums; publish all test results publicly.
2. **Eliminate fear-based marketing** — prohibit advertising that creates parental anxiety; require evidence for all developmental and safety claims.
3. **Adopt COPPA-plus data practices** — collect no data from children under 13; delete all child data within 30 days of request; prohibit sale of family data under any circumstances.
4. **Implement pricing fairness for essentials** — commit to stable pricing on safety-critical items during shortages; cap formula price increases at inflation.
5. **Publish full material disclosures** — list all ingredients and materials in baby products; flag any known chemicals of concern; provide third-party safety certifications for all materials that contact skin or mouth.
