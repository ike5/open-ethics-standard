# Water & Sewage Utilities — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Water & Sewage Utilities  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to municipal and private water utilities, sewage treatment operators, and water infrastructure companies. Access to clean water is a fundamental human right; unethical practices in this domain directly threaten public health, environmental safety, and social equity.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Billing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WA-1.1 | **Regressive Rate Structures**   | Structuring rates so fixed charges represent a disproportionate burden on low-volume residential users | Economic inequity in essential service |
| WA-1.2 | **Infrastructure Surcharge Opacity** | Adding surcharges for capital projects without clear disclosure of project status, timeline, or cost breakdown | Hidden cost practice on monopoly service |
| WA-1.3 | **Shutoff Without Alternative Provision** | Disconnecting water service for non-payment without ensuring access to alternative safe water sources | Endangering basic human needs         |
| WA-1.4 | **Billing Estimation Abuse**     | Charging based on estimated usage during meter malfunctions, consistently overestimating in the utility's favor | Systematic overcharging               |
| WA-1.5 | **Privatization Cost Escalation** | Acquiring public systems and raising rates significantly beyond pre-privatization levels | Monopoly exploitation of captive market |

## Category 2: Water Quality & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WA-2.1 | **Contamination Notice Delay**   | Delaying public notification of water quality violations to avoid regulatory scrutiny or public alarm | Withholding health-critical information |
| WA-2.2 | **Testing Frequency Reduction**  | Reducing water quality testing frequency to cut costs, particularly in low-income service areas | Disparate safety risk                 |
| WA-2.3 | **Lead Service Line Replacement Delay** | Deferring lead service line replacement despite known health risks, particularly in disadvantaged communities | Environmental injustice               |
| WA-2.4 | **PFAS Concealment**            | Failing to test for or disclose the presence of per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances in drinking water | Withholding emerging contaminant data |
| WA-2.5 | **Boil Notice Communication Gaps** | Issuing boil water notices through channels that do not reach non-English speakers, elderly, or non-digital residents | Excluding vulnerable populations from safety alerts |

## Category 3: Access & Equity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WA-3.1 | **Shutoff Impact on Vulnerable Populations** | Disconnecting water to households with infants, elderly, or medically dependent residents | Endangering health of vulnerable individuals |
| WA-3.2 | **Private Well Neglect**          | Failing to provide testing support or contamination warnings to private well users near utility contamination plumes | Excluding residents from safety net   |
| WA-3.3 | **Community System Underinvestment** | Allocating infrastructure investment disproportionately to affluent areas while aging systems serve low-income communities | Infrastructure inequity               |
| WA-3.4 | **Liens for Water Debt**          | Placing liens on homes for unpaid water bills, leading to tax foreclosure | Extracting housing wealth for utility debt |

## Category 4: Environmental & Infrastructure

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WA-4.1 | **Sewage Overflow Concealment**   | Failing to report combined sewer overflows that release raw sewage into waterways | Environmental law violation; public health risk |
| WA-4.2 | **Treatment Chemical Risk Opacity** | Not disclosing the types and quantities of treatment chemicals used in water processing | Withholding information about consumer exposure |
| WA-4.3 | **Stormwater Management Neglect** | Underinvesting in green stormwater infrastructure while passing flood costs to property owners | Cost externalization                  |
| WA-4.4 | **Biosolids Disposal Opacity**   | Disposing of sewage sludge on agricultural land without full contaminant disclosure | Agricultural and food chain risk       |

## Category 5: Data & Technology

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WA-5.1 | **Water Usage Data Sale**         | Selling anonymized consumption data to third parties including real estate investors | Privacy violation; data exploitation  |
| WA-5.2 | **Smart Meter Surveillance**      | Deploying smart meters that collect granular usage data enabling inference of household occupancy and activity | Excessive surveillance                |
| WA-5.3 | **Customer Profiling from Usage** | Using consumption patterns to identify and target high-use customers for premium service upselling | Data misuse for commercial gain       |
| WA-5.4 | **Infrastructure Data Withholding** | Refusing to share system maps and condition data with municipalities or public researchers | Obstructing accountability and planning |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Adopt income-based rate structures** — implement lifeline rates for essential usage; cap fixed charges at a percentage of total bills for low-volume users.
2. **Guarantee shutoff protections** — prohibit disconnection for vulnerable households; provide at least 60 days notice and multiple repayment options before any service interruption.
3. **Accelerate lead line replacement** — publish an inventory of lead service lines; commit to full replacement within 10 years, prioritizing disadvantaged communities.
4. **Publish water quality in real time** — make all testing results publicly accessible online within 24 hours; provide contamination notices in all community languages.
5. **Implement data rights** — allow customers to access and delete their usage data; prohibit sale of consumption data; minimize smart meter data granularity.
