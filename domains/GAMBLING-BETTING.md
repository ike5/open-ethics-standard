# Gambling & Betting — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Gambling & Betting (Casinos, Sportsbooks, Online Platforms, Lotteries)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to gambling operators, sportsbooks, online betting platforms, and lotteries. Gambling is an industry whose core product can produce compulsive behavior and severe financial harm, creating an inherent tension between revenue maximization and consumer protection that demands explicit ethical guardrails.

---

## Category 1: Addiction Exploitation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GA-1.1 | **Loss Disguised as Win Feedback** | Celebrating near-misses or partial returns with winning sounds, animations, and visual cues that neurologically mimic actual wins | Deceptive reinforcement; exploiting cognitive bias |
| GA-1.2 | **VIP Programs for Problem Gamblers** | Extending VIP status, bonuses, and personalized hospitality to players whose loss patterns indicate addiction rather than recreational play | Rewarding and sustaining harmful behavior |
| GA-1.3 | **Targeted Marketing to Self-Excluded** | Sending promotions, free bets, or direct outreach to individuals who have registered on self-exclusion or self-ban lists | Violating explicit harm-avoidance consent |
| GA-1.4 | **Loss Chasing Facilitation**     | Offering boosted odds, free bets, or deposit matches specifically after significant losses to encourage continued play | Exploiting vulnerability; enabling harm |
| GA-1.5 | **Time Distortion Design**        | Removing clocks, windows, and natural time cues from physical and digital environments to obscure session duration | Environmental manipulation; informed-consent erosion |

## Category 2: Pricing & Odds

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GA-2.1 | **House Edge Opacity**            | Failing to disclose the effective house edge, return-to-player rate, or expected loss per wager on individual games and bets | Concealing true cost; informed-bet denial |
| GA-2.2 | **Parlay Promotion Manipulation** | Aggressively promoting parlay and accumulator bets that carry far higher house edges than single wagers without disclosing the difference | Exploiting complexity; hidden cost |
| GA-2.3 | **In-Play Betting Pressure**      | Using countdown timers, push alerts, and micro-market openings to rush bettors into rapid in-play wagers without deliberation time | Manufactured urgency; impaired judgment |
| GA-2.4 | **Odds Shifting Without Notice**  | Adjusting odds or payouts after bet placement on technicalities, or displaying different odds on different interfaces | Bait pricing; contract ambiguity |
| GA-2.5 | **Fee Concealment**               | Embedding withdrawal fees, currency conversion charges, or transaction costs in fine print | Hidden cost; deceptive pricing |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GA-3.1 | **Behavioral Profiling for Retention** | Building detailed behavioral models (session length, bet size escalation, game-switching patterns) to maximize engagement and minimize churn | Surveillance-driven exploitation |
| GA-3.2 | **Loss-Chasing Identification and Exploitation** | Identifying players who increase bets after losses and targeting them with personalized offers that reinforce the pattern | Weaponizing addiction signals |
| GA-3.3 | **Location-Based Push Notifications** | Sending betting prompts when users are near physical venues or during live events tied to their betting history | Contextual manipulation; consent overreach |
| GA-3.4 | **Financial Data Harvesting**     | Collecting and analyzing deposit patterns, bank details, and payment method data to assess and exploit spending capacity | Financial surveillance; privacy violation |

## Category 4: Youth Protection

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GA-4.1 | **Loot Boxes as Gambling Gateway** | Offering randomized purchasable rewards in gaming-adjacent platforms that mirror gambling mechanics without age verification or odds disclosure | Unregulated gambling access for minors |
| GA-4.2 | **Esports Betting Targeting Minors** | Marketing bets on esports events with youth-heavy audiences through influencer partnerships and platform-native advertising | Targeting minors; platform exploitation |
| GA-4.3 | **Cartoon-Style Gambling Apps**    | Designing gambling or gambling-adjacent apps with cartoon aesthetics, bright colors, and gamified mechanics appealing to children | Child-targeting design; normalization of gambling |
| GA-4.4 | **Age Verification Circumvention** | Implementing weak or easily bypassed age gates (self-declaration only, no ID verification) to maximize user acquisition | Regulatory circumvention; youth access |
| GA-4.5 | **Social Media Influencer Partnerships** | Using influencers popular with underage audiences to promote betting platforms without adequate age-gating on content | Indirect marketing to minors |

## Category 5: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GA-5.1 | **Withdrawal Delay Tactics**      | Imposing lengthy processing windows, manual review steps, or minimum withdrawal thresholds that keep funds on-platform | Financial hostage-taking; friction extraction |
| GA-5.2 | **KYC as Delay Mechanism**        | Requesting identity verification only at withdrawal, not deposit, creating asymmetrical friction that discourages cash-outs | Selective compliance; barrier to funds |
| GA-5.3 | **Account Closure Resistance**    | Making account closure require phone calls, multi-day cooling periods, or repeated confirmation steps while signup is instant | Asymmetric friction; exit barrier |
| GA-5.4 | **Bonus Trap Clauses**           | Structuring bonus terms so that any withdrawal forfeits the entire bonus and associated winnings, effectively locking deposits | Predatory contract terms |
| GA-5.5 | **Dispute Resolution Favoritism** | Requiring arbitration in operator-friendly jurisdictions, imposing short complaint windows, or using internal adjudication without external oversight | Unfair dispute process; accountability avoidance |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Implement robust harm detection** — deploy real-time monitoring for loss-chasing, session escalation, and self-exclusion breaches; automatically impose mandatory cool-off periods when indicators are triggered.
2. **Disclose true costs** — display house edge, return-to-player rate, and expected loss per wager prominently on every game and bet type before confirmation.
3. **Protect minors** — enforce verified identity checks at signup and deposit; prohibit marketing on platforms with significant underage audiences; remove gambling-adjacent mechanics (loot boxes) from youth-accessible products.
4. **Ensure fund accessibility** — process withdrawals within 24 hours through the same method used for deposit; complete KYC at signup, not at withdrawal; offer instant account closure.
5. **Cease exploitation-based engagement** — prohibit targeted marketing to self-excluded individuals, loss-chasers, and VIPs showing addiction indicators; ban loss-disguised-as-win feedback.
