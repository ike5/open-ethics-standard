# Plumbing & Home Repair — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Plumbing & Home Repair (Plumbing Contractors, HVAC Technicians, Electricians, General Handymen)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to plumbing and home repair service providers. Homeowners and tenants rely on trade professionals in moments of vulnerability, and unethical practices in this domain exploit that urgency through price gouging, unnecessary work, code violations, and data misuse.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PL-1.1 | **Emergency Markup Exploitation**            | Charging significantly higher rates for emergency or after-hours calls without disclosing the premium in advance | Exploiting urgent situations when consumers have no time to compare alternatives |
| PL-1.2 | **Unnecessary Service Recommendation**        | Recommending repairs, replacements, or services that are not required to address the reported issue | Generating revenue through deception about the necessity of work |
| PL-1.3 | **Parts Markup Concealment**                  | Charging retail or inflated prices for parts without disclosing the markup over wholesale cost | Hiding profit margins in parts to make total pricing appear lower than it is |
| PL-1.4 | **Flat-Rate Pricing Opacity**                 | Quoting flat-rate prices that far exceed the time-and-materials cost without disclosing the calculation | Using flat-rate structures to obscure excessive per-hour effective rates |
| PL-1.5 | **Diagnostic Fee Conversion**                | Advertising free or low-cost diagnostics that convert into costly repair commitments without re-quoting | Baiting customers with low entry prices and switching to expensive services |

## Category 2: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PL-2.1 | **Apprentice Billing at Journeyman Rate**     | Charging clients for journeyman-level labor while assigning apprentices or unqualified workers | Overcharging for labor that does not meet the billed skill level |
| PL-2.2 | **Safety Shortcut in Confined Spaces**        | Skipping required safety protocols (ventilation, gas testing, protective equipment) in crawlspaces, basements, or attics | Endangering worker health and homeowner safety to reduce time and cost |
| PL-2.3 | **Licensing Misrepresentation**              | Claiming or implying licenses, certifications, or insurance coverage that is expired, revoked, or nonexistent | Deceiving consumers about qualifications and leaving them unprotected |
| PL-2.4 | **Unpaid Travel and Setup Time**             | Not compensating workers for travel time, vehicle preparation, or job-site setup while billing clients for it | Pocketing the difference between worker compensation and client charges |
| PL-2.5 | **Wage Theft Through Piece-Rate Manipulation** | Setting piece-rate or per-job pay at levels that result in sub-minimum-wage earnings for typical work speeds | Violating labor law by shifting efficiency risk onto workers |

## Category 3: Quality

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PL-3.1 | **Code Violation Concealment**                | Performing work that does not meet building or plumbing code and concealing the non-compliance from the property owner | Creating hidden safety hazards and exposing homeowners to future liability |
| PL-3.2 | **Temporary Fix Designed to Fail**            | Implementing superficial repairs intended to fail shortly, guaranteeing a return service call | Engineering repeat business through deliberate underperformance |
| PL-3.3 | **Used Part Installation as New**             | Installing previously used, refurbished, or salvaged components while billing for new materials | Fraudulently representing the condition and origin of installed materials |
| PL-3.4 | **Unpermitted Work Encouragement**           | Advising homeowners to skip required permits to save time or money, leaving work un-inspected | Exposing homeowners to code enforcement, insurance denial, and safety risks |
| PL-3.5 | **Improper Drainage or Venting**             | Installing plumbing that lacks proper venting, drainage slope, or backflow prevention to save time | Creating conditions for water damage, sewer gas, or contamination |

## Category 4: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PL-4.1 | **Estimate Overrun Without Approval**        | Exceeding an agreed estimate by significant margins without stopping work to obtain customer approval | Binding customers to charges they never consented to |
| PL-4.2 | **Lien Threat for Disputed Work**            | Filing or threatening mechanic's liens against property owners who dispute incomplete or defective work | Using legal leverage to coerce payment for deficient services |
| PL-4.3 | **Warranty Claim Denial**                    | Structuring warranties with vague terms, narrow exclusions, or burdensome proof requirements to avoid honoring claims | Selling guarantees that systematically evade fulfillment |
| PL-4.4 | **Hard-Sell Service Contracts**              | Pressuring homeowners into annual service agreements using fear of system failure or emergency costs | Using anxiety-based selling to lock in recurring revenue |
| PL-4.5 | **Verbal Agreement Misrepresentation**       | Claiming verbal agreements for additional work that the homeowner did not authorize | Fabricating consent to justify unauthorized charges |

## Category 5: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PL-5.1 | **Home Layout Data Sale**                    | Selling information about home plumbing layouts, system age, or property conditions to third parties | Monetizing detailed knowledge of private property without consent |
| PL-5.2 | **Smart Device Access During Service**       | Accessing, configuring, or resetting smart home devices (thermostats, leak detectors) beyond the scope of the service call | Intruding into connected home systems beyond authorized work |
| PL-5.3 | **Customer Information Sharing**             | Sharing client names, addresses, project details, or contact information with affiliate or partner businesses | Distributing personal data for cross-marketing without explicit consent |
| PL-5.4 | **Pre-Texting for Future Solicitation**      | Collecting contact and property information under the pretense of service delivery for use in future marketing | Using service interactions as data harvesting opportunities |
| PL-5.5 | **Photo Documentation Misuse**               | Taking and retaining photos of home interiors, valuables, or private spaces beyond what is needed for work documentation | Creating records that compromise home security and personal privacy |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Ensure pricing transparency** — disclose all rates, markups, and potential emergency premiums before work begins, and provide written estimates that bind the final bill.
2. **Uphold labor standards** — bill at the skill level actually delivered, enforce safety protocols, verify all licenses, and compensate workers fairly for all hours worked.
3. **Deliver quality work** — meet building codes, use genuine new materials as specified, obtain required permits, and design repairs for durability rather than repeat business.
4. **Respect consumer rights** — require approval for overages, refrain from lien threats on disputed work, honor warranties in good faith, and document all agreements in writing.
5. **Protect homeowner data** — prohibit the sale of property information, limit smart device interaction to authorized scope, and destroy unnecessary interior photos after job completion.
