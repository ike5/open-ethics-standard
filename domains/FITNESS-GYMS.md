# Fitness & Gyms — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Fitness & Gyms (Gym Chains, Boutique Studios, Franchises)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to fitness centers, gym chains, and boutique studios. Gyms operate on recurring-revenue models that create persistent incentives to trap members in unwanted contracts, extract hidden fees, and commodify personal health data — all while bearing responsibility for the physical safety of patrons on their premises.

---

## Category 1: Membership & Cancellation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FI-1.1 | **Roach Motel Signup**           | Making enrollment available online in seconds while requiring in-person visits, notarized letters, or certified mail to cancel | Asymmetric friction; cancellation barrier |
| FI-1.2 | **Cancellation Maze**            | Designing multi-step cancellation processes with confusing portals, hidden links, or mandatory "retention calls" that pressure members to stay | Dark pattern; intentional obstruction |
| FI-1.3 | **Zombie Billing After Cancellation** | Continuing to charge members after cancellation is submitted, citing "processing periods" or "billing cycle completion" | Unauthorized charges; financial extraction |
| FI-1.4 | **Contract Auto-Renewal Lock-In** | Automatically renewing annual contracts into new long-term commitments without affirmative member consent | Consent bypass; forced continuity |
| FI-1.5 | **Transfer Fee Trap**            | Charging excessive fees to transfer memberships between locations or to another person | Predatory fee on routine request |

## Category 2: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FI-2.1 | **Hidden Fees**                   | Adding maintenance fees, equipment fees, or "enhancement" charges not disclosed at signup | Undisclosed cost; deceptive pricing |
| FI-2.2 | **Annual Fee Surprise**           | Billing a large annual fee with minimal notice, buried in contract fine print | Surprise billing; lack of transparency |
| FI-2.3 | **Personal Training Upsell Pressure** | Requiring front-desk staff to aggressively push personal training packages during check-in or tours | High-pressure sales; exploiting commitment |
| FI-2.4 | **Price Discrimination by Location** | Charging different rates for the same membership tier based on perceived neighborhood income without transparent justification | Opaque differential pricing |
| FI-2.5 | **Class Credit Expiration**       | Selling class packs with short expiration windows that force over-purchasing or forfeiture | Manufactured urgency; value confiscation |

## Category 3: Health & Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FI-3.1 | **Equipment Maintenance Deferral** | Delaying repair or replacement of worn cables, frayed belts, or damaged machines to cut costs | Physical endangerment; negligence |
| FI-3.2 | **Overcrowding Risk**             | Selling far more memberships than facility capacity allows, creating unsafe conditions during peak hours | Profit over patron safety |
| FI-3.3 | **Unqualified Trainer Certification** | Employing trainers with dubious or internally manufactured credentials, presenting them as qualified professionals | Misrepresentation; physical harm risk |
| FI-3.4 | **AED and Emergency Readiness**   | Failing to maintain accessible automated external defibrillators or train staff in emergency response | Life-safety negligence |
| FI-3.5 | **Hazardous Facility Conditions** | Ignoring mold, poor ventilation, standing water, or broken flooring that poses slip-and-fall risks | Negligent premises maintenance |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FI-4.1 | **Biometric Data Collection and Sale** | Collecting body scans, heart rate data, or fitness assessments and selling to insurers, employers, or data brokers | Sensitive data exploitation; consent failure |
| FI-4.2 | **Health Metric Sharing Without Consent** | Sharing workout frequency, weight trends, or health goals with app partners, wearables platforms, or advertisers | Undisclosed health surveillance |
| FI-4.3 | **Location Tracking of Gym Visits** | Tracking member check-in patterns and visit frequency for behavioral profiling or targeted advertising | Location privacy violation |
| FI-4.4 | **App Data Over-Collection**      | Requiring mobile app permissions (contacts, photos, microphone) unrelated to gym functionality | Over-collection; consent bundling |

## Category 5: Marketing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FI-5.1 | **Body Shaming Advertising**      | Using fear-based messaging implying inadequacy about body size or shape to drive membership sales | Psychological manipulation; body harm |
| FI-5.2 | **Before/After Photo Manipulation** | Altering, staging, or selectively editing transformation photos to exaggerate results | Deceptive representation |
| FI-5.3 | **Miracle Result Claims**         | Promising specific weight loss, muscle gain, or health outcomes without disclosing individual variability or effort required | Misleading health claims |
| FI-5.4 | **Testimonial Cherry-Picking**    | Showcasing only exceptional results while suppressing the typical member experience | Survivorship bias; deceptive marketing |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Enable friction-free cancellation** — allow cancellation through the same channels as signup (online, in-app) with no more than two steps; confirm within 48 hours; cease billing immediately.
2. **Disclose total cost at signup** — present the full annualized cost including all recurring fees, annual charges, and add-ons before requiring payment information.
3. **Maintain safety standards** — inspect equipment on a published schedule; maintain AEDs and first-aid kits; cap memberships relative to verified facility capacity.
4. **Protect health and biometric data** — require explicit opt-in consent before collecting or sharing any biometric, health, or location data; prohibit sale of such data to third parties.
5. **Adopt honest marketing** — ban body-shaming imagery; require unedited and dated before/after photos; include disclaimers on results claims reflecting typical outcomes.
