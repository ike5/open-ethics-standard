# Pest Control — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Pest Control & Extermination  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to pest control and extermination service providers, including residential, commercial, and agricultural applicators. The pest control industry handles regulated toxic substances in spaces where people live, work, and play. Unethical practices in chemical application, pricing, and environmental stewardship create risks to human health, non-target species, and ecosystems while exploiting consumer anxiety about infestations.

---

## Category 1: Chemical Safety

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PS-1.1 | **Pesticide Application Beyond Label** | Applying chemicals at concentrations, frequencies, or locations exceeding label specifications | Regulatory violation; human and environmental health risk |
| PS-1.2 | **Indoor Residue Non-Disclosure**    | Failing to inform occupants about residual toxicity, required ventilation periods, or re-entry intervals | Withholding safety-critical information |
| PS-1.3 | **Pet and Child Risk Concealment**   | Not disclosing that applied chemicals pose specific risks to children, pets, or immunocompromised individuals | Vulnerable population endangerment    |
| PS-1.4 | **Restricted-Use Chemical Availability** | Making restricted-use pesticides available to uncertified applicators or using them without proper licensing | Regulatory violation; public health risk |
| PS-1.5 | **Chemical Mixing Without Disclosure** | Combining chemicals in ways not specified on labels or failing to disclose tank-mix contents | Unknown exposure risk; label violation |
| PS-1.6 | **Expired Chemical Use**             | Applying pesticides past their expiration date when efficacy and safety profiles are uncertain | Potential toxicity increase; efficacy fraud |

## Category 2: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PS-2.1 | **Unnecessary Treatment Upselling**  | Recommending treatments for pests not present or applying preventive chemicals without evidence of threat | Consumer fraud; unnecessary chemical exposure |
| PS-2.2 | **Quarterly Contract Auto-Renewal**  | Automatically enrolling customers in recurring treatment plans with difficult opt-out processes | Consumer lock-in; deceptive billing   |
| PS-2.3 | **Emergency Markup Exploitation**    | Charging premium rates for emergency or after-hours service when infestations pose urgent health risks | Price gouging during vulnerability    |
| PS-2.4 | **Warranty Exclusion Fine Print**    | Including broad exclusions in service guarantees that effectively void warranties for common scenarios | Consumer deception; accountability avoidance |

## Category 3: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PS-3.1 | **Non-Target Species Harm**          | Applying broad-spectrum pesticides that kill beneficial insects, birds, or wildlife beyond the target pest | Ecosystem damage; unnecessary ecological harm |
| PS-3.2 | **Pollinator Impact Disregard**      | Using neonicotinoids or other pollinator-toxic chemicals without pollinator risk assessment or mitigation | Environmental stewardship failure; biodiversity threat |
| PS-3.3 | **Waterway Contamination**           | Applying chemicals near waterways, storm drains, or wells without required buffer zones or runoff mitigation | Water supply contamination; regulatory violation |
| PS-3.4 | **IPM Avoidance in Favor of Chemical Solutions** | Defaulting to chemical treatment instead of Integrated Pest Management approaches that minimize pesticide use | Unnecessary environmental chemical burden; sustainable practice avoidance |

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PS-4.1 | **Property Infestation Data Sale**    | Selling or sharing data about client infestations, pest types, or severity to data brokers or real estate platforms | Privacy violation; property value manipulation |
| PS-4.2 | **Home Vulnerability Assessment Sharing** | Distributing structural vulnerability reports identifying entry points or sanitation issues to third parties | Privacy breach; enabling exploitation |
| PS-4.3 | **Before/After Photo Privacy**       | Taking, retaining, or sharing interior photographs beyond what is necessary for service documentation | Privacy violation; surveillance       |
| PS-4.4 | **Treatment History Disclosure to Buyers** | Providing property treatment histories to prospective buyers or real estate agents without owner consent | Privacy violation; information asymmetry exploitation |

## Category 5: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                              | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| PS-5.1 | **Technician Production Quota Pressure** | Setting unrealistically high daily job counts that incentivize rushed, careless application | Quality compromise; safety risk from rushed work |
| PS-5.2 | **Training Inadequacy**              | Providing insufficient training on chemical handling, label compliance, or emergency response | Worker endangerment; public safety risk |
| PS-5.3 | **Safety Gear Cost Shifting to Workers** | Requiring technicians to purchase their own respirators, gloves, or protective clothing | Worker financial exploitation; safety discouragement |
| PS-5.4 | **Route Overloading**                | Assigning more stops than can be safely completed, forcing technicians to skip safety protocols or work excessive hours | Worker exhaustion; quality and safety compromise |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Follow label law strictly** — never exceed label specifications for concentration, frequency, or application area; maintain current applicator certifications.
2. **Disclose all treatment details** — provide clients with product names, active ingredients, re-entry intervals, and specific risk warnings for vulnerable populations before application.
3. **Adopt Integrated Pest Management** — offer non-chemical and least-toxic alternatives as first-line treatments; document justification for chemical use when chosen.
4. **Implement data minimization** — collect only data necessary for service delivery; never sell client data; destroy access credentials and photos upon request or contract termination.
5. **Protect workers** — provide all safety equipment at no cost; limit daily routes to hours that allow safe, thorough work; maintain comprehensive training records.
6. **Practice transparent pricing** — provide binding quotes before treatment; clearly explain warranty terms and exclusions; make auto-renewal opt-in with easy cancellation.
