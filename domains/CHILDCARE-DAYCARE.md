# Childcare & Daycare — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Childcare & Daycare (Daycare Centers, In-Home Care, Preschools, After-School Programs)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to childcare and daycare providers. Children are among the most vulnerable members of society, and unethical practices in this domain exploit families during critical developmental periods, compromise child safety, and undervalue the essential workforce that cares for the next generation.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CH-1.1 | **Fee Escalation Without Service Improvement** | Raising tuition or fees without corresponding improvements in staff, facilities, or programming | Extracting additional revenue from families without delivering additional value |
| CH-1.2 | **Late Pick-Up Penalty Exploitation**         | Imposing disproportionate financial penalties for minor lateness without clear prior agreement | Using punitive fees as a revenue stream rather than a deterrent |
| CH-1.3 | **Sibling Discount Absence**                   | Refusing to offer sibling discounts despite reduced per-child marginal costs | Overcharging families with multiple children relative to actual cost of care |
| CH-1.4 | **Hidden Supply and Activity Fees**           | Charging mandatory fees for supplies, field trips, or special activities not disclosed at enrollment | Concealing the true cost of care through fragmented billing |
| CH-1.5 | **Deposit and Withdrawal Penalties**           | Requiring large non-refundable deposits or imposing excessive notice-period penalties | Trapping families financially and discouraging mobility |

## Category 2: Quality

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CH-2.1 | **Staff-to-Ratio Violations**                 | Exceeding permitted child-to-caregiver ratios to reduce labor costs | Compromising child safety and individual attention for profit |
| CH-2.2 | **Unqualified Caregiver Concealment**         | Assigning individuals without required credentials to caregiving roles and concealing this from parents | Placing children in the care of unvetted or underqualified adults |
| CH-2.3 | **Safety Incident Underreporting**            | Failing to report or downplaying injuries, illnesses, or safety breaches to parents and regulators | Withholding information that parents need to protect their children |
| CH-2.4 | **Curriculum Misrepresentation**              | Advertising educational programs or pedagogical approaches that are not actually implemented | Misleading parents about the developmental benefits their children receive |
| CH-2.5 | **Facility Maintenance Neglect**             | Deferring safety-related maintenance or hygiene standards to cut costs | Creating unsafe or unhealthy environments for vulnerable children |

## Category 3: Access

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CH-3.1 | **Waitlist Opacity**                          | Operating waitlists without transparent criteria, estimated timelines, or priority explanations | Denying families fair and predictable access to care |
| CH-3.2 | **Subsidy Acceptance Failure**                | Declining government subsidy programs or creating administrative barriers for subsidized families | Excluding lower-income families from access to care |
| CH-3.3 | **Special Needs Exclusion**                   | Refusing enrollment or providing inadequate accommodations for children with disabilities or special needs | Discriminating against children who require additional support |
| CH-3.4 | **Schedule Rigidity**                         | Requiring full-time enrollment without offering part-time or flexible schedules | Excluding families who need partial or non-standard care arrangements |
| CH-3.5 | **Geographic Redlining**                      | Concentrating facilities in affluent areas while avoiding lower-income neighborhoods | Creating childcare deserts that disproportionately affect disadvantaged communities |

---

## Category 4: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CH-4.1 | **Child Development Data Sale**               | Selling developmental assessments, behavioral records, or learning data to third parties | Monetizing sensitive information about children's growth and vulnerabilities |
| CH-4.2 | **Parent Surveillance via Cameras**           | Providing live camera access to parents without clear policies on who views footage and how it is stored | Creating surveillance environments that erode trust and privacy |
| CH-4.3 | **Photo Sharing Without Clear Consent**       | Posting or sharing children's images on social media or marketing materials without explicit, informed parental consent | Exploiting children's images for promotional gain |
| CH-4.4 | **Behavioral Data Retention Beyond Necessity** | Retaining detailed behavioral and developmental records indefinitely after a child leaves the program | Maintaining unnecessary data that could be misused or exposed in breaches |

---

## Category 5: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| CH-5.1 | **Childcare Worker Wage Suppression**         | Paying caregivers below market or living wage despite high parent fees | Exploiting a historically undervalued workforce for profit |
| CH-5.2 | **Qualification Inflation for Entry Roles**   | Requiring advanced degrees or certifications for positions that do not warrant them while not compensating accordingly | Creating unnecessary barriers to entry while extracting higher qualifications at low pay |
| CH-5.3 | **Burnout from Ratio Violations**             | Sustaining unsustainable workloads through inadequate staffing that leads to caregiver burnout and turnover | Prioritizing cost savings over worker wellbeing and care consistency |
| CH-5.4 | **Unpaid Professional Development Expectations** | Requiring caregivers to complete continuing education on their own time without compensation | Shifting the cost of maintaining qualifications onto underpaid workers |
| CH-5.5 | **Retaliation for Safety Reporting**          | Penalizing workers who report safety violations, ratio breaches, or regulatory non-compliance | Silencing whistleblowers and perpetuating unsafe conditions |

---

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Practice full pricing transparency** — disclose all fees at enrollment, maintain reasonable late-pick-up policies, and offer sibling discounts where applicable.
2. **Maintain rigorous quality standards** — adhere to staff-to-child ratios, verify caregiver qualifications, and promptly report all safety incidents.
3. **Ensure equitable access** — operate transparent waitlists, accept subsidies without administrative barriers, and provide meaningful accommodations for children with special needs.
4. **Protect children's data** — prohibit the sale of developmental or behavioral records, secure camera access policies, and obtain explicit consent for image use.
5. **Uphold fair labor practices** — pay living wages, compensate professional development, maintain safe workloads, and protect whistleblowers.
