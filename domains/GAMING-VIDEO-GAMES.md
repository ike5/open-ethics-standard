# Gaming & Video Games — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Gaming & Video Games (Publishers, Developers, Platform Holders)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to video game publishers, developers, and platform holders. Games reach billions of players — including children — through digital storefronts where in-game purchases, data collection, and labor practices operate with minimal regulatory oversight, creating systemic opportunities for exploitation.

---

## Category 1: Monetization

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GV-1.1 | **Loot Box Probability Opacity**  | Failing to disclose drop rates for randomized purchasable items, preventing players from making informed spending decisions | Concealed odds; gambling-like exploitation |
| GV-1.2 | **Pay-to-Win Mechanics**          | Selling gameplay advantages (stat boosts, exclusive gear, faster progression) that create competitive imbalances between paying and non-paying players | Purchased unfairness; competitive integrity erosion |
| GV-1.3 | **Battle Pass FOMO Pressure**     | Designing time-limited progression tracks that require daily play to complete, exploiting fear of missing out to drive engagement and spending | Manufactured urgency; behavioral manipulation |
| GV-1.4 | **Premium Currency Obfuscation**   | Using multi-layer virtual currencies with non-equivalent exchange rates to obscure real-money costs | Deceptive pricing; mental accounting exploit |
| GV-1.5 | **Grind-or-Pay Design**           | Intentionally making free progression painfully slow to push players toward paid shortcuts | Coercive monetization; degrading free experience |

## Category 2: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GV-2.1 | **Crunch Culture**                | Sustaining mandatory or culturally expected overtime periods of 60–100+ hour weeks for months before ship dates | Systemic burnout; health endangerment |
| GV-2.2 | **Contractor Exploitation**       | Classifying long-term workers as contractors or temp staff to deny benefits, job security, and profit-sharing while expecting full-time dedication | Misclassification; benefit avoidance |
| GV-2.3 | **Layoff After Profit**           | Terminating large portions of development teams immediately after profitable launches to avoid sharing ongoing revenue | Extractive employment; disposable workforce |
| GV-2.4 | **Credit Omission**               | Removing or omitting developer credits from shipped titles for departing employees or contractors | Erasure of professional contribution |
| GV-2.5 | **Non-Compete and Mobility Restrictions** | Imposing broad non-compete clauses on creative workers that prevent movement between studios | Restricting career mobility; suppressing wages |

## Category 3: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GV-3.1 | **Always-Online DRM**             | Requiring persistent internet connection for single-player games, enabling continuous telemetry collection and rendering purchases useless without server access | Unnecessary surveillance; ownership erosion |
| GV-3.2 | **Anti-Cheat Kernel Access**      | Installing kernel-level anti-cheat software that has system-wide access to all processes, files, and network traffic | Security overreach; system integrity risk |
| GV-3.3 | **Telemetry Over-Collection**     | Collecting hardware specs, browsing habits, communication metadata, and play-pattern data far beyond what is needed for game improvement | Surveillance overreach; consent failure |
| GV-3.4 | **Child Data Harvesting**         | Collecting behavioral and personal data from players known to be under 13 without verifiable parental consent | COPPA violation; child privacy exploitation |

## Category 4: Community

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GV-4.1 | **Toxicity Moderation Failures**  | Under-investing in moderation tools, human moderators, and enforcement against harassment, hate speech, and grooming in multiplayer environments | Neglecting player safety; enabling abuse |
| GV-4.2 | **Dark Pattern UI**               | Designing social features (friend lists, gifting, team recruitment) to create social pressure for spending or continued play | Social manipulation; behavioral coercion |
| GV-4.3 | **Community Support Abandonment** | Reducing support staff, closing forums, or deprecating community tools after launch while continuing to sell the product | Post-sale neglect; community exploitation |
| GV-4.4 | **Fan Content Claim Abuse**        | Issuing overbroad copyright takedowns against fan art, mods, or community creations while profiting from community-generated engagement | Corporate overreach; community parasitism |

## Category 5: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| GV-5.1 | **Broken Releases**              | Shipping games in demonstrably unfinished or unplayable states with the intention of patching post-launch, using pre-order revenue as interest-free funding | Deceptive product delivery; beta-as-release |
| GV-5.2 | **Sunsetting Without Refund**     | Shutting down online-only games and making purchased content permanently inaccessible without offering refunds, data export, or offline mode | Confiscation of purchased goods; ownership denial |
| GV-5.3 | **EULA Forced Arbitration**       | Requiring binding arbitration clauses in end-user license agreements that waive class-action rights and jury trials as a condition of use | Contractual rights stripping; accountability avoidance |
| GV-5.4 | **Retroactive Terms Changes**    | Unilaterally updating EULAs or terms of service after purchase to reduce consumer rights or expand data collection | Unilateral contract modification; consent after sale |
| GV-5.5 | **Refund Gatekeeping**            | Imposing arbitrary play-time limits, hidden windows, or opaque processes that prevent players from receiving refunds for defective products | Consumer right obstruction; refund barrier |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Disclose all monetization odds** — publish exact drop rates for randomized purchases before checkout; classify loot boxes as gambling where legally applicable and apply corresponding age gates.
2. **Protect labor standards** — cap overtime at contractually agreed limits with mandatory premium pay; reclassify long-term contractors as employees where duties are equivalent; include all contributors in game credits permanently.
3. **Minimize data collection** — collect only data necessary for game operation; require explicit opt-in for telemetry; remove kernel-level anti-cheat in favor of userspace alternatives; comply with COPPA at minimum.
4. **Invest in community safety** — staff moderation proportionally to player base; provide transparent reporting and appeals processes; publish quarterly moderation transparency reports.
5. **Respect consumer ownership** — offer refunds for defective products within reasonable play-time windows; provide offline modes or data export before sunsetting; eliminate forced arbitration clauses from EULAs.
