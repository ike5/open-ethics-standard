# Insurance — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Insurance (Health, Life, Property & Casualty)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to insurance carriers, brokers, and InsurTech platforms. Insurance is a promise of future protection; unethical practices convert that promise into a profit extraction mechanism, leaving policyholders vulnerable precisely when they need help most.

---

## Category 1: Claims Handling

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| IN-1.1 | **Claim Delay Tactics**          | Using bureaucratic delays, repetitive documentation requests, and slow responses to wear down claimants | Bad faith; financial coercion         |
| IN-1.2 | **Lowball Settlements**          | Making initial offers drastically below documented loss, expecting claimants to accept out of exhaustion | Exploitation of vulnerability         |
| IN-1.3 | **Policy Interpretation Games**  | Interpreting ambiguous policy language exclusively in the insurer's favor, contradicting the reasonable expectations of the policyholder | Bad faith contract interpretation     |
| IN-1.4 | **Pre-Authorization Barriers**  | Requiring excessive pre-authorization steps that delay medically necessary treatment | Endangering health for cost savings   |
| IN-1.5 | **Denial Without Explanation**  | Rejecting claims without providing specific policy provisions or reasoning, making appeal impossible | Due process violation                 |

## Category 2: Pricing & Underwriting

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| IN-2.1 | **Proxy Discrimination**         | Using seemingly neutral factors (credit scores, ZIP codes, occupation) that correlate with protected classes to set rates | Algorithmic discrimination            |
| IN-2.2 | **Premium Creep Without Claim**  | Raising premiums annually regardless of claims history or actual risk changes | Exploitation of customer lock-in      |
| IN-2.3 | **Loyalty Penalty**              | Charging long-term customers more than new customers for identical coverage | Penalizing loyalty; price discrimination |
| IN-2.4 | **Forced Bundling**              | Requiring purchase of unnecessary coverage as a condition for desired policies | Coerced purchase                      |
| IN-2.5 | **Teaser Rate Traps**            | Offering low initial premiums that escalate significantly after the first renewal | Bait-and-switch pricing               |

## Category 3: Data & Surveillance

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| IN-3.1 | **Wearable Data Coercion**       | Offering meaningful discounts only to customers who share wearable or telematics data, penalizing privacy-conscious individuals | Coerced surveillance                  |
| IN-3.2 | **Social Media Scrutiny**        | Monitoring claimants' social media for claim denial evidence without disclosure | Undisclosed surveillance              |
| IN-3.3 | **Genetic Data Profiling**       | Using or requesting genetic testing data for underwriting decisions | Genetic discrimination; privacy violation |
| IN-3.4 | **Third-Party Data Broker Abuse** | Purchasing consumer data from brokers to adjust rates or deny coverage without the consumer's knowledge or consent | Opaque data exploitation             |

## Category 4: Policy Design & Communication

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| IN-4.1 | **Complexity as Shield**         | Writing policies in unnecessarily complex language to obscure coverage limitations | Deliberate obfuscation                |
| IN-4.2 | **Silent Coverage Erosion**      | Renewing policies with reduced coverage at the same or higher premium without highlighting changes | Stealth downgrading                  |
| IN-4.3 | **Exclusion Hiding**             | Burying critical exclusions in subsections or appendices rather than presenting them alongside coverage summaries | Withholding material information     |
| IN-4.4 | **Renewal Pressure Tactics**      | Using urgency, fear-based messaging, or artificial deadlines to pressure policy renewals or upgrades | Manipulative sales practices          |

---

## Category 5: Cancellation & Non-Renewal

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| IN-5.1 | **Post-Claim Cancellation**      | Cancelling or non-renewing policies after a claim is filed, particularly for legitimate first claims | Retaliation for using the product     |
| IN-5.2 | **Redlining by Non-Renewal**     | Systematically non-renewing policies in geographic areas with higher claim rates, creating insurance deserts | Community abandonment                |
| IN-5.3 | **Cancellation Without Transition** | Dropping policyholders without providing reasonable time or assistance to find replacement coverage | Endangering financial security        |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish claims resolution metrics** — disclose average time-to-resolution, denial rates, and appeal outcomes by product line, updated annually.
2. **Implement algorithmic fairness audits** — audit underwriting and pricing models annually for disparate impact across protected classes.
3. **Adopt plain-language policy standards** — all policies must include a one-page coverage summary in plain language at or below an 8th-grade reading level.
4. **Establish claims transparency** — provide claimants with real-time status tracking, specific denial reasons citing policy sections, and clear appeal instructions.
5. **Limit data collection to actuarially justified factors** — publish the factors used in pricing and underwriting; eliminate proxies for protected classes.
