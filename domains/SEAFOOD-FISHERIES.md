# Seafood & Fisheries — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Seafood & Fisheries (Commercial Fishing, Aquaculture, Seafood Processing, Retail)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to the seafood and fisheries industry. The global seafood supply chain is plagued by labor exploitation, ecological destruction, and systematic fraud, and unethical practices in this domain deplete ocean ecosystems, abuse vulnerable workers, and deceive consumers about the origin and quality of their food.

---

## Category 1: Sustainability

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SQ-1.1 | **Overfishing Concealment** | Underreporting catch volumes, fishing beyond quotas, or concealing true harvest levels from regional fisheries management organizations | Ecosystem depletion; regulatory violation |
| SQ-1.2 | **Bycatch Non-Disclosure** | Failing to report or disclose the volume of non-target species killed during fishing operations, including marine mammals, turtles, and seabirds | Ecological harm concealment; biodiversity threat |
| SQ-1.3 | **Endangered Species Mislabeling** | Selling endangered or threatened species under common or mislabeled names to circumvent consumer awareness and trade protections | Species endangerment; consumer fraud |
| SQ-1.4 | **MSC Certification Process Gaming** | Exploiting weaknesses in Marine Stewardship Council certification standards, including conditions-based certifications that allow continued unsustainable practices during compliance periods | Certification fraud; greenwashing |
| SQ-1.5 | **Quota Shell Games** | Transferring quotas between vessels, regions, or corporate entities to obscure true catch levels and evade regional limits | Regulatory circumvention; accountability avoidance |

## Category 2: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SQ-2.1 | **Forced Labor on Fishing Vessels** | Using debt bondage, confiscated documents, or coercion to retain crew members on vessels for extended voyages without freedom to leave | Modern slavery; human trafficking |
| SQ-2.2 | **At-Sea Worker Abuse Concealment** | Committing physical violence, withholding food, or confining workers aboard vessels far from oversight or legal recourse | Worker abuse; jurisdictional exploitation |
| SQ-2.3 | **Migrant Worker Exploitation** | Hiring migrant workers through labor brokers who charge recruitment fees, withhold wages, or impose contract terms that leave workers indebted | Labor trafficking; debt bondage |
| SQ-2.4 | **Shore-Based Processing Wage Theft** |Paying processing plant workers below minimum wage, denying overtime, or misclassifying employees to avoid labor protections in shore-based facilities | Wage theft; labor violation |
| SQ-2.5 | **Isolation as Intimidation** | Deliberately keeping vessels at sea for months beyond contracted periods to prevent crew from accessing authorities or filing complaints | Forced labor continuation; abuse concealment |

## Category 3: Product Integrity

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SQ-3.1 | **Species Substitution and Mislabeling** | Selling lower-value species as higher-value species, such as tilapia as red snapper or pollock as cod | Consumer fraud; economic adulteration |
| SQ-3.2 | **Country of Origin Fraud** | Falsifying the country of origin to bypass tariffs, avoid health inspection flags, or command premium pricing based on perceived quality | Trade fraud; regulatory evasion |
| SQ-3.3 | **Farmed-Wild Misrepresentation** | Marketing farmed seafood as wild-caught to command price premiums and exploit consumer preference for wild products | Label fraud; consumer deception |
| SQ-3.4 | **Freshness Date Manipulation** | Resetting or extending sell-by and freeze-by dates through repackaging, re-icing, or chemical treatments that mask spoilage | Food safety violation; consumer endangerment |
| SQ-3.5 | **Weight Enhancement Through Glazing** | Over-glazing frozen seafood with excessive ice to inflate product weight beyond industry-accepted limits | Short-weight fraud; consumer deception |
| SQ-3.6 | **Treated Seafood Non-Disclosure** | Failing to disclose chemical treatments such as carbon monoxide, sodium tripolyphosphate, or preservatives applied to improve appearance or retain moisture | Informed consent violation; safety risk |

## Category 4: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SQ-4.1 | **Ocean Floor Destruction From Bottom Trawling** | Deploying bottom trawl gear in sensitive benthic habitats including coral, sponge beds, and seamounts, causing irreversible ecological damage | Habitat destruction; ecosystem collapse |
| SQ-4.2 | **Marine Protected Area Fishing Incursion** | Fishing within designated marine protected areas or no-take zones through boundary ambiguity, GPS manipulation, or weak enforcement exploitation | Protected area violation; conservation undermining |
| SQ-4.3 | **Microplastic From Fishing Gear** | Abandoning, losing, or discarding fishing gear (ghost gear) that degrades into microplastics and continues entangling marine life | Marine pollution; wildlife endangerment |
| SQ-4.4 | **Carbon Footprint of Long-Distance Seafood** | Marketing global-sourced seafood as sustainable without accounting for the significant refrigeration and transport emissions across supply chains | Sustainability greenwashing; climate impact concealment |
| SQ-4.5 | **Aquaculture Chemical Discharge** | Releasing antibiotics, pesticides, and chemical treatments from open-net aquaculture operations into surrounding marine ecosystems | Water pollution; antimicrobial resistance spread |

---

## Category 5: Traceability

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| SQ-5.1 | **Supply Chain Opacity** | Constructing complex multi-tier supply chains with insufficient documentation, making it impossible to trace seafood from catch to consumer | Accountability avoidance; traceability failure |
| SQ-5.2 | **Transshipment Concealment** | Transferring catch at sea between vessels to mix legal and illegal catch, obscure origins, or evade port state controls | Illegal fishing facilitation; laundering of catch |
| SQ-5.3 | **Mixed Catch Certification** | Certifying entire loads based on a portion of legally caught fish while including undocumented or illegally caught species within the same shipment | Certification fraud; regulatory circumvention |
| SQ-5.4 | **Vessel Tracking Avoidance** | Disabling, tampering with, or failing to install AIS or VMS tracking systems to evade monitoring of fishing locations and activities | Monitoring evasion; illegal fishing enablement |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Verify sustainability claims** — source only from fisheries with scientifically verified stock assessments; disclose bycatch rates; maintain honest MSC engagement without exploiting conditions.
2. **Eliminate forced labor** — conduct due diligence across all vessel and processing operations; require crew contracts with repatriation guarantees; prohibit recruitment fee structures that create debt bondage.
3. **Ensure product truth** — implement DNA or isotope testing for species verification; disclose country of origin, farming method, and all chemical treatments; honor accurate date labeling.
4. **Protect marine ecosystems** — avoid bottom trawling in sensitive habitats; respect marine protected area boundaries; account for full supply chain carbon footprint in sustainability claims.
5. **Guarantee traceability** — maintain catch-to-consumer documentation; prohibit at-sea transshipment unless independently monitored; require active vessel tracking on all fishing vessels.
