# Home Appliances & Electronics — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Home Appliances & Electronics (Manufacturers, Retailers, Smart Device Makers, Warranty Providers)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to manufacturers and retailers of home appliances and consumer electronics. These products are essential to daily life, and unethical practices in this domain shorten product lifespans, extract hidden costs, surveil domestic spaces, generate environmental harm, and deny consumers the right to repair.

---

## Category 1: Planned Obsolescence

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HA-1.1 | **Limited Lifespan Design**                   | Engineering products with intentionally shortened functional lifespans through material or component choices | Forcing premature replacement to sustain sales at consumer and environmental expense |
| HA-1.2 | **Repair Prevention via Parts Unavailability** | Discontinuing replacement parts or restricting their sale to authorized channels shortly after product launch | Making economically and environmentally rational repairs impossible |
| HA-1.3 | **Software Deprecation of Functional Hardware** | Withholding firmware updates or intentionally slowing devices via software to render hardware obsolete | Using software as a weapon against product longevity |
| HA-1.4 | **Component Pairing Restrictions**            | Using serialized or paired components that prevent third-party or self-repair with compatible parts | Creating artificial monopolies over repair to extract continued revenue |
| HA-1.5 | **Cosmetic Redesign as Obsolescence**         | Releasing minor cosmetic updates that render accessories, cases, or peripherals incompatible | Driving replacement through style changes rather than functional improvement |

## Category 2: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HA-2.1 | **MSRP Inflation for Discount Illusion**      | Setting artificially high manufacturer suggested retail prices so retailers can advertise deep discounts | Deceiving consumers about the true value and savings of a purchase |
| HA-2.2 | **Warranty Upsell on Reliable Products**     | Pressuring consumers to purchase extended warranties on products with historically low failure rates | Profiting from fear-based selling of unnecessary protection |
| HA-2.3 | **Extended Warranty Claim Denial Patterns**   | Designing warranty terms with broad exclusions, procedural hurdles, or documentation requirements that routinely deny valid claims | Selling protection that systematically avoids paying out |
| HA-2.4 | **Feature Gating Across Model Tiers**         | Including hardware capabilities in lower-cost models that are software-disabled to justify premium tier pricing | Charging consumers for hardware they already own but cannot access |
| HA-2.5 | **Subscription Requirements for Core Functionality** | Requiring ongoing subscription payments for features that were previously one-time purchases or that cost nothing to deliver | Converting ownership into indefinite rental |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HA-3.1 | **Smart Appliance Data Collection**           | Gathering usage patterns, household composition, and behavioral data from connected appliances without meaningful consent | Surveillance of domestic life under the guise of product improvement |
| HA-3.2 | **Always-On Microphone Concerns**            | Deploying voice-activated features that maintain microphone access without clear indication or user control | Creating always-listening environments in private spaces |
| HA-3.3 | **Firmware Update Data Harvesting**          | Bundling data collection or privacy-term changes into mandatory security updates | Coercing consent by linking data extraction to safety-critical patches |
| HA-3.4 | **Cross-Device Household Profiling**         | Correlating data across multiple connected devices to build comprehensive household behavioral profiles | Building invasive domestic profiles without aggregate informed consent |
| HA-3.5 | **Cloud Dependency for Basic Functions**      | Requiring cloud connectivity for core appliance functions, collecting data as a condition of use | Making data surrender a prerequisite for product operation |

## Category 4: Environmental

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HA-4.1 | **E-Waste Generation Encouragement**          | Designing products that are difficult to disassemble, recycle, or dispose of responsibly | Accelerating environmental harm through disposability by design |
| HA-4.2 | **Recycling Program Greenwashing**            | Promoting take-back or recycling programs that divert minimal waste from landfills while claiming environmental leadership | Misleading consumers about the environmental impact of their purchases |
| HA-4.3 | **Energy Rating Manipulation**               | Configuring devices for testing conditions that do not reflect real-world energy consumption | Deceiving consumers and regulators about environmental performance |
| HA-4.4 | **Non-Recyclable Material Selection**         | Choosing composite or non-recyclable materials when recyclable alternatives exist at comparable cost | Prioritizing manufacturing convenience over end-of-life environmental impact |
| HA-4.5 | **Packaging Excess**                         | Using oversized or non-recyclable packaging that generates unnecessary waste | Contributing to solid waste problems beyond product protection needs |

---

## Category 5: Repair Rights

| Ref   | Practice                                      | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HA-5.1 | **Schematic Withholding**                     | Refusing to publish repair manuals, wiring diagrams, or service schematics to independent repair shops | Denying access to information needed for safe and effective repair |
| HA-5.2 | **Specialized Tool Requirements**             | Designing products that require proprietary tools available only to authorized service providers | Creating tool-based monopolies that prevent independent and self-repair |
| HA-5.3 | **Void-if-Repaired Stickers**                | Applying labels that falsely claim warranties are voided by independent or self-repair | Intimidating consumers out of legal repair rights |
| HA-5.4 | **Diagnostic Software Restrictions**          | Limiting access to diagnostic software or error codes to authorized technicians only | Preventing independent repairers from identifying and fixing problems |
| HA-5.5 | **Parts Serialization and DRM**              | Embedding digital rights management in replacement parts that prevent third-party components from functioning | Using technology to enforce a repair monopoly |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Design for longevity** — establish minimum product lifespan commitments, maintain parts availability for a defined period, and decouple software from hardware obsolescence.
2. **Ensure pricing honesty** — set realistic MSRPs, disclose warranty claim rates, and eliminate feature gating and forced subscriptions for core functionality.
3. **Protect consumer privacy** — minimize data collection, provide clear microphone and camera controls, separate security updates from privacy term changes, and enable offline operation.
4. **Reduce environmental impact** — design for disassembly and recyclability, publish verified real-world energy data, and minimize packaging waste.
5. **Respect repair rights** — publish repair documentation, eliminate void-if-repaired stickers, make diagnostic tools available, and allow third-party parts.
