# Ride-Hailing & Mobility Platforms — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Ride-Hailing & Mobility Platforms  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to ride-hailing platforms, scooter-share operators, and mobility-as-a-service providers. These platforms mediate essential transportation while classifying workers as independent contractors; unethical practices exploit both workers and riders while evading the regulations that govern traditional transportation.

---

## Category 1: Worker Classification & Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RH-1.1 | **Forced Contractor Status**      | Classifying drivers as independent contractors to deny benefits, minimum wage guarantees, and labor protections | Worker misclassification             |
| RH-1.2 | **Benefits Denial**               | Withholding health insurance, paid sick leave, workers' compensation, and unemployment coverage from drivers who work full-time hours | Essential benefits exclusion          |
| RH-1.3 | **Algorithmic Wage Manipulation** | Using opaque algorithms to adjust driver pay rates downward while maintaining the same passenger fare, pocketing the difference | Wage theft via algorithm              |
| RH-1.4 | **Deactivation Without Due Process** | Terminating driver accounts based on automated fraud detection or rating thresholds without meaningful appeal | Denial of livelihood without recourse |
| RH-1.5 | **Multi-App Penalty**              | Penalizing or deprioritizing drivers who work on competing platforms through reduced ride allocation | Anti-competitive worker control     |

## Category 2: Rider Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RH-2.1 | **Inadequate Background Checks**  | Using name-based rather than fingerprint-based background checks, allowing convicted offenders to drive | Passenger safety risk                 |
| RH-2.2 | **Sexual Assault Reporting Gaps** | Failing to promptly report assaults to law enforcement; settling claims privately with NDAs | Obstructing justice; endangering future riders |
| RH-2.3 | **Driver Screening Inconsistency** | Applying different screening standards across jurisdictions based on local regulation rather than best practice | Geographic safety inequality           |
| RH-2.4 | **Passenger Verification Absence** | Lacking identity verification for riders, enabling harassment and assaults against drivers | Driver safety neglect                 |
| RH-2.5 | **Safety Feature Paywalls**       | Requiring drivers to pay for safety features like dash cams or rider verification that the platform should provide | Monetizing basic safety              |

## Category 3: Pricing & Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RH-3.1 | **Emergency Surge Pricing**       | Raising prices during natural disasters, mass shootings, or public emergencies when people need transport most | Price gouging on essential mobility    |
| RH-3.2 | **Upfront Pricing Opacity**        | Showing passengers a total price without disclosing the driver's share, platform fee, or surge multiplier | Hiding fare decomposition             |
| RH-3.3 | **Driver Pay Concealment**         | Not showing drivers the estimated earnings before they accept a ride, preventing informed decision-making | Information asymmetry exploitation    |
| RH-3.4 | **Tip Diversion**                  | Including tips in the displayed fare total, making it unclear whether tips reach the driver | Tip misdirection                      |
| RH-3.5 | **Cancellation Penalty Asymmetry** | Charging riders significant cancellation fees while imposing no penalty on drivers for no-shows | Asymmetric contract terms             |

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RH-4.1 | **Location Tracking Beyond Rides** | Collecting driver and rider location data continuously, even when the app is not actively in use | Persistent surveillance               |
| RH-4.2 | **Ride Data Sale to Municipalities** | Selling aggregated and individual ride data to cities for traffic planning and enforcement without user consent | Data brokering without authorization  |
| RH-4.3 | **Passenger Profiling**           | Using ride history, destinations, and behavioral data to profile riders for targeted advertising or risk scoring | Behavioral surveillance              |
| RH-4.4 | **Driver Movement Analytics**       | Tracking driver idle patterns, acceptance rates, and route choices to optimize platform revenue rather than driver earnings | Exploitative data use                |

## Category 5: Market & Community Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| RH-5.1 | **Predatory Pricing vs. Transit** | Subsidizing rides below cost to draw riders from public transit, then raising prices once transit is weakened | Undermining public infrastructure     |
| RH-5.2 | **Driver Supply Manipulation**    | Over-recruiting drivers to ensure short wait times, spreading demand too thin for drivers to earn sustainably | Over-saturation for platform benefit  |
| RH-5.3 | **Regulatory Arbitrage**          | Operating in new cities without local permits, then lobbying for favorable regulation after establishing user base | Preemptive regulatory evasion         |
| RH-5.4 | **Accessibility Compliance Delays** | Delaying wheelchair-accessible vehicle requirements and ADA compliance while claiming innovation flexibility | Disability exclusion                 |
| RH-5.5 | **Scooter & Bike Litter**          | Deploying shared scooters and bikes without designated parking, creating sidewalk obstructions and accessibility barriers | Public space privatization            |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Reclassify workers where appropriate** — provide full-time drivers with benefits, minimum earnings guarantees, and due process protections; publish driver earnings data transparently.
2. **Implement robust safety programs** — adopt fingerprint-based background checks; report all assaults to law enforcement within 24 hours; provide in-app emergency features at no cost to drivers.
3. **Cap surge pricing in emergencies** — limit surge multipliers during declared emergencies; disclose all fee components in upfront pricing; show drivers estimated earnings before ride acceptance.
4. **Protect data rights** — stop location tracking when the app is not in use; obtain opt-in consent before sharing ride data with municipalities; delete individual trip data within 90 days.
5. **Support public transit integration** — participate in regional transit planning; provide first-mile/last-mile connections rather than competing with transit; meet accessibility requirements in all operating markets.
