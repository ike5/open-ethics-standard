# Wedding & Event Planning — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Wedding & Event Planning Services  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to wedding planners, event coordinators, venues, and related service providers. The wedding industry operates in a high-emotion, high-stakes market where consumers are vulnerable to price inflation and contractual exploitation during one of the most stressful periods of their lives.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WE-1.1 | **Wedding Tax Markup**           | Charging significantly more for identical services (catering, photography, florals) when labeled "wedding" vs. "event" | Price discrimination based on occasion |
| WE-1.2 | **Package Bait-and-Switch**      | Advertising all-inclusive packages that exclude essential items revealed only after commitment | Hidden cost practice                  |
| WE-1.3 | **Vendor Kickback Concealment**  | Receiving undisclosed commissions from recommended vendors while presenting recommendations as impartial | Undisclosed conflict of interest       |
| WE-1.4 | **Mandatory Upgrade Pressure**   | Presenting base packages as inadequate to create social embarrassment, driving upgrades | Emotional manipulation for upsell      |
| WE-1.5 | **Service Charge Opacity**        | Adding mandatory service charges and gratuities not included in quoted prices | Total cost deception                  |

## Category 2: Contracts & Commitments

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WE-2.1 | **Non-Refundable Deposit Exploitation** | Requiring large non-refundable deposits that exceed reasonable liquidated damages | Unfair contract terms                 |
| WE-2.2 | **Force Majeure Imbalance**      | Drafting force majeure clauses that protect the vendor but not the client | Asymmetric risk allocation           |
| WE-2.3 | **Vendor Substitution Without Consent** | Replacing contracted vendors with lower-cost alternatives while charging original rates | Bait-and-switch service delivery      |
| WE-2.4 | **Cancellation Penalty Disproportion** | Imposing cancellation penalties that exceed actual lost revenue | Punitive contract enforcement         |

## Category 3: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WE-3.1 | **Vendor List Sale**             | Selling client contact information and guest lists to third-party vendors | Privacy violation; data exploitation  |
| WE-3.2 | **Guest Data Harvesting**         | Collecting guest information (dietary needs, addresses, emails) through RSVP systems for marketing | Undisclosed secondary data use        |
| WE-3.3 | **Photo & Video Rights Retention** | Retaining copyright or usage rights to client event photos and videos beyond portfolio use | Content ownership exploitation        |
| WE-3.4 | **Social Media Content Coercion** | Requiring clients to grant social media posting rights or tag vendors as a contract condition | Forced endorsement                   |

## Category 4: Service Quality & Delivery

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WE-4.1 | **Subcontractor Substitution**    | Using less experienced subcontractors than represented during booking | Service quality bait-and-switch        |
| WE-4.2 | **Material Downgrade**           | Substituting lower-quality materials (flowers, linens, food) while charging for premium specifications | Delivering less than contracted       |
| WE-4.3 | **Timeline Manipulation for Overtime** | Designing event timelines that guarantee overtime charges for key vendors | Engineering additional costs           |
| WE-4.4 | **Portfolio Misrepresentation**  | Showcasing work from styled shoots as actual client events          | Misleading marketing                  |

## Category 5: Labor & Worker Treatment

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| WE-5.1 | **Event Worker Minimum Wage Violations** | Paying setup, service, and breakdown workers below minimum wage during long event days | Labor exploitation                    |
| WE-5.2 | **Tip Withholding**              | Collecting mandatory gratuities from clients but not distributing full amounts to service staff | Wage theft                            |
| WE-5.3 | **Break Denial During Long Events** | Preventing event staff from taking legally required breaks during multi-hour events | Labor law violation                   |
| WE-5.4 | **Contractor Misclassification** | Treating regular event staff as independent contractors to avoid benefits and workers' compensation | Worker classification fraud           |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish standardized pricing** — provide itemized pricing for all services; disclose "wedding tax" differentials; offer price-matching for equivalent non-wedding services.
2. **Disclose vendor relationships** — reveal all financial relationships with recommended vendors before client commits; offer clients the option to use any vendor without penalty.
3. **Implement fair contract terms** — limit non-refundable deposits to 20% of total; include mutual force majeure protections; provide transparent cancellation schedules.
4. **Respect data rights** — prohibit sale of client and guest data; return all event media to clients with full rights transfer; delete guest data within 90 days post-event.
5. **Ensure fair labor practices** — pay all event workers at least local living wage; distribute full gratuities to staff; provide mandatory rest periods during events exceeding 6 hours.
