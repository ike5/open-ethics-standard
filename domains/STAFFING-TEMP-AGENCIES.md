# Staffing & Temp Agencies — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Staffing & Temporary Agencies  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to staffing agencies, temporary employment firms, labor hire companies, and workforce platforms. These organizations sit between employers and workers, controlling access to livelihoods while often operating with limited regulatory oversight. Ethical violations exploit this gatekeeping position to extract value from both sides—underpaying workers while overcharging clients.

---

## Category 1: Worker Classification

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ST-1.1 | **Permanent Temp Classification** | Assigning workers to indefinite temporary or contract status to avoid benefits, job security, and legal protections | Misclassifying long-term employment to evade obligations |
| ST-1.2 | **Benefits Denial**              | Excluding temp and contract workers from health insurance, paid leave, retirement, and other benefits available to permanent employees | Creating a two-tier workforce based on classification, not role |
| ST-1.3 | **Wage Theft via Markup**       | Charging clients significantly more than worker pay rate while concealing the markup percentage, effectively appropriating earned wages | Extracting disproportionate value from labor without transparency |
| ST-1.4 | **Misclassification as Independent Contractor** | Labeling workers as contractors to evade payroll taxes, workers' compensation, and unemployment insurance while controlling their work conditions | Shifting employer costs and risks onto workers |
| ST-1.5 | **Perma-Lancer Loops**           | Cycling workers through successive contract terms or between agencies to prevent conversion to permanent status | Sustaining indefinite contingency to block employment rights |

## Category 2: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ST-2.1 | **Client Overcharging**          | Inflating bill rates beyond market benchmarks while presenting costs as standard industry rates | Deceiving clients on true labor costs |
| ST-2.2 | **Worker Underpaying**           | Paying workers below market rate for equivalent permanent roles while collecting full client billing | Profiting from the pay gap without transparency |
| ST-2.3 | **Fee Stacking on Both Sides**   | Charging workers recruitment, placement, or processing fees while simultaneously charging clients finder or management fees | Double-charging across the transaction |
| ST-2.4 | **Buyout Fee Suppression**       | Setting prohibitively high temp-to-perm conversion fees that discourage clients from hiring workers directly | Trapping workers in temporary status for agency revenue |
| ST-2.5 | **Hidden Deduction Structures** | Subtracting uniform, background check, training, or administrative costs from worker paychecks without clear disclosure | Reducing take-home pay through opaque deductions |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ST-3.1 | **Resume Database Sale**         | Selling or licensing candidate resume databases to third-party recruiters, data brokers, or marketers without explicit consent | Monetizing personal career data beyond its intended purpose |
| ST-3.2 | **Worker Surveillance**           | Monitoring temp worker location, activity, keystrokes, or communications through client-mandated tools without transparency | Subjecting workers to surveillance they cannot negotiate or decline |
| ST-3.3 | **Performance Scoring Opacity**  | Assigning workers internal performance or reliability scores that affect placement opportunities without disclosing criteria or providing appeal | Creating opaque gatekeeping systems that control livelihoods |
| ST-3.4 | **Background Check Overreach**   | Conducting more extensive background or credit checks than required for the role and retaining results indefinitely | Collecting more data than necessary; retention beyond purpose |
| ST-3.5 | **Cross-Client Profile Sharing** | Sharing worker performance data between client companies without worker knowledge, creating informal blacklists | Coordinated exclusion from employment opportunities |

## Category 4: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ST-4.1 | **Inadequate Workplace Safety for Temps** | Placing workers in roles without proper safety training, orientation, or equipment equivalent to permanent employees | Exposing contingent workers to disproportionate risk |
| ST-4.2 | **Hazard Communication Failure** | Failing to inform temp workers about known workplace hazards, chemical exposures, or physical risks before assignment | Withholding safety-critical information |
| ST-4.3 | **Injury Reporting Discouragement** | Discouraging temp workers from reporting injuries, directing them to personal insurance instead of workers' compensation, or failing to report incidents | Suppressing injury data; denying workers' legal protections |
| ST-4.4 | **Repetitive Assignment to Hazardous Sites** | Repeatedly assigning the same temp workers to high-injury workplaces without escalating safety interventions | Using worker replaceability to tolerate dangerous conditions |
| ST-4.5 | **Heat and Fatigue Risk Neglect** | Assigning workers to extended shifts, outdoor work in extreme conditions, or consecutive heavy-labor assignments without mandated rest | Prioritizing staffing fill rates over worker health |

## Category 5: Career Advancement

| Ref   | Practice                         | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ST-5.1 | **Temp-to-Perm Promise Without Delivery** | Marketing assignments as temp-to-perm opportunities when the conversion rate is negligible or the position is structurally temporary | Exploiting hope of permanent employment to fill temporary roles |
| ST-5.2 | **Training Cost Shifting to Worker** | Requiring workers to pay for certifications, training, or assessments needed for placement—effectively charging for access to work | Shifting employer investment costs onto precarious workers |
| ST-5.3 | **Non-Compete on Low-Wage Workers** | Imposing non-compete or non-solicitation clauses on temp or low-wage workers that restrict their ability to seek direct employment | Restricting worker mobility far beyond legitimate business interest |
| ST-5.4 | **Skill Stagnation Assignment**  | Placing workers in repetitive, low-skill roles that offer no professional development while promising career growth | Trapping workers in dead-end assignments |
| ST-5.5 | **Credit Suppression**           | Failing to provide references, performance documentation, or skill verification that would help workers transition to permanent roles | Withholding career currency that enables advancement |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish pay transparency** — disclose worker pay rate and agency markup as separate line items on client invoices; workers shall know their bill rate.
2. **Classify workers correctly** — apply the same classification standards used for direct employees; convert workers to permanent after defined thresholds.
3. **Eliminate double-charging** — choose either client-side or worker-side fee models; prohibit simultaneous fees to both parties for the same placement.
4. **Protect worker data** — obtain explicit consent before sharing resume data; delete candidate records after 12 months of inactivity; prohibit cross-client blacklist sharing.
5. **Ensure equivalent safety** — provide temp workers the same safety training, orientation, and equipment as permanent employees at the same site.
6. **Support career progression** — publish temp-to-perm conversion rates; cover required training costs; eliminate non-competes for workers below management level.
