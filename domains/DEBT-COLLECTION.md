# Debt Collection & Recovery — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Debt Collection & Recovery  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to debt collection agencies, debt buyers, and recovery firms. Debt collection operates at the intersection of financial obligation and consumer vulnerability; unethical practices turn legitimate debt recovery into harassment, exploitation, and civil rights violation.

---

## Category 1: Harassment & Intimidation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DE-1.1 | **Excessive Contact**            | Calling debtors beyond legal frequency limits, at inconvenient hours, or at their workplace after being told to stop | Harassment; FDCPA violation          |
| DE-1.2 | **Threats of Arrest or Jail**    | Threatening criminal prosecution, arrest, or imprisonment for non-payment of civil debts | False legal threat; coercion         |
| DE-1.3 | **Third-Party Contact Abuse**    | Contacting family members, neighbors, employers, or social media contacts to embarrass debtors into paying | Privacy violation; intimidation      |
| DE-1.4 | **Verbal Abuse & Profanity**     | Using insulting language, racial slurs, or personal attacks during collection calls | Abuse; dignity violation             |
| DE-1.5 | **Fabricated Legal Action**       | Threatening lawsuits, liens, or garnishments that the collector has no intention or authority to pursue | Deceptive coercion                   |

## Category 2: Accuracy & Verification

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DE-2.1 | **Unverified Debt Pursuit**      | Attempting to collect debts without obtaining validation documentation from the original creditor | Collection without standing          |
| DE-2.2 | **Time-Barred Debt Collection**  | Pursuing or suing on debts beyond the statute of limitations without disclosing that they are unenforceable | Misleading consumers on legal rights  |
| DE-2.3 | **Identity Errors**               | Pursuing the wrong person through sloppy matching of names, addresses, or Social Security numbers | Collecting from non-debtors           |
| DE-2.4 | **Zombie Debt Revival**           | Re-aging old debts on credit reports or reopening settled debts to restart collection clocks | Credit reporting fraud                |
| DE-2.5 | **Inflated Balance Claims**      | Adding unauthorized fees, interest, or charges beyond what is legally permitted or contractually owed | Overcharging; phantom debt          |

## Category 3: Legal & Process

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DE-3.1 | **Robo-Signing Court Documents** | Filing affidavits and court documents signed by employees who did not personally verify the debt information | Perjury; court fraud                  |
| DE-3.2 | **Distant Jurisdiction Filing**   | Suing debtors in courts far from their residence to increase default judgment likelihood | Forum manipulation                    |
| DE-3.3 | **Default Judgment Exploitation** | Filing suit knowing the debtor cannot appear, then seeking garnishment or liens on default | Exploitation of inability to defend   |
| DE-3.4 | **Garnishment Overreach**         | Garnishing wages or bank accounts beyond legal limits or without required hardship exemptions | Depriving basic living standards      |
| DE-3.5 | **Settlement Bait-and-Switch**   | Offering settlement amounts in verbal calls that are not reflected in written agreements | Deceptive settlement practices        |

## Category 4: Data & Privacy

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DE-4.1 | **Credit Reporting Without Verification** | Reporting debts to credit bureaus without verifying accuracy, then resisting dispute resolution | Defamation via credit report          |
| DE-4.2 | **Debt Sale with Inaccurate Data** | Selling debt portfolios containing inaccurate, incomplete, or unverified account information to other collectors | Passing on bad data; perpetuating fraud |
| DE-4.3 | **Unauthorized Disclosure**        | Sharing debt details, amounts, or creditor names with unauthorized third parties including employers and neighbors | FDCPA disclosure violation            |
| DE-4.4 | **Data Breach Negligence**         | Failing to secure consumer financial data, leading to breaches that expose Social Security numbers and account details | Data negligence; identity theft risk  |

---

## Category 5: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| DE-5.1 | **Debt Validation Refusal**      | Failing to provide written debt validation within the required timeframe after a consumer disputes | Denying legal right to verification    |
| DE-5.2 | **Cease-and-Desist Ignorance**   | Continuing contact after receiving a written cease-and-desist request from the debtor | Willful legal violation               |
| DE-5.3 | **Dispute Rights Non-Disclosure** | Failing to inform consumers of their right to dispute the debt or request verification in initial communications | Withholding legal rights information  |
| DE-5.4 | **Payment Plan Misrepresentation** | Offering payment plans that do not reduce the total owed or extend interest accrual without disclosure | Hidden cost in repayment agreements   |
| DE-5.5 | **Partial Payment Traps**          | Accepting small payments that restart the statute of limitations without disclosing this consequence to the debtor | Legal rights reset without consent    |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Verify before collecting** — obtain and retain original creditor documentation before initiating collection; provide validation within 5 business days of dispute.
2. **Respect communication boundaries** — honor cease-and-desist requests within 24 hours; limit contact to legal frequency; prohibit workplace contact after request.
3. **Maintain data accuracy** — implement identity verification protocols; audit debt portfolios for accuracy before purchase or sale; correct credit reporting errors within 15 days.
4. **Provide full disclosure** — inform consumers of dispute rights in every initial communication; disclose time-barred debt status; provide written settlement terms.
5. **Implement complaint tracking** — maintain a searchable complaint database; resolve disputes within 30 days; report systemic violation patterns to regulators.
