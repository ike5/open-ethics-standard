# Hospitality & Hotels — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Hospitality & Hotels (Hotel Chains, Resorts, Short-Term Rentals, Booking Platforms)
**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to hotel chains, resorts, and hospitality platforms. The hospitality industry handles guests at their most personal — sleeping, bathing, and living in temporary spaces — where pricing opacity, labor exploitation, surveillance, and safety lapses directly affect physical and financial well-being in environments where consumers have limited alternatives.

---

## Category 1: Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HO-1.1 | **Resort Fee Concealment**        | Advertising nightly rates without mandatory resort, facility, or destination fees that can add 25–50% to the total cost | Deceptive pricing; mandatory hidden fees |
| HO-1.2 | **Dynamic Pricing Opacity**       | Adjusting room rates in real time based on demand, browsing history, or perceived willingness to pay without disclosing the pricing algorithm or factors | Opaque algorithmic pricing; rate discrimination |
| HO-1.3 | **Rate Parity Suppression**       | Contractually preventing third-party platforms or direct bookings from offering lower rates, limiting consumer price comparison | Anti-competitive price control; market manipulation |
| HO-1.4 | **Mini-Bar and Incidentals Gouging** | Charging exorbitant markups (300–800%) on in-room items with automatic sensor billing that charges for items merely moved or examined | Hidden auto-billing; price exploitation |
| HO-1.5 | **Early Departure Penalties**     | Imposing full-stay charges or penalty fees when guests check out early, even when the room can be resold | Unjustified penalty; double recovery |

## Category 2: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HO-2.1 | **Housekeeper Workload**          | Requiring housekeepers to clean excessive rooms per shift (12–16+) with piece-rate pay that incentivizes skipping safety protocols | Worker endangerment; quota exploitation |
| HO-2.2 | **Wage Theft via Tip Credit**     | Using the tip credit provision to pay tipped employees below minimum wage while assigning non-tipped duties (room setup, linen sorting) that dilute actual tip income | Wage manipulation; labor law exploitation |
| HO-2.3 | **Seasonal Worker Exploitation**  | Recruiting workers on temporary visas with housing deductions, restricted mobility, and deportation threats if they complain about conditions | Coercive labor; indentured workforce |
| HO-2.4 | **Schedule Instability**           | Publishing schedules with minimal advance notice, split shifts, or on-call requirements that prevent workers from securing second jobs or childcare | Schedule precarity; life disruption |

## Category 3: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HO-3.1 | **Guest Data Sale**               | Selling guest names, stay histories, spending patterns, and preferences to data brokers, advertisers, or third-party marketers without explicit consent | Privacy commodification; consent failure |
| HO-3.2 | **Room Entry Surveillance**        | Using in-room cameras, smart TV cameras, or voice assistants to monitor guests under the guise of security or personalization | In-room surveillance; privacy violation |
| HO-3.3 | **Key Card Tracking**              | Logging and analyzing electronic key card data (room entry times, duration, frequency) for behavioral profiling or operational metrics without guest disclosure | Covert behavioral tracking |
| HO-3.4 | **Wi-Fi Interception**            | Intercepting, logging, or analyzing guest internet traffic on hotel networks, including injecting ads or redirecting searches | Network surveillance; communication interception |

## Category 4: Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HO-4.1 | **Security Staffing Cuts**        | Reducing overnight security staff to below-safe levels while maintaining messaging that implies 24/7 security presence | False safety representation; guest endangerment |
| HO-4.2 | **Bed Bug Concealment**           | Failing to disclose known infestations to incoming guests, suppressing online reviews that mention infestations, or relocating affected guests without treatment notification | Health hazard concealment; informed-consent failure |
| HO-4.3 | **Food Safety in Room Service**   | Operating room service and minibar restocking with less rigorous food safety oversight than public dining venues | Unequal safety standards; hidden risk |
| HO-4.4 | **Fire Safety Non-Compliance**   | Delaying fire system inspections, disabling smoke detectors, or blocking emergency exits during renovations without guest notification | Life-safety negligence |
| HO-4.5 | **Carbon Monoxide and Mold Negligence** | Failing to install or maintain CO detectors and neglecting mold remediation in humid climates while renting affected rooms | Health hazard; premises negligence |

## Category 5: Consumer Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| HO-5.1 | **Booking Non-Refundability**     | Designing default booking flows to pre-select non-refundable rates while making refundable options hard to find or significantly more expensive | Dark pattern; choice manipulation |
| HO-5.2 | **Damage Claim Fabrication**      | Charging guests for pre-existing damage, normal wear, or fabricated incidents after checkout with no opportunity for the guest to dispute before charges are processed | Unverified charges; financial extraction |
| HO-5.3 | **Loyalty Program Devaluation**    | Unilaterally increasing point requirements, reducing redemption availability, or expiring points with short notice, undermining accumulated loyalty value | Retroactive contract change; value confiscation |
| HO-5.4 | **Review Suppression**            | Using legal threats, non-disparagement clauses, or manipulated flagging systems to remove negative guest reviews from public platforms | Free speech suppression; reputation manipulation |
| HO-5.5 | **Holding Luggage or Belongings** | Refusing to return left-behind items or holding luggage hostage pending payment of disputed charges | Property extortion; coercion |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Disclose total price upfront** — include all mandatory fees (resort, facility, destination) in the advertised nightly rate; provide a total cost breakdown before payment is requested.
2. **Protect worker dignity** — cap housekeeping quotas at evidence-based safe levels; eliminate tip credit abuse by paying full minimum wage for all hours; provide seasonal workers with documented rights in their language.
3. **Respect guest privacy** — prohibit in-room surveillance devices; require explicit opt-in for any behavioral tracking; delete guest data within 30 days of checkout unless retention is consented to; never sell guest data.
4. **Maintain safety transparency** — disclose known infestations or environmental hazards before booking; post security staffing levels; maintain and inspect fire and CO safety systems on published schedules.
5. **Honor consumer rights** — default to refundable booking options; provide itemized damage claims with photographic evidence before charging; honor loyalty point values with 12 months' notice for any devaluation; eliminate non-disparagement clauses.
