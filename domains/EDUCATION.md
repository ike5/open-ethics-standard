# Education — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Education (K-12, Higher Education, EdTech)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to educational institutions, edtech platforms, student loan providers, and educational publishers. Education shapes life trajectories; unethical practices in this domain compound disadvantage and betray a public trust.

---

## Category 1: Tuition & Fee Transparency

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ED-1.1 | **Tuition Bait-and-Switch**      | Advertising low tuition while hiding mandatory fees that increase total cost by 20%+ | Hidden cost practice                  |
| ED-1.2 | **Fee Creep**                    | Introducing new mandatory fees (technology, activity, wellness) each year without corresponding service improvements | Revenue extraction via lock-in        |
| ED-1.3 | **Scholarship Displacement**     | Reducing institutional aid when students win external scholarships, keeping total cost the same rather than reducing student burden | Undermining student effort; fund diversion |
| ED-1.4 | **Deposit Forfeiture Pressure**  | Requiring non-refundable enrollment deposits before financial aid packages are finalized | Financial coercion; premature commitment |

## Category 2: EdTech & Student Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ED-2.1 | **Surveillance Proctoring**      | Requiring AI-powered exam monitoring that flags students of color at higher rates and collects biometric data without adequate safeguards | Discriminatory algorithm; privacy violation |
| ED-2.2 | **Student Data Monetization**    | Selling or sharing student learning data, engagement metrics, or personal information to third parties | Violation of FERPA spirit; exploiting minors |
| ED-2.3 | **Forced Platform Adoption**    | Requiring students to purchase specific devices or proprietary platforms when open alternatives exist | Vendor lock-in; artificial cost barrier |
| ED-2.4 | **Predictive Analytics Discrimination** | Using predictive models to track "at-risk" students in ways that create self-fulfilling prophecies and stigmatize vulnerable populations | Algorithmic bias; labeling harm       |
| ED-2.5 | **Data Retention Beyond Necessity** | Retaining student records, browsing data, and learning analytics indefinitely after the educational relationship ends | Unnecessary data hoarding             |

## Category 3: Enrollment & Admissions

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ED-3.1 | **Enrollment Funnel Manipulation** | Using high-pressure sales tactics (limited seats, countdown timers, fear of missing out) to drive enrollment decisions | Manipulative recruitment              |
| ED-3.2 | **Outcome Misrepresentation**     | Advertising inflated job placement rates or salary outcomes without disclosing methodology or sample bias | Deceptive advertising                 |
| ED-3.3 | **Accreditation Ambiguity**      | Failing to clearly disclose accreditation status or presenting unaccredited programs as equivalent to accredited ones | Misrepresentation of credentials      |
| ED-3.4 | **Legacy Preference**            | Giving admissions advantage to children of alumni, perpetuating generational privilege | Structural inequity                   |

## Category 4: Student Loans & Financial Products

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ED-4.1 | **Preferred Lender Kickbacks**   | Institutions receiving payments from lenders in exchange for steering students toward less favorable loan terms | Conflict of interest; financial harm  |
| ED-4.2 | **Interest Capitalization Concealment** | Failing to clearly disclose when and how interest capitalizes on deferred loans | Hiding long-term cost                 |
| ED-4.3 | **Repayment Plan Obfuscation**   | Making income-driven repayment plans difficult to find, enroll in, or recertify for | Creating barriers to affordable repayment |
| ED-4.4 | **Campus Card Exploitation**     | Partnering with financial institutions to issue campus cards with overdraft fees and high ATM charges to captive student population | Predatory financial product targeting |

## Category 5: Content & Curriculum

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| ED-5.1 | **Forced Textbook Rotation**     | Requiring new editions with minimal changes, disabling used-book markets and access codes that expire | Artificial scarcity; cost inflation   |
| ED-5.2 | **Curriculum Gatekeeping**        | Withholding course availability or sequence information to extend enrollment time | Prolonging enrollment for revenue     |
| ED-5.3 | **Accessibility Failures**        | Failing to provide accessible digital materials (screen-reader compatible, captioned, alternative formats) | Exclusion of disabled students        |
| ED-5.4 | **Corporate Curriculum Influence** | Allowing corporate sponsors to influence curriculum content or research outcomes without disclosure | Undisclosed conflict of interest      |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Publish total cost of attendance** — provide a comprehensive, itemized cost breakdown before enrollment commitment, including all mandatory fees.
2. **Adopt student data rights** — implement data minimization, publish annual data-sharing reports, and allow students to delete non-academic records upon departure.
3. **Standardize outcome reporting** — disclose job placement methodology, response rates, and exclude employer-subsidized positions from placement statistics.
4. **Ensure platform neutrality** — allow students to use any device or platform that meets technical requirements; do not mandate single-vendor solutions.
5. **Commit to textbook affordability** — adopt open educational resources where available; limit edition changes to substantively revised content; decouple access codes from textbooks.
