# Mining & Extraction — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Mining & Resource Extraction  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to mining companies, oil and gas extractors, and mineral processing operations. Extraction industries leave permanent marks on landscapes and communities; unethical practices cause irreversible environmental damage, displace indigenous peoples, and endanger workers for profit.

---

## Category 1: Environmental Impact

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| MI-1.1 | **Tailings Dam Risk Concealment** | Understating the structural risk of tailings dams to avoid costly upgrades or closure | Catastrophic risk concealment          |
| MI-1.2 | **Water Contamination**          | Discharging mining effluent into waterways without adequate treatment or monitoring | Environmental destruction; public health risk |
| MI-1.3 | **Habitat Destruction Underreporting** | Minimizing the scope of ecological impact in environmental assessments to secure permits | Regulatory fraud                      |
| MI-1.4 | **Reclamation Bond Underfunding** | Posting insufficient reclamation bonds, leaving taxpayers liable for site cleanup | Financial risk transfer to public     |
| MI-1.5 | **Acid Mine Drainage Neglect**   | Failing to prevent or treat acid mine drainage that contaminates waterways for decades | Long-term environmental degradation   |

## Category 2: Labor & Worker Safety

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| MI-2.1 | **Worker Safety Negligence**      | Failing to implement known safety measures (ventilation, ground support, monitoring) to reduce operational costs | Endangering worker lives              |
| MI-2.2 | **Community Displacement**        | Resettling communities for mine expansion without fair compensation or equivalent livelihood restoration | Forced displacement; cultural destruction |
| MI-2.3 | **Artisanal Mining Exploitation** | Sourcing from artisanal and small-scale miners who use child labor or hazardous practices without verification | Complicity in labor abuse            |
| MI-2.4 | **Black Lung Concealment**        | Suppressing or manipulating dust exposure data to avoid implementing required controls | Deliberate health endangerment        |
| MI-2.5 | **Contract Worker Precarity**     | Using contract labor for high-risk tasks to avoid direct liability for injuries | Risk shifting to vulnerable workers   |

## Category 3: Community & Indigenous Rights

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| MI-3.1 | **FPIC Violation**                | Proceeding with extraction on indigenous lands without free, prior, and informed consent | Violation of indigenous sovereignty    |
| MI-3.2 | **Revenue Sharing Opacity**       | Opaque community development agreements with unenforceable commitments and hidden clawbacks | Broken social contract                |
| MI-3.3 | **Community Health Impact Concealment** | Suppressing data linking mining operations to increased rates of respiratory disease, cancer, or birth defects | Withholding health-critical information |
| MI-3.4 | **Cultural Heritage Destruction** | Destroying or desecrating cultural and archaeological sites during extraction operations | Irreversible cultural loss             |
| MI-3.5 | **Security Force Abuse**          | Deploying private or state security forces that intimidate, surveil, or harm community members opposing operations | Human rights violation                |

## Category 5: Market & Pricing

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| MI-5.1 | **Transfer Pricing Manipulation** | Shifting profits to low-tax jurisdictions through internal commodity pricing at below-market rates | Tax avoidance; depriving host countries of revenue |
| MI-5.2 | **Royalty Evasion**              | Underreporting production volume or value to reduce royalty payments to governments | Fiscal fraud                          |
| MI-5.3 | **Commodity Market Influence**    | Using market dominance to manipulate commodity spot prices or futures markets | Market manipulation                   |
| MI-5.4 | **Mine-Life Manipulation**       | Publicly projecting extended mine life to maintain investor confidence while privately planning premature closure | Investor deception; community false expectation |
| MI-5.5 | **Stranded Asset Externalization** | Abandoning mines without adequate closure, leaving environmental and social costs to the public | Cost externalization                  |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Obtain free, prior, and informed consent** — secure documented FPIC from indigenous communities before any exploration or extraction; publish community benefit agreements with enforceable commitments.
2. **Implement independent tailings review** — submit all tailings storage facilities to independent third-party review aligned with the Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management.
3. **Publish environmental monitoring data** — make real-time water quality, air emission, and seismic monitoring data publicly accessible online.
4. **Fund full reclamation** — post reclamation bonds covering 100% of estimated closure costs, adjusted annually; begin progressive reclamation during operations.
5. **Disclose all payments to governments** — publish country-by-country reports of royalties, taxes, and fees in compliance with EITI standards.
