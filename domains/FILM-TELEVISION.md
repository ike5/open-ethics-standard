# Film & Television — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Film & Television Production  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to film and television production, encompassing studios, streaming platforms, distributors, and production companies. The screen industries wield enormous cultural influence and employ vast creative workforces, yet persistent ethical failures in labor practices, representation, data transparency, and content responsibility undermine both the people who create these works and the audiences who consume them. This standard addresses the systemic practices that exploit workers, distort cultural narratives, and hoard power within the industry.

---

## Category 1: Labor

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FL-1.1 | **Below-Minimum Pay via Expense Tricks** | Classifying worker compensation through per diem, expense accounts, or production fees that circumvent minimum wage and overtime requirements | Wage theft; regulatory evasion        |
| FL-1.2 | **Unsafe Working Hours**           | Scheduling shoots exceeding 14 hours, mandating turnaround periods under 8 hours, and creating conditions that lead to fatigue-related injuries and deaths | Worker endangerment; safety violation |
| FL-1.3 | **Contract Writer Exclusion from Residuals** | Hiring writers under contract or day-play designations that disqualify them from residual payments, depriving them of ongoing compensation for successful work | Residual evasion; creator underpayment |
| FL-1.4 | **Crew Replacement During Strikes** | Replacing striking workers with non-union or international crew to break collective bargaining actions and circumvent labor disputes | Union-busting; collective rights violation |
| FL-1.5 | **Intern and Trainee Exploitation** | Using unpaid or underpaid internships, fellowships, and training programs as a source of free labor rather than genuine professional development | Labor exploitation; opportunity gatekeeping |

## Category 2: Representation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FL-2.1 | **Whitewashing Cast Roles**       | Casting white actors in roles written for or based on people of color, erasing authentic representation from the screen | Cultural erasure; casting discrimination |
| FL-2.2 | **Diversity Quota Theater**       | Implementing superficial diversity targets in background roles or minor characters while reserving lead and creative positions for dominant groups | Performative inclusion; systemic exclusion |
| FL-2.3 | **Disability Representation Erasure** | Casting able-bodied actors in disability roles, failing to provide set accommodations, and excluding disabled talent from both on-screen and behind-the-camera opportunities | Disability erasure; access discrimination |
| FL-2.4 | **Pay Gaps Across Demographics**  | Maintaining persistent pay disparities for women, people of color, and LGBTQ+ performers and crew compared to white male counterparts in equivalent roles | Pay discrimination; structural inequality |

## Category 3: Content

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FL-3.1 | **Undisclosed Product Placement** | Integrating branded products, services, or messaging into narrative content without clear disclosure to audiences | Commercial manipulation; transparency violation |
| FL-3.2 | **Cultural Appropriation**        | Exploiting sacred, traditional, or culturally significant practices, attire, or narratives from marginalized communities without consultation, credit, or compensation | Cultural exploitation; creative theft |
| FL-3.3 | **Historical Inaccuracy as Fact** | Presenting heavily distorted or fabricated historical narratives as factual depictions, particularly when misrepresenting the experiences of marginalized groups | Misinformation; narrative manipulation |
| FL-3.4 | **Trigger Content Absence**        | Failing to provide content warnings for graphic violence, sexual assault, suicide, or other triggering material, leaving viewers without the ability to make informed viewing choices | Viewer harm; informed consent failure |
| FL-3.5 | **Stereotype Perpetuation**       | Repeatedly casting and writing marginalized characters through reductive stereotypes that reinforce harmful social narratives | Cultural harm; prejudice reinforcement |

## Category 4: Distribution

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FL-4.1 | **Windowing for Maximum Per-Viewer Revenue** | Restricting content availability through staggered release windows designed solely to extract maximum per-viewer revenue rather than maximizing audience access | Access restriction; rent extraction    |
| FL-4.2 | **Regional Content Blocking**      | Using geographic restrictions and geo-blocking to prevent audiences in certain regions from accessing content available elsewhere | Geographic discrimination; access inequality |
| FL-4.3 | **Accessibility Delay for Captioning** | Releasing content without closed captions or audio description, or delaying accessibility features days or weeks after initial release | Disability exclusion; accessibility violation |
| FL-4.4 | **Independent Film Shelf Suppression** | Acquiring independent films through exclusive distribution deals and then minimizing their marketing and theatrical release to eliminate competition with studio-owned content | Anti-competitive suppression; market manipulation |
| FL-4.5 | **Theatrical Run Manipulation**   | Shortening or extending theatrical runs strategically to inflate streaming platform metrics rather than serving audience demand | Artificial scarcity; market distortion |

## Category 5: Data

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| FL-5.1 | **Viewership Data Opacity**        | Refusing to disclose audience measurement data to creators, investors, and the public, preventing informed decisions about content value | Transparency violation; information asymmetry |
| FL-5.2 | **Audience Measurement Manipulation** | Using proprietary, non-auditable measurement methodologies that inflate or suppress viewership figures to serve contractual or financial interests | Data manipulation; fraud risk        |
| FL-5.3 | **Location Tracking During Production** | Collecting geolocation data from cast, crew, and extras through production apps, wearables, or on-set systems without clear purpose or consent | Privacy violation; surveillance overreach |
| FL-5.4 | **Minor Consent Gaps in Reality TV** | Featuring minors in reality television productions without adequate informed consent, parental oversight, or long-term psychological support provisions | Child exploitation; consent violation |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Protect labor rights** — pay above minimum wage inclusive of all compensation structures; enforce safe working hour limits with mandatory rest periods; provide residuals to all contributing writers; commit to no-replacement policies during strikes.
2. **Ensure authentic representation** — cast authentically across demographics; eliminate pay gaps through regular equity audits; provide meaningful roles and accommodations for disabled talent; reject performative diversity metrics.
3. **Practice responsible content** — disclose all product placements clearly; consult with cultural advisors on sensitive material; present historical narratives with accuracy disclosures; provide timely content warnings.
4. **Expand distribution access** — reduce unnecessary content windowing; eliminate regional blocking where possible; release captioning and audio description simultaneously with premiere; support independent theatrical distribution.
5. **Increase data transparency** — share viewership data with creators and stakeholders; adopt auditable measurement standards; limit location tracking to essential production needs with explicit consent; implement robust minor consent protocols for all unscripted content.
