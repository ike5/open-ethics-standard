# Entertainment & Streaming — Domain-Specific Ethics Standards

**Domain:** Entertainment & Streaming (Video, Music, Gaming Platforms)  
**Version:** 2026.0.1  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This document extends the Open Ethics Standard (OES) to entertainment and streaming platforms, including video, music, and gaming services. Streaming platforms have become primary cultural gatekeepers; unethical practices—from subscription manipulation to labor exploitation—shape what billions see, hear, and believe while extracting value from creators and audiences alike.

---

## Category 1: Pricing & Subscriptions

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EN-1.1 | **Subscription Tier Proliferation** | Creating numerous overlapping subscription tiers with strategically withheld features to force upgrades, making it difficult for users to identify the plan that meets their needs | Choice overload; engineered upsell    |
| EN-1.2 | **Ad-Tier Bait-and-Switch**       | Introducing low-cost ad-supported tiers that progressively increase ad load, degrade experience quality, or move formerly ad-free content behind higher tiers | Degraded service; false expectations |
| EN-1.3 | **Regional Pricing Discrimination** | Charging vastly different prices for identical content across regions without cost justification, or blocking VPN access to fairer regional pricing | Geographic exploitation               |
| EN-1.4 | **Silent Price Increases**         | Raising subscription fees without prominent notification, banking on automatic renewal and user inattention | Financial inertia exploitation        |
| EN-1.5 | **Bundle Obfuscation**            | Bundling streaming services together in ways that obscure individual service costs and make partial cancellation difficult | Hidden cost; forced retention         |

## Category 2: Content Manipulation

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EN-2.1 | **Content Removal Without Notice** | Removing titles from libraries without advance notice, leaving subscribers unable to finish series or access purchased content | Destroying cultural access            |
| EN-2.2 | **Silent Censorship for Markets**  | Editing or removing content to appease government censors or advertiser preferences without disclosing the alterations | Undisclosed editorial compromise      |
| EN-2.3 | **Editorial Independence Erosion** | Allowing advertiser, government, or corporate pressure to influence content recommendations, visibility, or commissioning decisions | Compromised editorial integrity       |
| EN-2.4 | **Algorithmic Content Burying**    | Deprioritizing or suppressing content that contradicts platform business interests while claiming neutral recommendation | Covert content suppression            |
| EN-2.5 | **Fringe Content Amplification**   | Promoting extreme, divisive, or conspiratorial content because it generates longer engagement sessions | Addictive recommendation; harm amplification |

## Category 3: Data Practices

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EN-3.1 | **Viewing Habit Sale to Advertisers** | Selling granular viewing histories—including content type, watch times, and completion rates—to advertising networks or data brokers | Privacy violation; data trafficking    |
| EN-3.2 | **Bandwidth Throttling of Competitors** | Deprioritizing or degrading network traffic from competing streaming services while favoring owned or affiliated content | Anti-competitive manipulation         |
| EN-3.3 | **Download Restriction DRM**       | Imposing overly restrictive digital rights management that prevents offline viewing, limits device transfers, or expires downloaded content arbitrarily | Undermining fair use; user hostility   |
| EN-3.4 | **Cross-Service Data Sharing**     | Sharing viewing data across owned or partnered platforms (music, gaming, social) without unified, transparent consent | Scope creep; data consolidation        |
| EN-3.5 | **Emotional Profiling**            | Inferring mental health, mood, or emotional states from content preferences to target advertising or adjust recommendations | Exploitative psychographic profiling  |

## Category 4: Labor & Creator Treatment

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EN-4.1 | **Residual Elimination**           | Replacing traditional residual and royalty structures with flat buyout payments that eliminate ongoing compensation regardless of viewership | Devaluing creative labor              |
| EN-4.2 | **AI Replacement of Creatives**    | Using generative AI to produce scripts, music, voiceovers, or visual effects in place of human creatives without disclosure or negotiation | Labor displacement; creative devaluation |
| EN-4.3 | **Algorithmic Content Suppression** | Deprioritizing or hiding creator content to reduce payout obligations, manipulating recommendation algorithms to lower per-view compensation | Wage suppression via algorithm        |
| EN-4.4 | **Opaque Revenue Sharing**         | Failing to disclose how streaming revenue, ad income, and subscription fees are allocated to creators whose content generates that revenue | Financial opacity; unfair compensation |
| EN-4.5 | **Contractual Content Lock-Up**    | Requiring creators to grant exclusive, long-term, or perpetual rights to their work as a condition of distribution | Unfair contractual extraction         |

## Category 5: Accessibility

| Ref   | Practice                          | Description                                                        | Ethical Violation                     |
|-------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EN-5.1 | **Captioning Failures**            | Providing incomplete, inaccurate, or delayed closed captions, or launching content without captions and adding them weeks later | Excluding deaf and hard-of-hearing users |
| EN-5.2 | **Audio Description Gaps**         | Failing to provide audio descriptions for visual content, or describing only a fraction of a catalog | Excluding blind and low-vision users  |
| EN-5.3 | **Price Barriers for Disabled Features** | Charging premium tier prices for accessibility features such as caption customization, audio descriptions, or accessible player interfaces | Monetizing accessibility              |
| EN-5.4 | **Interface Inaccessibility**       | Designing apps and websites that fail to meet WCAG standards, preventing screen reader navigation or keyboard-only operation | Structural digital exclusion         |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this domain standard shall:

1. **Guarantee subscription transparency** — provide 30-day advance notice of price changes; clearly differentiate tiers with side-by-side feature comparisons; offer simple cancellation.
2. **Protect editorial independence** — publish content moderation policies; disclose government-mandated content alterations; maintain separation between advertising and content decisions.
3. **Respect creator rights** — disclose revenue-sharing formulas; offer fair residual structures; obtain informed consent before using AI in creative production.
4. **Ensure accessibility parity** — provide captions and audio descriptions for all content at launch; include accessibility features in every tier at no additional cost.
5. **Limit data exploitation** — prohibit sale of individual viewing data; require affirmative consent for cross-service data sharing; ban emotional profiling for ad targeting.
