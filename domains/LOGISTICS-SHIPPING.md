# Domain: Logistics & Shipping

**Version:** 2026.0.1
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Purpose

This standard defines ethical practices and violations within the logistics and shipping industry, covering pricing integrity, labor rights, environmental responsibility, data stewardship, and consumer protections. It addresses practices that exploit information asymmetry, endanger workers, degrade the environment, misuse data, or harm consumers. Organizations operating under this domain standard commit to transparent, fair, and sustainable logistics and shipping operations.

---

## Pricing

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| LO-1.1 | Dimensional weight transparency | Clearly disclose dimensional weight calculation methods, thresholds, and surcharge triggers before shipment confirmation | Manipulating dimensional weight factors or applying undisclosed dimensional weight surcharges to inflate shipping costs beyond reasonable measure |
| LO-1.2 | Fuel surcharge clarity | Publish fuel surcharge formulas with clear basis indices, update schedules, and historical data | Imposing opaque fuel surcharges that do not correspond to actual fuel cost fluctuations or that embed unrelated profit margins |
| LO-1.3 | Residential delivery fee fairness | Apply residential delivery surcharges proportionate to actual cost differences and disclose them at checkout | Adding residential delivery fees that far exceed actual delivery cost differentials without clear upfront disclosure |
| LO-1.4 | Rate structure simplicity | Provide clear, comparable rate structures that allow customers to understand and compare total shipping costs | Designing rate structures with hidden fees, surcharge layers, and complex zone definitions that prevent customers from understanding total cost |
| LO-1.5 | Promotional pricing honesty | Ensure promotional shipping rates reflect genuine discounts and are not offset by undisclosed fees | Advertising free or discounted shipping while recovering costs through inflated base rates, handling fees, or mandatory add-on charges |

## Labor

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| LO-2.1 | Driver classification accuracy | Classify delivery drivers based on the true nature of the working relationship, applying legal and ethical classification tests | Misclassifying drivers as independent contractors to avoid minimum wage guarantees, workers' compensation, and benefits obligations |
| LO-2.2 | Warehouse quota reasonableness | Set warehouse productivity quotas that account for safety, fatigue, and human limitations, with transparent adjustment processes | Imposing unreasonably high pick-and-pack quotas that force workers to skip breaks, compromise safety, or risk disciplinary action |
| LO-2.3 | Safety investment adequacy | Invest in workplace safety infrastructure, training, and staffing levels that protect workers from injury | Cutting corners on safety equipment, training time, or staffing to reduce costs at the expense of worker injury rates |
| LO-2.4 | Rest break compliance | Ensure mandatory rest and meal breaks are available, unimpeded, and not penalized in productivity tracking | Designing shift structures or tracking systems that make rest breaks functionally impossible or penalize workers who take them |
| LO-2.5 | Injury reporting accessibility | Maintain accessible, non-retaliatory injury and incident reporting systems with prompt investigation and remediation | Discouraging injury reporting through fear of retaliation, quota penalties, or dismissal, leading to underreported workplace hazards |

## Environmental

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| LO-3.1 | Emissions disclosure | Measure and publicly report last-mile and overall shipping emissions, including scope 3 contributions | Concealing or understating last-mile emissions data, omitting scope 3 contributions, or refusing to publish environmental impact reports |
| LO-3.2 | Packaging waste reduction | Minimize packaging waste through right-sizing, recycled materials, and take-back programs | Using excessive single-use packaging, oversized boxes, and non-recyclable materials when viable alternatives exist |
| LO-3.3 | Returns reuse prioritization | Prioritize refurbishment, resale, or donation of returned items over disposal or destruction | Systematically sending returned or overstock goods to landfills or incineration rather than reuse, resale, or donation pathways |
| LO-3.4 | Fleet transition planning | Develop and publicly share timelines for transitioning delivery fleets to low-emission and zero-emission vehicles | Continuing to rely exclusively on fossil fuel fleets without investing in or planning for vehicle electrification or alternative fuels |
| LO-3.5 | Carbon offset integrity | Use verified, additional, and permanent carbon offsets for any emissions claimed as neutralized, and disclose offset details | Purchasing low-quality or non-additional carbon offsets to claim carbon neutrality without genuine emissions reduction |

## Data

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| LO-4.1 | Shipment data consent | Obtain shipper and recipient consent before using shipment data for purposes beyond delivery fulfillment | Monetizing shipment metadata—origin, destination, contents, frequency—by selling it to third parties for marketing, profiling, or surveillance |
| LO-4.2 | Recipient profiling prohibition | Do not build behavioral or demographic profiles of shipment recipients from delivery data | Creating detailed recipient profiles from delivery patterns, purchase inferences, and location data for resale or internal commercial targeting |
| LO-4.3 | Delivery surveillance limitation | Limit tracking data collection to what is necessary for delivery confirmation and customer service | Operating delivery tracking systems that capture disproportionate location data, enabling persistent surveillance of recipients' movements |
| LO-4.4 | Data retention minimization | Retain shipment and delivery data only for the period necessary for operational, legal, or customer service requirements | Retaining shipment data indefinitely for commercial reuse without clear justification or data subject consent |
| LO-4.5 | Third-party data sharing restriction | Restrict data sharing with logistics partners and vendors to the minimum necessary for service fulfillment and under contractual privacy obligations | Sharing shipment data with marketing partners, data brokers, or analytics providers without shipper or recipient knowledge and consent |

## Consumer Rights

| Ref | Practice | Description | Ethical Violation |
|-----|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| LO-5.1 | Lost package accountability | Establish clear, timely resolution processes for lost packages including refund, replacement, or investigation timelines | Creating accountability gaps where neither the shipper nor carrier accepts responsibility, leaving consumers without recourse for lost shipments |
| LO-5.2 | Insurance upsell fairness | Present shipping insurance options transparently, disclosing coverage already included and the realistic likelihood of claims | Upselling shipping insurance on low-value items while concealing that existing coverage or liability limits already protect the shipment |
| LO-5.3 | Delivery photo privacy | Obtain recipient consent before photographing delivery locations and store images only as long as needed for proof of delivery | Photographing doorsteps, residences, or personal property as proof of delivery and retaining or sharing those images beyond the delivery confirmation purpose |
| LO-5.4 | Refund and claims timeliness | Process consumer claims and refunds within published timeframes and provide clear status updates | Delaying refund and claims processing through bureaucratic obstacles, unresponsive customer service, or unreasonable documentation requirements |
| LO-5.5 | Delivery option transparency | Present all available delivery speeds and pricing options clearly, without steering consumers toward higher-cost options | Defaulting customers to premium shipping options or obscuring standard delivery choices to increase average shipping revenue |

## Compliance Guidelines

Organizations adopting this standard should:

1. **Publish Pricing Guides** — Make complete rate cards, surcharge schedules, and dimensional weight calculators publicly available and updated annually.
2. **Conduct Labor Audits** — Perform regular audits of worker classification, quota systems, safety records, and injury reporting, with findings reported to leadership.
3. **Set Environmental Targets** — Establish measurable emissions reduction, packaging waste, and fleet transition targets with annual public progress reporting.
4. **Implement Data Governance** — Create and enforce data minimization, consent management, and retention policies aligned with this standard.
5. **Establish Consumer Advocacy Channels** — Provide accessible, responsive dispute resolution and claims processes with published response timelines.
6. **Train Operations Staff** — Require regular ethics training for pricing, warehouse, delivery, customer service, and data management personnel.
